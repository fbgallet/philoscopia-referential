# To each their customs?

*Herodotus, *Histories*, 430 BCE*

Herodotus tells that King Darius asked some Greeks, who burned their dead, for what price they would agree to eat the bodies instead: horrified, they answered that no sum could persuade them. He then put the reverse question to some Callatians, who ate their dead: for what price would they burn them? They cried out in the same horror. Each held their own custom self-evident, and the other's a monstrosity.

**What should we conclude from such disagreement between peoples?**

**Axes at stake** : [Status of norms (metaethics)](../axes/moral-scope.md)

**Possible stances** :

- Some things stay right or wrong everywhere: custom is not all, and one can judge from outside.
- Each culture has its own measure of good: no one can judge from outside what holds among others.
- We can move past the disagreement: humans of different origins can, by reasoning together, agree on shared rules.
