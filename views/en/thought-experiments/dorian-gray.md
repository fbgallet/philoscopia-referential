# The picture of Dorian Gray

*Oscar Wilde, *The Picture of Dorian Gray*, 1890*

Dorian Gray stays forever young and beautiful, free to devote his life to the sheer enjoyment of beautiful sensations and to allow himself anything, while a hidden portrait ages and grows hideous with each misdeed in his place. No excess marks his face or costs him a thing. Imagine you were offered this pact: to make your existence a pure pursuit of beauty and pleasure, freed from any moral price, since another pays it for you.

**Would you accept this pact, and would a life wholly devoted to beauty be the highest one?**

**Axes at stake** : [Beauty in life](../axes/aesthetic-life.md)

**Possible stances** :

- I accept. A life need not justify itself before morality: making it beautiful, intense, accomplished like a work of art, that is the highest thing; the rest is convention.
- I refuse. A beauty paid for by wrongdoing, or cut off from all uprightness, rings hollow: a beautiful life indifferent to the good is not a flourishing one, it is empty.
- Beauty, but not at that price: I want a beautiful life, provided it is not paid for by my wrongs. Beauty truly matters, so long as it does not betray the good.
