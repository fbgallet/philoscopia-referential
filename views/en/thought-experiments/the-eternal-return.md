# The eternal return

*Nietzsche, *The Gay Science*, 1882*

Imagine that one night, in your loneliest solitude, a demon slips beside you and says: « This life, as you live it now and have lived it, you will have to live once more and countless times more; and there will be nothing new in it, but every pain, every pleasure, every thought, and this very moment, will return in the same order. » The hourglass of existence would be turned over endlessly, and you with it. [Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md) does not ask whether this is true: he makes it a trial. For the question it raises reaches you today: would you want to live your days, as you fill them, over and over forever? [Eternal recurrence](../glossary/eternal-recurrence.md) is less a cosmology than a test: do you love your life enough to will it, identical, for all time?

**Would this thought crush you, or would you welcome it as good news? What you answer says a great deal about the time you are living.**

**Axes at stake** : [Temporal orientation](../axes/time-orientation.md) · [Relation to the past](../axes/past-relation.md)

**Possible stances** :

- I would want it: if I had to live it all again, identical, I would say yes. That is the sign that I truly live my days, without hanging them on some later time.
- It would crush me: so many days spent rushing, waiting, putting off until tomorrow. I would not want to live them again as they are.
- Only if I could first change the way I live my time: then, perhaps, I could will this return.
