# The Watch and the Watchmaker

*Paley, *Natural Theology*, 1802*

You are crossing a deserted heath and find a watch lying on the ground. You would never believe it had assembled itself: so many gears fitted to a purpose betray a hand that designed them. Now, Paley observes, the eye that sees, the wing that carries, the order of the stars are mechanisms a thousand times more intricate. How could so much order, beauty and complexity, down to the fine tuning of the laws of the universe, exist without having been intended?

**Does this admirable order prove a creator who designed it, or can an appearance of design arise from blind processes, with no intention at all?**

**Axes at stake** : [Existence of God](../axes/god-existence.md) · [Tonality of reality](../axes/cosmic-outlook.md)

**Possible stances** :

- Such an arrangement cannot be the work of chance: the eye, the living world, the order of the heavens presuppose an author who designed them on purpose.
- The world's precise fitness for life makes a designer more probable than chance, even if it is no proof.
- The analogy is too weak to conclude: the world is not a watch, and it holds as much disorder and waste as harmony.
- What looks designed can arise without design: blind variations, sifted by survival, suffice to produce the appearance of purpose.
