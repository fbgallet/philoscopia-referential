# The teletransporter

*Derek Parfit, *Reasons and Persons*, 1984*

You step into a teletransporter. The machine records the exact state of each of your cells, destroys your body, then transmits the information to Mars, where an identical body is rebuilt from fresh matter. The person who walks out has all your memories, your character, and the feeling of having just stepped into the booth. Convinced she is you, she takes up your life where you left it. Usually the trip goes without a hitch; but today, a question holds you back on the threshold.

**Is the person who wakes on Mars you, or did you die in the booth, replaced by a copy who believes she is you?**

**Axes at stake** : [Nature of the self](../axes/self-nature.md)

**Possible stances** :

- It is not me: whatever was transmitted, what I truly am did not travel. The original died, the copy is someone else.
- It is me: what makes me myself is the continuity of my memory and character, and the person on Mars has all of it.
- My story continues in her: she takes up my projects, my bonds, my past as her own. That is what it is to still be me.
- There is no deep answer: "me" was never more than a bundle of states. Asking whether the copy is "really" me assumes a self that does not exist.
