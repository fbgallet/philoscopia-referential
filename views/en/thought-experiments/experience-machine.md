# The experience machine

*Robert Nozick, *Anarchy, State, and Utopia*, 1974*

Imagine a machine that could give you any experience you desire. Neuropsychologists would stimulate your brain so that you believed you were writing a great novel, making wonderful friends, or living the most thrilling adventures, while in reality you floated in a tank with electrodes attached to your skull. Once plugged in, you would no longer know the experiences are simulated: from the inside, your life would feel perfectly happy. You can program an entire lifetime of happiness in advance, with no risk of malfunction. You are offered the chance to plug in for the rest of your life.

**Would you plug in? If you hesitate, what does that reveal: that a good life requires something more than pleasant experiences, however perfect they may be?**

**Axes at stake** : [The highest good](../axes/sovereign-good.md)

**Possible stances** :

- If, from the inside, my life feels perfectly happy, nothing else matters: I plug in.
- I would gladly take those experiences, but spending my whole life in the tank would deprive me of something: experience matters, but not only experience.
- I want to actually do things and touch the world, not merely seem to: I refuse.
