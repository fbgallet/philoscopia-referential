# The experience machine

*Robert Nozick, *Anarchy, State, and Utopia*, 1974*

Imagine a machine that could give you any experience you desire. Neuropsychologists would stimulate your brain so that you believed you were writing a great novel, making wonderful friends, or living the most thrilling adventures, while in reality you floated in a tank with electrodes attached to your skull. Once plugged in, you would no longer know the experiences are simulated: from the inside, your life would feel perfectly happy. You can program an entire lifetime of happiness in advance, with no risk of malfunction. You are offered the chance to plug in for the rest of your life.

**Would you plug in? If you hesitate, what does that reveal: that a good life requires something more than pleasant experiences, however perfect they may be?**

**Axes at stake** : [The highest good](../axes/sovereign-good.md)

## What it does in the debate

### Objects to [The highest good](../axes/sovereign-good.md) : Hedonism — attacks the position

If we refuse to plug in for life, it is because we want more than pleasure: to act for real, to touch the world, to be someone.

**The argument it makes**

1. If pleasure were the only good, plugging in for life to a machine giving the perfect illusion of every pleasure would be the best possible choice.
2. Yet almost no one would plug in forever: we want to do things and not merely believe we do, to be someone and not merely feel ourselves to be, to touch the real and not to dream it.
3. This refusal reveals that we value things, acting for real, being in contact with the world, that felt pleasure alone does not contain.

**⇒** So pleasure is not the highest good: a life of pleasure without reality lacks something we take to be essential.

- **The objection hits home: what I want is not merely to feel good, it is to live for real, to act, to love real people. Pleasure accompanies a good life, it is not its measure.** : You slide toward eudaimonism: the good is real fulfillment, of which pleasure is only a sign.
- **Pleasure stays my measure, but I want it lived in the real, not in a tank: an authentic pleasure, not just any.** : Reality becomes a condition of pleasure, not a rival good: a hedonism 'of the real'.
- **I refute it: if the machine were perfect, I would never lack anything. My refusal comes from a fear of the fake, not from a value higher than my wellbeing.** : You hold the consistent hedonist's reply: our repugnance would be a prejudice, not an insight into the good.

**Puts to the test** : [The cradle argument](../arguments/hedonism-cradle-argument.md)

**Possible stances** :

- If, from the inside, my life feels perfectly happy, nothing else matters: I plug in.
- I would gladly take those experiences, but spending my whole life in the tank would deprive me of something: experience matters, but not only experience.
- I want to actually do things and touch the world, not merely seem to: I refuse.
