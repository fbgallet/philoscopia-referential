# "That's just how I am"

*Sartre, *Existentialism Is a Humanism*, 1946*

Someone you know well keeps doing the same thing: they bolt the moment things get hard, let down those who were counting on them, then vanish. One day you tell them so. They don't even defend themselves. They answer, almost gently: "I know. But that's how I am, I always have been. You can't change your nature." It isn't an excuse they throw back at you, it's a fact they note about themselves, the way one might note the color of one's eyes. What remains is to know what that claim is worth.

**When someone says "it's my nature, I can't change," what is that worth?**

**Axes at stake** : [Essence and existence](../axes/essence.md)

**Possible stances** :

- It's true. Each of us has a character, formed early and deep, that no act of will remakes from scratch. Lucidity means recognizing it and working with it, not believing oneself free to reinvent oneself.
- A part of you is given, granted, but what you make of it falls to you. To invoke one's nature so as not to answer for one's acts is to confuse what one is with what one chooses.
- It's bad faith. There is no hidden "nature" behind your acts: you are nothing but what you do, and "that's how I am" serves to flee the freedom that remains yours. You made yourself this way, and you can make yourself otherwise.
