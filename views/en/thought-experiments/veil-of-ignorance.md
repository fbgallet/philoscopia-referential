# The Veil of Ignorance

*Rawls, *A Theory of Justice*, 1971*

Imagine you must set the rules of your society, but from behind a "veil of ignorance": you do not yet know who you will be in it. Rich or poor, gifted or not, well or badly born, healthy or sick, none of this is known to you. What rules of distribution would you choose, knowing you might end up in any position at all, including the very lowest?

**Behind this veil, would you accept large inequalities, or would you first insist on protecting the lot of the least advantaged?**

**Axes at stake** : [Distributive justice](../axes/distributive-justice.md)

**Possible stances** :

- The safest choice is strict equality, sustained over time: that no one ever has more than another.
- Inequalities, yes, but on one condition: that they also improve the lot of the least advantaged. Otherwise I reject them.
- I would guarantee everyone a decent floor, then let inequalities run freely above that threshold.
- I would bet on myself: rules that leave each person the fruit of their efforts, even at the risk of a modest place, rather than leveling everything.
