# The trolley problem

*Philippa Foot, 1967*

A runaway trolley is hurtling down the track. Straight ahead, five people are working on the line and cannot escape in time. You are standing next to a switch: by pulling it, you can divert the trolley onto a side track. But one person is working on that track, and will be killed if you act. If you do nothing, the five die; if you pull the switch, one person dies, by your hand. You have only seconds to decide.

**Would you pull the switch? And what makes such a choice right or wrong: the number of lives saved, or the fact of causing a death yourself?**

**Axes at stake** : [Criterion of right action](../axes/ethics-foundation.md)

**Possible stances** :

- I pull the switch: one life lost against five saved, the outcome is what counts.
- I do nothing: deliberately causing a death remains forbidden, whatever the number of lives at stake.
- I ask what a just and courageous person would do, and act accordingly, relying neither on a calculation nor on an absolute rule.
