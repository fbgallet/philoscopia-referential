# Three Dialogues between Hylas and Philonous

*[George Berkeley](../philosophers/berkeley.md) · 1713*

A dialogue restatement of the immaterialism of the *[Principles](../works/principles-of-human-knowledge-berkeley.md)*, in which Philonous, Berkeley's spokesman, leads the materialist Hylas step by step to admit that no mind-independent matter is even conceivable. Berkeley there presents idealism as the only secure rampart against scepticism and atheism, and as the defence of common sense.
