# Discourse on the Origin of Inequality

*[Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md) · 1755 · *Discours sur l'origine et les fondements de l'inégalité parmi les hommes**

Rousseau reconstructs, by hypothesis, the human of the state of nature: solitary, moved by self-love and pity alone, neither good nor wicked but innocent. It is society, with property, comparison, and inequality, that gives birth to amour-propre and to evil. The work founds the idea of a natural goodness corrupted by history.
