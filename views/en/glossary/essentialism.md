# Essentialism

Essentialism holds that things have an essence, a set of necessary properties that make them what they are, independent of our conventions: there is a nature of the triangle, of the oak, perhaps of the human being. [Plato](../philosophers/plato.md) locates it in the Ideas, [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md) in the form immanent to things. The question flares up again around living kinds, gender and identity, where essence is suspected of fixing and excluding. It stands against [existentialism](../glossary/existentialism.md) and, on kinds, against [nominalism](../glossary/nominalism.md).
