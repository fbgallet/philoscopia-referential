# Grace

Grace is, in Christian theology, the supernatural help God freely grants to the human being to raise him beyond his natural powers and orient him toward his final end. It cannot be earned and is not owed: it is a gift. According to the formula summed up by [Thomas Aquinas](../philosophers/aquinas.md), “grace does not destroy nature but perfects it”: far from contradicting reason or freedom, it presupposes and completes them. Thus, without grace, the will can accomplish a natural good but cannot of itself orient itself toward the [vision of God](../glossary/beatific-vision.md). The doctrine lay at the heart of major debates, over the accord of grace and [free will](../glossary/free-will.md), which set [Augustine](../philosophers/augustine.md) against his opponents and then ran through the whole of modernity.
