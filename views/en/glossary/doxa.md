# Doxa

In [Plato](../philosophers/plato.md), doxa is opinion, halfway between ignorance and knowledge. An opinion can be right and enough to act on; but so long as it is not tied down by an account that explains it, it remains unstable: whoever holds it can neither defend it nor keep hold of it, at the mercy of the clever who know how to turn it around. Hence a founding divide: the rhetoric that persuades without knowing reigns over doxa, philosophy seeks the examination that turns it into knowledge.
