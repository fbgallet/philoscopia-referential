# Fideism

From the Latin fides, "faith", fideism is the thesis that religious truth rests on faith, not on reason, which is too weak to reach God and prove anything of him. In [Montaigne](../philosophers/montaigne.md) it follows paradoxically from [skepticism](../glossary/skepticism.md): in the *[Apology for Raymond Sebond](../works/essays-montaigne.md)*, ruining reason's claims does not destroy faith but strips it of its false supports, religion being thereafter able to hold only by grace and authority. Fideism thus separates [faith and reason](../axes/faith-reason.md) while ranking them, where others would have them agree or clash.
