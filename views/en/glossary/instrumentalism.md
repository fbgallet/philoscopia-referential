# Instrumentalism

Instrumentalism holds that a technology is in itself neither good nor bad: it is merely a neutral means whose value depends entirely on the use made of it. Responsibility then falls on the user, not the object, as the common phrase has it: « it's not the tool, it's the use ». A middle position between [techno-optimism](../axes/technology.md) and [techno-criticism](../axes/technology.md), it is challenged by those who show that a device designed to produce a certain behaviour is never quite neutral.
