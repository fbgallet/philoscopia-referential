# The unmoved mover

*Concept associated with [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md)*

The unmoved mover is, in [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md), the ultimate principle of the universe's motion: to avoid an infinite regress of causes, one must posit a term that moves without itself being moved. This mover acts not mechanically but as a final cause, by being desired: it moves the world as the beloved object moves the one who loves it. Pure act and thought thinking itself, this impersonal divine is neither a creator nor a providence, but the perfection toward which everything tends.
