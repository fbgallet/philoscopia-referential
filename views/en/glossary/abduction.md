# Abduction

*Concept associated with [Peirce](../philosophers/peirce.md)*

The third form of inference isolated by [Peirce](../philosophers/peirce.md), beside deduction and [induction](../glossary/induction.md): faced with a surprising fact, abduction proposes the hypothesis which, if true, would render it a matter of course. It proves nothing, but it is the only mode of reasoning that introduces a new idea; deduction draws out its consequences and induction tests them. It is the creative moment of inquiry, the disciplined guess that opens research.
