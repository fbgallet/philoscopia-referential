# Theodicy

A theodicy, from the Greek theos, god, and dikê, justice, is an attempt to justify the goodness and omnipotence of God despite the existence of evil. The word is coined by [Leibniz](../philosophers/leibniz.md), who argues that our world, for all its ills, is "the best of all possible worlds". Others appeal to free will, the harmony of the whole, or an end that surpasses us. It is the great answer to the [problem of evil](../axes/problem-of-evil.md); its head-on refusal is called anti-theodicy.
