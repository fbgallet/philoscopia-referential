# Agape (love as gift)

The Greek term the early Christian texts use for the love of charity: a love that gives itself without condition or calculation, turned toward the other for their own sake rather than toward a good one would seek to possess. From the Greek agapē, to be distinguished from erōs, desire, and philia, friendship. It stands in this against [Plato](../philosophers/plato.md)'s [erōs](../glossary/eros.md) as lack: where erōs rises from need toward what would fill it, agape pours itself out freely, like charity (caritas) in [Thomas Aquinas](../philosophers/aquinas.md) or the exposure to the other in [Levinas](../philosophers/levinas.md). On the [nature of love](../axes/love-nature.md) axis, it gives its name to the pole of gift, facing erōs as lack and [philia](../glossary/philia.md) as friendship.
