# Contractualism

Contractualism grounds society and political power in a contract, real or hypothetical, between free and equal individuals: the common order is legitimate if everyone could consent to it. Starting from a [state of nature](../glossary/etat-de-nature.md), [Hobbes](../philosophers/hobbes.md) derives the sovereign State that protects from the war of all against all, [Locke](../philosophers/locke.md) a power limited by natural rights, [Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md) the [general will](../glossary/volonte-generale.md); [Rawls](../philosophers/rawls.md) renews the method in the twentieth century. It stands against the idea of a community that would precede individuals.
