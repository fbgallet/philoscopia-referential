# The tripartite soul

*Concept associated with [Plato](../philosophers/plato.md)*

In *[the Republic](../works/republic-plato.md)*, [Plato](../philosophers/plato.md) distinguishes three parts of the soul: reason, which knows and should govern; the spirited part (thymos), seat of courage and drive; and appetite, which craves bodily pleasures. The justice of the soul, like virtue, consists in reason ruling, the spirited part backing it, and appetite obeying. This hierarchy makes the good life a matter of the [rational governance](../axes/emotions.md) of the passions rather than of merely heeding them.
