# Finalism

Finalism explains natural phenomena by the ends for which they occur: the eye is made for seeing, the seed tends toward becoming a tree. Against the mere play of material causes, it holds nature to be ordered, directed toward completed forms. [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md) makes this the final cause, one of his [four causes](../glossary/four-causes.md); [Leibniz](../philosophers/leibniz.md) extends it to the scale of a world willed for the best. It stands against [mechanism](../glossary/mechanism.md), which admits only efficient causes.
