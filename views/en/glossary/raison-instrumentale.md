# Instrumental reason

*Concept associated with [Horkheimer](../philosophers/horkheimer.md)*

Instrumental reason is reason reduced to the calculation of means toward an end, indifferent to the worth of the ends themselves: a thought that assesses only efficiency. [Horkheimer](../philosophers/horkheimer.md) and [Adorno](../philosophers/adorno.md), in Dialectic of Enlightenment, make it the mainspring of domination: by learning to master nature through calculation, humanity extends the same scheme of control over other people and over itself. They read [Odysseus](../characters/odysseus.md), who binds himself to the mast and renounces in order to survive, as the first figure of this calculating subject who masters himself in order to master the world.
