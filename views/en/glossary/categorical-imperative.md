# Categorical imperative

*Concept associated with [Kant](../philosophers/kant.md)*

The categorical imperative is, for [Kant](../philosophers/kant.md), the moral command that holds unconditionally, independent of any aim or desire: it stands against hypothetical imperatives, which hold only as means toward some sought-after end. Its first formula demands that one act only on a **maxim** one could will to become a universal law, and another requires treating humanity, in oneself and in others, always also as an end and never merely as a means. The moral worth of an act thus lies in the form of duty, not in its consequences.
