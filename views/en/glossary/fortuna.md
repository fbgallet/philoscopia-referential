# Fortune

Fortune names the share of our lives that escapes our mastery: chance, circumstances, the shifting course of things. The ancient and medieval tradition, from the Stoics to [Boethius](../philosophers/boethius.md), urges detachment from it, the “wheel of Fortune” raising and lowering men without justice. [Machiavelli](../philosophers/machiavelli.md) gives it an active sense: fortune arbitrates about half our actions, the other half falling to [virtù](../glossary/virtu-machiavelli.md); it must be faced like a river to be dammed, and it smiles on the daring.
