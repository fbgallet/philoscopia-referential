# Pu 樸 (the uncarved block)

*Concept associated with [Laozi](../philosophers/laozi.md)*

Pu, or "the uncarved block," is in Taoist thought the image of natural simplicity before all culture: wood not yet carved, prior to distinctions, to manufactured desires and to the roles civilization imposes. The character 樸 denotes raw, unworked wood. The sage seeks neither to create nor to perfect himself, but to recover this original state by trimming away artifice, as one returns to a given nature. It is the exact reverse of the ideal of an [existence that freely invents itself](../axes/essence.md), and the path to a [quieting of desire](../axes/desire.md) through return to what is enough.
