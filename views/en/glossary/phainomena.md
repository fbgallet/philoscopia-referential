# Appearances (phainomena)

Phainomena are appearances, what shows itself to us and imposes itself without our deciding: that honey appears sweet, that a tower appears round from afar. The Pyrrhonian sceptic does not contest the appearance, indeed he regulates himself by it as the sole criterion of action, but he [suspends judgment](../glossary/epoche.md) about what things would be "by nature", beyond how they appear. Following appearances, natural inclinations and customs lets him act and live without asserting anything dogmatic about the hidden real. [Sextus Empiricus](../philosophers/sextus-empiricus.md) makes it the ground on which the sceptic consents to move.
