# Cosmopolitanism

Cosmopolitanism holds that one is first a citizen of the world (kosmos), not of a particular city: the moral community extends to all of humanity, beyond the arbitrariness of borders and of birth. The word kosmopolitēs is coined by [Diogenes](../philosophers/diogenes.md), who turns it into a provocative refusal of any homeland; the Stoics take it up by backing it with a universal city ordered by the [logos](../glossary/logos.md). It stands against rootedness in a local [belonging](../axes/belonging.md) and tends to relativise the [special obligations](../glossary/special-obligations.md) owed to one's near ones.
