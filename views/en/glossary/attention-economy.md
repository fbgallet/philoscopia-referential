# Attention economy

An economic model in which human attention, made scarce by the flood of information, is treated as a resource that devices seek to capture and then monetize. Our viewing time becomes the real commodity, which is why so many digital tools are built to hold us for as long as possible. The concept illuminates contemporary [criticism of technology](../axes/technology.md): a device is no longer quite neutral once its profitability depends on the time it takes from us.
