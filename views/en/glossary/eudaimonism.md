# Eudaimonism

Eudaimonism makes happiness (eudaimonia) the supreme end of human existence, understood not as a sum of pleasures but as the fulfillment of a flourishing life. For [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md), who works it out in the *[Nicomachean Ethics](../works/nicomachean-ethics-aristotle.md)*, this happiness lies in the virtuous activity of the soul in accordance with reason. It thus differs from [hedonism](../glossary/hedonism.md), which identifies the good with felt pleasure.
