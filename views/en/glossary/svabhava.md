# Svabhāva (inherent existence)

*Concept associated with [Nāgārjuna](../philosophers/nagarjuna.md)*

Svabhāva is the own-nature or intrinsic essence that a thing would possess by itself, independently of anything else: an autonomous, unconditioned existence. This is exactly what [Nāgārjuna](../philosophers/nagarjuna.md)'s Madhyamaka denies to every phenomenon. To deny svabhāva is to affirm [emptiness](../glossary/sunyata.md): nothing is grounded in itself, everything is relational and conditioned.
