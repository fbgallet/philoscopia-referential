# Dichotomy of control

*Concept associated with [Epictetus](../philosophers/epictetus.md)*

The dichotomy of control is the Stoic distinction between what is up to us (judgments, desires, will) and what is not (body, reputation, fortune). Formulated by [Epictetus](../philosophers/epictetus.md) at the very opening of the *[Enchiridion](../works/enchiridion-epictetus.md)*, it prescribes focusing effort on the former domain and meeting the latter with serenity. The practical core of [Stoicism](../movements/stoicism.md), it paves the way for [amor fati](../glossary/amor-fati.md).
