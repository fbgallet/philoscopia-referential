# Semiotics (the sign)

*Concept associated with [Peirce](../philosophers/peirce.md)*

The general theory of signs developed by [Peirce](../philosophers/peirce.md): every sign is a three-term relation, the **representamen** that stands for something, the object it represents, and the interpretant, the effect of meaning it produces in a mind. Far from being confined to language, semiotics covers everything that signifies, and Peirce includes thought itself: to think is to handle signs referring to other signs, to the point that « man is a sign ». Meaning thus never resides in an isolated object but in this triadic process of interpretation.
