# Scientism

Scientism is the conviction that experimental science is the only source of genuine knowledge, and that it will eventually answer every question, including moral, political or metaphysical ones. Carried by the positivist enthusiasm of the nineteenth century, in [Auguste Comte](../philosophers/comte.md) and his heirs, it makes the scientific method the measure of all meaningful discourse. Its critics see in it a science turned ideology, blind to whatever escapes measurement, meaning, value, lived experience. The word is most often pejorative.
