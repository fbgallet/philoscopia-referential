# The general will

*Concept associated with [Jean-Jacques Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md)*

A central concept of [Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md)'s Social Contract: the general will is the will of the entire body politic directed toward the common good, not the sum of particular interests (the « will of all »). In obeying the law he helped to will as a citizen, the individual obeys only himself and remains free: this is political liberty as autonomy. Much debated, the idea grounds a [positive liberty](../axes/political-liberty.md) and a democratic legitimacy, but its invocation to « force men to be free » has also fed the critique of an authoritarian risk.
