# Self-love and amour-propre

*Concept associated with [Jean-Jacques Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md)*

A cardinal distinction of [Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md). Self-love (amour de soi) is the natural and good passion by which every being attends to its preservation and well-being; it bears no relation to others and is tempered by [pity](../glossary/pitie.md). Amour-propre, by contrast, is born in society: a relative, factitious sentiment that drives each to compare himself, to want to come first and to draw his self-esteem from the regard of others. From this comparative passion arise vanity, envy, inequality and domination. All moral evil thus comes not from man's nature but from this denaturing.
