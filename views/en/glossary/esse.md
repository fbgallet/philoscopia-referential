# Esse (the act of being)

*Concept associated with [Thomas Aquinas](../philosophers/aquinas.md)*

Esse from the Latin esse, the verb "to be" taken as a noun, the act of existing designates, in [Thomas Aquinas](../philosophers/aquinas.md), the act by which a thing exists, distinct from its essence, that is, from what it is. Taking up Aristotelian [hylomorphism](../glossary/hylomorphism.md), Thomas adds to it this real distinction: in every creature, the essence does not possess existence of itself, it receives it. In God alone is the essence to exist: he is “subsistent being itself” (ipsum esse subsistens), the source that communicates being to all the rest. The [nature of things](../axes/essence.md) is therefore genuinely real, yet radically dependent, hanging at every instant on the act of being that God gives it.
