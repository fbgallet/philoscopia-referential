# Abstract general ideas

The doctrine that the mind forms general ideas by abstraction, isolating what individuals have in common: the idea of **man** in general, or of a triangle that is neither equilateral nor scalene. [Locke](../philosophers/locke.md) makes it the mainspring of knowledge and language. [Berkeley](../philosophers/berkeley.md) denies it: one cannot form the idea of a triangle that is no particular triangle; every idea is particular, and a word becomes general not through an abstract essence but because a particular sign stands indifferently for all its likes. At stake is the whole axis of [universals](../axes/universals.md), from realism to nominalism.
