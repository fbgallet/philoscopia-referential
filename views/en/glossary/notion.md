# Notion

*Concept associated with [Berkeley](../philosophers/berkeley.md)*

[Berkeley](../philosophers/berkeley.md)'s technical term for the knowledge we have of spirits and relations, where we can have no idea of them at all. An idea, for him, is always passive and perceived; but the mind that perceives and wills is active, and God still more so. We therefore have no idea of the self or of God, but a **notion**, an immediate grasp of our own activity and of what resembles it. This is how [immaterialism](../glossary/immaterialism.md) can deny matter without denying spirit, even though no one perceives spirit as an idea.
