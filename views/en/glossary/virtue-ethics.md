# Virtue ethics

Virtue ethics shifts the moral question: not "what should I do?" but "who should I be?". Acting well is acting as the virtuous person would, courageous, just, temperate; morality is a matter of character, formed by habit, more than of rules or calculations. [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md) sets its model: the virtues are acquired through practice and aim at the accomplished life, [eudaimonia](../glossary/eudaimonism.md). Rediscovered in the twentieth century by Anscombe and MacIntyre, it stands against [consequentialism](../glossary/consequentialism.md) and [deontologism](../glossary/deontologism.md) alike, judged blind to character and to practical wisdom, [phronesis](../glossary/phronesis.md).
