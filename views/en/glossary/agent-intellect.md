# Agent intellect

The agent intellect is, in the Aristotelian and scholastic tradition, the power of the mind that abstracts intelligible forms from sense images. Since all knowledge begins with the senses, the mind first receives only particular data: it is the agent intellect that draws the universal out of them, illuminating the sensible content so as to make its essence intelligible. The notion comes from [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md)'s De anima and was at the centre of fierce medieval debates: [Averroes](../philosophers/averroes.md) held this intellect to be single and separate, common to all human beings, where [Thomas Aquinas](../philosophers/aquinas.md) holds it to be a faculty proper to each soul. It explains how the [mind rises from the sensible to the intelligible](../axes/knowledge-source.md) without resorting to [innate ideas](../glossary/innate-ideas.md).
