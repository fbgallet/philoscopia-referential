# Spiritual exercises

The name [Pierre Hadot](../philosophers/hadot.md) took up and established for the practices by which the ancient schools made philosophy a way of life: meditating on maxims, examining one's conscience, rehearsing misfortunes in advance, attending to the present. These exercises aim less at knowing more than at becoming other: working on one's desires, fears and judgments so as to transform one's way of existing, without excluding study, which is part of them. The word 'spiritual' keeps a religious resonance that Hadot accepted: it marks that the exercise engages the whole person, not the intellect alone.
