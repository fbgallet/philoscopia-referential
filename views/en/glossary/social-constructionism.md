# Social constructionism

Social constructionism holds that many categories taken to be natural, gender, race, madness, value, are in fact produced by history, language and social relations: they could have been otherwise. "One is not born, but rather becomes, a woman", writes [Beauvoir](../philosophers/beauvoir.md); [Foucault](../philosophers/foucault.md) shows how institutions shape the madman or the delinquent, Judith Butler how gender is performed. The thesis liberates by denaturalizing, but is charged with dissolving all reality. It stands against social naturalism and social realism.
