# Natural law

Natural law is the rational creature's participation in the [eternal law](../glossary/eternal-law.md) by which God orders all things to their end. It is neither a variable convention nor an arbitrary decree: it consists of the precepts that reason derives from [human nature](../axes/human-nature.md) itself, beginning with the first of them, "do good and avoid evil". Accessible to every human being through reason alone, believer or not, it grounds an objective and universal morality. The doctrine, classically formulated by [Thomas Aquinas](../philosophers/aquinas.md), makes natural law the medieval ancestor of natural rights and the criterion to which human law must conform in order to bind in conscience.
