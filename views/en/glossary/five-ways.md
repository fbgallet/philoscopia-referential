# The Five Ways

*Concept associated with [Thomas Aquinas](../philosophers/aquinas.md)*

The Five Ways are the five a posteriori demonstrations, that is, arguments from observed effects by which [Thomas Aquinas](../philosophers/aquinas.md) establishes God's existence. Each starts from a fact of experience and rises, to avoid an infinite regress, to a first term: motion leads to a first mover, causation to an uncaused cause, contingency to a necessary being, the degrees of perfection to a supremely perfect being, and the ordering of ends to an intelligence directing nature. Against [Anselm](../philosophers/anselm.md), Thomas refuses to deduce God from the concept alone: his existence is not self-evident to us, it is proved from the sensible world. The Five Ways have become the classic model of the [rational proof of God](../axes/god-existence.md).
