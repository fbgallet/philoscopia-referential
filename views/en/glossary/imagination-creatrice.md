# Creative imagination

*Concept associated with [Coleridge](../philosophers/coleridge.md)*

For Romanticism, imagination is not the faculty that combines borrowed images but a creative power that brings forth the new and gives form to reality. [Coleridge](../philosophers/coleridge.md) distinguishes it from mere fancy: the « primary » imagination repeats in the human being the infinite creative act, the « secondary » is the artist's. It thus becomes the organ by which the mind does not reflect the world but [recreates](../axes/art-nature.md) it.
