# Philia (friendship)

The Greek term for the love of friendship: a reciprocal, chosen bond in which each wills the other's good for the other's own sake. From the Greek philia, distinct from erōs, desire, and agapē, gift. In the *[Nicomachean Ethics](../works/nicomachean-ethics-aristotle.md)*, [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md) makes it the summit of the good life: perfect friendship, founded on shared virtue, in which the friend is like another self, is distinguished from the fragile friendships founded on usefulness or pleasure. On the [nature of love](../axes/love-nature.md) axis, it gives its name to the pole of friendship, facing [erōs](../glossary/eros.md) as lack and [agape](../glossary/agape.md) as gift.
