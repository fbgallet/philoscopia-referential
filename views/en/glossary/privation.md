# Privation (privatio boni)

*Concept associated with [Plotinus](../philosophers/plotinus.md)*

Privation from the Latin privatio, a taking away is the absence of a perfection a thing ought to possess, conceived not as a positive reality but as a lack of being. Taken up to resolve [the problem of evil](../axes/problem-of-evil.md), it denies evil any substance of its own: evil is not a power rivaling the good but a defect, an absence of good. [Plotinus](../philosophers/plotinus.md) thus identifies matter, at the lowest degree of being, with a near-nothingness, and it is from him that [Augustine](../philosophers/augustine.md) inherits the formula of evil as privatio boni, the privation of good.
