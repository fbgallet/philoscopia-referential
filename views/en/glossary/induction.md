# Induction

Induction is the reasoning that moves from the particular to the general, or from the past to the future: having always seen the sun rise, I conclude that it will rise tomorrow. It is the mainspring of all knowledge drawn from experience, and so of science. Yet [Hume](../philosophers/hume.md) exposed its fragility: nothing proves that nature will remain tomorrow what it has been, and our confidence in that regularity rests on habit more than on reason. This "problem of induction" bears on our [relation to certainty](../axes/certainty.md) as much as on the [source of our knowledge](../axes/knowledge-source.md).
