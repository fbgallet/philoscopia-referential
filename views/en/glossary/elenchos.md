# Elenchus (refutation)

*Concept associated with [Socrates](../philosophers/socrates.md)*

The elenchus is the method of [Socrates](../philosophers/socrates.md): through a series of questions, he leads the interlocutor to acknowledge that their answers contradict one another, and so that they did not know what they thought they knew. From the Greek elenkhos: examination, testing, refutation. Far from a mere art of winning, this refutation has a moral aim: by dispelling false certainties, it reopens the ground for inquiry and embodies the [examined life](../axes/interiority.md).
