# Natural attitude

*Concept associated with [Husserl](../philosophers/husserl.md)*

The natural attitude is, in [Husserl](../philosophers/husserl.md), our spontaneous, unreflective relation to the world: in it we take things, others and the world itself to be simply existent, set out there before us, without questioning what makes them appear to us. It is the stance of everyday life and of the sciences alike, turned toward their objects with no regard for the consciousness that intends them. The [phenomenological reduction](../glossary/phenomenological-reduction.md) consists precisely in suspending this attitude, not to deny it, but to make visible the work of consciousness it ordinarily leaves in shadow.
