# Anaideia (shamelessness)

*Concept associated with [Diogenes](../philosophers/diogenes.md)*

Anaideia names deliberate Cynic shamelessness: performing in public, without blushing, what convention requires to be hidden. [Diogenes](../philosophers/diogenes.md) makes it a method rather than gratuitous coarseness: to transgress accepted shame is to demonstrate by deed that it belongs to [nomos](../glossary/physis-nomos.md) and not to nature. Shamelessness thus exposes the arbitrariness of custom and calls for living according to [physis](../glossary/physis-nomos.md). It extends [parrhēsia](../glossary/parrhesia.md), frank speech, by carrying it into the register of the body and of scandal.
