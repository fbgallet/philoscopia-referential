# Pity (pitié)

*Concept associated with [Jean-Jacques Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md)*

In [Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md), pity is the natural, innate reluctance to see a sentient being suffer, prior to all reflection and all morality. Together with self-love it is one of the two principles of natural man: by making us identify with the one who suffers, it tempers our self-interest and grounds, before reason, all the social virtues. It is on this feeling, not on a calculation or a duty, that Rousseau builds morality, and he extends it to « every sentient being ».
