# Radical evil

*Concept associated with [Kant](../philosophers/kant.md)*

Radical evil designates, in [Kant](../philosophers/kant.md)'s *[Religion within the Bounds of Bare Reason](../works/religion-within-the-bounds-of-bare-reason-kant.md)*, an innate propensity of the human being to subordinate the moral law to self-love, that is, to grant duty only a place conditioned by one's own interest. Radical does not mean absolute or diabolical, but rooted: this tendency lies at the very root of our freedom and accompanies every human will. It does not, however, abolish responsibility, since it remains imputable and can be overcome through a revolution in moral disposition.
