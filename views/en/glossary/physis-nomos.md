# Physis and nomos (nature versus convention)

The opposition of physis and nomos is a great divide of Greek thought: physis names what holds by nature, valid everywhere and always, while nomos names what holds only by human custom and law, varying from one city to another. The sophists invoke it to denounce justice as mere convention; the Cynics, such as [Diogenes](../philosophers/diogenes.md), turn it into a sorting criterion: whatever is mere nomos, wealth, rank, modesties, can be undone, for nature alone binds. To live according to physis is to strip away that artifice down to real need alone.
