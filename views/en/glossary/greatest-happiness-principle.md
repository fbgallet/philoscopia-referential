# Greatest happiness principle

*Concept associated with [Jeremy Bentham](../philosophers/bentham.md)*

The greatest happiness principle, formulated by [Bentham](../philosophers/bentham.md) and taken up by [Mill](../philosophers/mill.md), holds that the right act is the one that maximizes the happiness of all those affected. Morality is then judged neither by intention nor by adherence to a rule, but by [consequences](../axes/ethics-foundation.md): one must weigh pleasures and pains for the greatest number. Resting on the idea that the ultimate good is pleasure, it extends [hedonism](../glossary/hedonism.md) to a collective scale.
