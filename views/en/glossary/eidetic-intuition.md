# Eidetic intuition (Wesensschau)

*Concept associated with [Husserl](../philosophers/husserl.md)*

Eidetic intuition is the grasping of an essence (eidos) itself, not of a particular fact. [Husserl](../philosophers/husserl.md) carries it out through free variation: starting from an example, even an imagined one, one varies its features in thought until the invariant emerges without which the thing would no longer be what it is, its necessary structure. It differs from mere empirical generalisation, which only gathers observed cases: the eidos gives itself with an evidence of its own, independent of how many examples there are. It is the act by which phenomenology becomes a science [of essences](../axes/essence.md) rather than of the merely existent.
