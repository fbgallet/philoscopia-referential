# Consequentialism

Consequentialism judges an action by its consequences alone: good is what produces the best state of the world, whatever the intentions or the rules followed. Its most famous version is the [utilitarianism](../movements/utilitarianism.md) of [Bentham](../philosophers/bentham.md) and [Mill](../philosophers/mill.md), which maximizes the happiness of the greatest number according to the [greatest happiness principle](../glossary/greatest-happiness-principle.md). Its classic opponent is [deontologism](../glossary/deontologism.md); the standard objection is that it could sacrifice the innocent to the good of the greater number.
