# Pragmatic maxim

*Concept associated with [Peirce](../philosophers/peirce.md)*

A rule for clarifying meaning stated by [Peirce](../philosophers/peirce.md) in *[How to Make Our Ideas Clear](../works/how-to-make-our-ideas-clear-peirce.md)*: to grasp the meaning of a conception, consider what conceivable practical effects, observable or experimental, its object would have; the whole meaning of the conception lies in those effects, and nothing more. Two ideas that entail no conceivable practical difference have the same meaning. Peirce intended it as a tool of logical clarity, not a criterion of truth; he later coined « pragmaticism » to set it apart from the use [James](../philosophers/william-james.md) made of it.
