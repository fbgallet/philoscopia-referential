# Principium individuationis

The principium individuationis, 'principle of individuation' in scholastic Latin, names what makes a being this particular individual, distinct from every other. [Schopenhauer](../philosophers/schopenhauer.md) takes up the phrase to name space and time: it is they that parcel the single [Will](../glossary/vouloir-vivre.md) into a multitude of apparently separate beings. The separateness of individuals is thus only an effect of our way of knowing, a veil of [māyā](../glossary/maya.md); [compassion](../glossary/compassion.md) is the moral experience in which this veil tears.
