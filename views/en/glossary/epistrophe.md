# Epistrophē (conversion, reversion)

*Concept associated with [Plotinus](../philosophers/plotinus.md)*

Epistrophē from the Greek epistrophê, a turning around, a returning designates, in the Neoplatonism of [Plotinus](../philosophers/plotinus.md), the return of each level of reality toward its principle, the reverse movement of [emanation](../glossary/emanation.md) that completes it. What proceeds from the [One](../glossary/the-one.md) tends to convert back to it, and procession is achieved only through this reversion. For the human soul, epistrophē is the contemplative ascent that brings it back to its source, up to [union](../glossary/henosis.md).
