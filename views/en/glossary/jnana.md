# Jñāna (liberating knowledge)

Jñāna is the path of knowledge by which one realizes the identity of [ātman](../glossary/atman.md) and [brahman](../glossary/brahman.md), not a mere theoretical knowing but a direct realization that transforms the one who attains it. For [Śaṅkara](../philosophers/shankara.md), it is this knowledge, and neither ritual action nor devotion, that liberates: one does not become brahman, one already is it, and it suffices to dispel the [ignorance](../glossary/avidya.md) that had veiled it. Jñāna thus stands against the path of works (karma) and that of devotion (bhakti), which are held to be preparatory.
