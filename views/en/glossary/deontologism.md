# Deontologism

Deontologism grounds morality in duty, from the Greek deon, what must be done: an action is good if it conforms to principles valid for everyone, regardless of its consequences. [Kant](../philosophers/kant.md) gives it its most rigorous form with the [categorical imperative](../glossary/categorical-imperative.md): do not lie, never treat another person as a mere means, even when it would be useful. It stands against [consequentialism](../glossary/consequentialism.md), which judges by effects.
