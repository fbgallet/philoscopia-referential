# Intentionality

Intentionality is the feature by which consciousness is always consciousness of something: every lived experience, perceiving, judging, desiring, aims at an object and refers to it. Taken over from the scholastics by Brentano to mark off the mental from the physical, it becomes in [Husserl](../philosophers/husserl.md) the guiding thread of [phenomenology](../movements/phenomenology.md), which describes the various ways an object gives itself to an act. It governs how one conceives the [mind's relation to the real](../axes/reality-nature.md), binding the two together rather than setting them face to face.
