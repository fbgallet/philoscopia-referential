# Life-world (Lebenswelt)

*Concept associated with [Husserl](../philosophers/husserl.md)*

The life-world is the world as perceived, lived and taken for granted prior to all theory: the shared ground of experience in which we move and understand ourselves. [Husserl](../philosophers/husserl.md) makes it the central theme of the *[Crisis of the European Sciences](../works/crisis-of-the-european-sciences-husserl.md)*: the exact sciences forget that their idealities are built upon this sensed world and presuppose it, to the point of mistaking their method for being itself. To recover the life-world is to restore science to [its place as a derived knowledge](../axes/science-place.md), led back to the experience that gives it meaning.
