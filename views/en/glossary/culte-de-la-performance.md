# The cult of performance

A phrase coined by the sociologist Alain Ehrenberg to describe a trait of our societies: the individual is enjoined to realize themselves through success, to outdo themselves without end, to be the entrepreneur of their own self. The sporting model, competition, ranking, the record, gives it a telling form. Its deeper spring is a recent norm, that of autonomy: when the rule is no longer so much to obey as to become oneself and succeed, failure ceases to be an accident and becomes a fault. Hence what Ehrenberg calls "the weariness of the self": exhaustion, even depression, as the underside of the injunction to be forever up to the mark.
