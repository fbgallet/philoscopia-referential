# The four causes

*Concept associated with [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md)*

For [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md), to explain a thing fully is to answer four questions, its four causes: the material cause (what it is made of, the bronze of a statue), the formal cause (its structure or definition), the efficient cause (what produces it, the sculptor), and the final cause (the end for the sake of which it exists). The final cause is decisive: nature does nothing in vain, and each being tends toward the end proper to it. This explanation by ends grounds Aristotelian **finalism** and connects with [hylomorphism](../glossary/hylomorphism.md).
