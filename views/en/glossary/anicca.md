# Anicca (impermanence)

*Concept associated with [The Buddha](../philosophers/buddha.md)*

Anicca is the mark of impermanence: all conditioned things arise, change and dissolve ceaselessly, and nothing lasting can be seized within the flux. It is the observation from which [non-self](../glossary/anatta.md) and [suffering](../glossary/dukkha.md) follow, for wanting to hold on to what passes breeds disappointment. For [the Buddha](../philosophers/buddha.md), contemplating impermanence, including that of our own death, calms attachment rather than feeding dread.
