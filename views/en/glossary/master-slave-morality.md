# Master and slave morality

*Concept associated with [Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md)*

In *[On the Genealogy of Morals](../works/on-the-genealogy-of-morality-nietzsche.md)*, [Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md) distinguishes two origins of our values. Master morality starts from itself: it calls "good" what is noble, strong, and affirmative, and "bad" what is weak. Slave morality, by contrast, is born of [ressentiment](../glossary/ressentiment.md): it begins by condemning the powerful as "evil" in order then to call itself "good", thereby inverting values. Depending on the standpoint from which one judges, "good" thus no longer names the same thing.
