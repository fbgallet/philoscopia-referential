# Beatific vision

*Concept associated with [Thomas Aquinas](../philosophers/aquinas.md)*

The beatific vision is, in [Thomas Aquinas](../philosophers/aquinas.md), the direct contemplation of God's essence, in which the human being's perfect beatitude consists. Taking up [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md)'s [eudaimonism](../glossary/eudaimonism.md), Thomas shows that no finite good satisfies human desire, which is stilled only in the universal good: God himself. This vision is [the final end](../axes/sovereign-good.md) to which nature aspires without being able to reach it alone, unreachable in this life and received by [grace](../glossary/grace.md). It marks the point where Greek [eudaimonism](../glossary/eudaimonism.md) is at once preserved and surpassed, happiness being referred to a supernatural perfection.
