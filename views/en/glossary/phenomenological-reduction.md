# Phenomenological reduction (transcendental epochē)

*Concept associated with [Husserl](../philosophers/husserl.md)*

The phenomenological reduction is the methodical move by which [Husserl](../philosophers/husserl.md) brackets the existence-positing of the [natural attitude](../glossary/natural-attitude.md): one stops naively holding the world to be simply existent, in order to describe phenomena purely as they give themselves to consciousness. It neither denies the world nor doubts it: it only suspends the judgment of being so as to bring out the field of lived experiences and their [intentionality](../glossary/intentionality.md). It thus differs from the sceptical [epochē](../glossary/epoche.md): where the sceptic suspends for want of evidence, Husserl suspends in order to reach a more originary evidence, that of the [thing showing itself](../axes/certainty.md).
