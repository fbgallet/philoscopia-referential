# Immanence

Immanence characterizes what remains interior to a domain, without appeal to an external principle: a meaning, values, or a divine that exist only within the world itself. [Spinoza](../philosophers/spinoza.md) is its great classical figure, with a God identified with nature, and [Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md) radicalizes it by calling on us to remain faithful to the earth. It stands opposed to [transcendence](../glossary/transcendence.md), which places the ground beyond the world.
