# Immaterialism

*Concept associated with [Berkeley](../philosophers/berkeley.md)*

Immaterialism is [Berkeley](../philosophers/berkeley.md)'s thesis that there is no mind-independent matter: what we call bodies is only an ordered collection of sensible ideas, and the only things that truly exist are the minds that perceive them and the ideas they have. Far from denying the world, Berkeley claims to save it from scepticism by removing the veil of an unknowable matter set between us and it. It is the most radical form of [idealism](../axes/reality-nature.md), in which to exist comes down to [being perceived](../glossary/esse-est-percipi.md).
