# Mētis (cunning intelligence)

Mētis, in Greek culture, is a form of practical intelligence made of skill, foresight, quickness and adaptation to the particular case: the art of the fitting stratagem, the feint and the right moment (kairos), by which the weak or the wily prevail over the strong. [Odysseus](../characters/odysseus.md), the man "of many turns", is its heroic figure, as the goddess Athena is among the gods. Distinct from theoretical knowledge as from force, it is akin to the practical [wisdom](../glossary/phronesis.md) that [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md) would later theorize.
