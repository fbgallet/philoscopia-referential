# Nirguṇa brahman (the attributeless absolute)

*Concept associated with [Śaṅkara](../philosophers/shankara.md)*

Nirguṇa brahman is the absolute as it ultimately is: [brahman](../glossary/brahman.md) without any attribute, beyond all quality, all form and all relation. [Śaṅkara](../philosophers/shankara.md) distinguishes it from the personal God, creator and object of devotion (saguṇa brahman, Īśvara), which holds reality only at the conventional level: God for the one who journeys, the impersonal for the one who knows. These are not two gods but two levels of truth, and ultimate knowledge passes through the personal figure to reach the absolute without determination.
