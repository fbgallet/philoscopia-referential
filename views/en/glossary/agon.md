# Agon

From the Greek agôn, struggle, contest, the agôn is the ruled competition through which the Greeks pursued excellence: the Olympic games, tragic contests, the debates of the city. This rivalry was nothing gentle: it mixed the thirst for glory, envy, and the risk of excess (**hubris**). [Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md), in a text on Homer's contest, draws from it above all a fertile rivalry, the "good Eris", which drives each person to surpass themselves not to crush the other but because the presence of a rival awakens the best in oneself. This reading makes the agôn the noble ancestor of sporting competition: a struggle that elevates, so long as it does not degenerate into a will to destroy.
