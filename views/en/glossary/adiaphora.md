# Indifferents (adiaphora)

Indifferents are, for the Stoics, all the things that are neither good nor bad in themselves: health, wealth, reputation, and their opposites. Only virtue and vice truly count, since they alone are up to us. The Stoics nonetheless distinguish preferred indifferents, in accordance with nature, from dispreferred ones: it is reasonable to pursue the former, provided one never pins one's happiness on them.
