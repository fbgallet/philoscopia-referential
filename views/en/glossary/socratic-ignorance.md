# Socratic ignorance

*Concept associated with [Socrates](../philosophers/socrates.md)*

Socratic ignorance is the awareness of one's own lack of knowledge, which [Socrates](../philosophers/socrates.md) holds to be the only wisdom within his reach: told by the Delphic oracle that he is the wisest of men, he understands that his superiority lies in not believing he knows what he does not. This "learned ignorance" is no renunciation of truth, but the starting point of all inquiry and the mainspring of the [elenchus](../glossary/elenchos.md). The phrase "learned ignorance," docta ignorantia, is in fact coined much later, in the fifteenth century, by Nicholas of Cusa.
