# Avidyā (ignorance)

Avidyā names the beginningless ignorance that veils true reality and makes one mistake the manifold for the ultimate. In Advaita Vedānta it is what superimposes the world's plurality and the individual ego onto the one [brahman](../glossary/brahman.md), as one believes one sees a snake where there is only a rope. It is not a fault but a cognitive error, and only [liberating knowledge](../glossary/jnana.md) can dispel it, revealing the [Self](../glossary/atman.md) it had concealed.
