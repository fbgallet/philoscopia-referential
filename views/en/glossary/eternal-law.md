# Eternal law

The eternal law is, in the thought of [Thomas Aquinas](../philosophers/aquinas.md), God's very reason insofar as it governs the universe and orders each being to its end. It is the [providential](../glossary/providence.md) plan presiding over the whole of creation, prior to any human law and to any particular will. The other laws derive from it: the [natural law](../glossary/natural-law.md) is only the share of it that the rational creature reaches through reason, while human law applies its requirements to circumstances. It thus expresses that the order of the world is not blind chance but the effect of a wisdom that regulates it from all eternity.
