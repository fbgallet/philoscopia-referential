# Causal closure

Causal closure is the thesis that the physical world forms a complete chain of causes: every physical effect has a cause that is itself physical, with no intervention from some other order of reality. It is a cornerstone of [determinism](../glossary/determinism.md) as applied to our decisions, since the brain that deliberates belongs to that physical order. In the philosophy of mind, it also tells against the idea of an immaterial soul acting on the body: if nothing is missing on the side of physical causes, a cause of another kind would have nowhere to insert itself. Yet it does not by itself entail that everything is settled in advance: it remains to be seen whether those causes fix a single outcome or leave some play.
