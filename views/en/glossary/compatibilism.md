# Compatibilism

Compatibilism holds that freedom and [determinism](../glossary/determinism.md) are not contradictory: an act is free when it flows from the agent's own will, even if that will itself has causes. This middle position, already defended by [Stoicism](../movements/stoicism.md) with Chrysippus' cylinder, refuses to sacrifice moral responsibility to the laws of nature. It thereby redefines [free will](../glossary/free-will.md) as the absence of constraint rather than an absolute power to have chosen otherwise.
