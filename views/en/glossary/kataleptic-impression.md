# Kataleptic impression (phantasia katalêptikê)

The kataleptic, or cognitive, impression is for the Stoics the representation that grasps its object with such evidence that it certifies itself and could not have come from a non-existent object. It is the Stoic criterion of truth: only this kind of impression deserves our [assent](../glossary/synkatathesis.md). This confidence in a certain knowledge makes Stoicism a [dogmatic](../glossary/dogmatism.md) school, against the Skeptics who disputed that such impressions even exist.
