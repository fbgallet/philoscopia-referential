# Taṇhā (craving, thirst)

*Concept associated with [The Buddha](../philosophers/buddha.md)*

Taṇhā, literally "thirst," is the craving that drives us to chase pleasure, existence, or even annihilation. It is the second of the [Four Noble Truths](../glossary/four-noble-truths.md): the cause of [suffering](../glossary/dukkha.md), for it is greedy attachment that binds us to the cycle of rebirth. For [the Buddha](../philosophers/buddha.md), extinguishing this thirst, rather than satisfying it, is the path to liberation.
