# Perfectibility

*Concept associated with [Jean-Jacques Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md)*

Coined by [Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md) in the Discourse on the Origin of Inequality, perfectibility names the properly human faculty of transforming and modifying oneself indefinitely, for better and for worse. Where the animal is wholly governed by instinct, man has no fixed nature: he makes himself through his history, his education and his institutions. This plasticity grounds the optimism of the [Enlightenment](../movements/enlightenment.md), for which vices stem from environment rather than from a corrupt nature; in [Condorcet](../philosophers/condorcet.md) it becomes the mainspring of a progress without end.
