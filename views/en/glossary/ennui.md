# Boredom

Boredom is not, in [Schopenhauer](../philosophers/schopenhauer.md), a mere passing discomfort but the revelation of the emptiness of existence: as soon as a desire is satisfied and nothing further solicits us, life discovers that it has, in itself, no content. It forms one of the two poles between which the pendulum of life swings without rest, the other being the pain of unsatisfied desire. Unlike diversion in [Pascal](../philosophers/pascal.md), which masks our misery, Schopenhauerian boredom lays it bare.
