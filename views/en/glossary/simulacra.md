# Simulacra (eidola)

In Epicurean physics, simulacra are the thin films of atoms that bodies continually shed from their surface and that, striking the senses, explain perception. Subtler still, they account for dreams and thought, in which the mind directly grasps these drifting images. [Epicurus](../philosophers/epicurus.md) sees in them the material mechanism of all knowledge through the senses, and [Lucretius](../philosophers/lucretius.md) gives their fullest account in book IV of the *[De rerum natura](../works/on-the-nature-of-things-lucretius.md)*.
