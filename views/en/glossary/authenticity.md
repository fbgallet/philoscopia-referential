# Authenticity

*Concept associated with [Jean-Paul Sartre](../philosophers/sartre.md)*

Authenticity is the stance by which one owns one's freedom and one's facticity, the inherited situation one did not choose, without fleeing it into [bad faith](../glossary/bad-faith.md). In [Sartre](../philosophers/sartre.md), it is acknowledging oneself as solely responsible for the meaning one gives one's [existence](../axes/essence.md); in Heidegger, from whom the term first comes, it is recovering oneself as one's own self against the conformism of the impersonal "they." It demands an inner lucidity and a coherence between what one is and what one shows.
