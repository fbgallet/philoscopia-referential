# The Two Truths (saṃvṛti / paramārtha)

*Concept associated with [Nāgārjuna](../philosophers/nagarjuna.md)*

The doctrine of the two truths distinguishes conventional truth (saṃvṛti), that of the everyday world, of things, words, and practices, from ultimate truth (paramārtha), which reveals their [emptiness](../glossary/sunyata.md). [Nāgārjuna](../philosophers/nagarjuna.md) stresses that the Buddha's teaching rests on this distinction. Emptiness therefore does not abolish conventional reality: it discloses its true status, without denying that things work and matter in ordinary life.
