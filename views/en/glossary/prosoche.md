# Prosochē

Prosochē (attention) is, for the [Stoics](../movements/stoicism.md), the continuous vigilance kept at every moment on the judgments and impulses of the [ruling faculty](../glossary/hegemonikon.md). To examine each [impression](../glossary/phantasia.md) before granting it [assent](../glossary/synkatathesis.md), rather than mechanically following one's impressions: such is the fundamental posture of spiritual exercise, which [Epictetus](../philosophers/epictetus.md) and [Marcus Aurelius](../philosophers/marcus-aurelius.md) never cease to recall. It is attention at every instant, by which wisdom is lived in the present rather than merely known.
