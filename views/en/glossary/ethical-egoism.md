# Ethical egoism

Ethical egoism holds that each person ought to pursue their own interest, and that this is indeed the only rational end of action: systematic devotion to others would be a mistake, even a servitude. To be distinguished from psychological egoism, the mere claim that we are as a matter of fact driven by self-interest: ethical egoism makes it a duty. Stirner anchors it in the sovereignty of the individual, Ayn Rand in a defense of rational self-interest. It stands against [altruism](../glossary/altruism.md).
