# Nominalism

Nominalism holds that only individuals exist: general properties, such as redness or humanity, are not realities but names, from the Latin nomen, that we apply to things that resemble one another. William of Ockham is its great medieval figure, at the heart of the quarrel over universals. It stands against [realism about universals](../glossary/realism-of-universals.md), for which redness or the circle really exist, apart from things or within them.
