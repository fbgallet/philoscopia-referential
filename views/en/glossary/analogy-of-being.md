# Analogy of being (analogia entis)

*Concept associated with [Thomas Aquinas](../philosophers/aquinas.md)*

The analogy of being is the doctrine by which [Thomas Aquinas](../philosophers/aquinas.md) explains how "being" and the perfections (goodness, wisdom) can be predicated both of God and of creatures. It is neither univocally, in the same sense, which would bring God down to the level of things, nor equivocally, in a wholly different sense, which would make all discourse about him impossible: it is by analogy, according to a relation of proportion. The perfections of creatures are found in God in an eminent way, in a degree and a mode that escape us. The doctrine thus makes it possible to speak of God without reducing him to the finite or sinking into sheer silence, honouring at once his [transcendence](../glossary/transcendence.md) and his presence to every thing.
