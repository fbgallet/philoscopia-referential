# Esse est percipi (to be is to be perceived)

*Concept associated with [Berkeley](../philosophers/berkeley.md)*

From the Latin esse, to be, and percipi, to be perceived. The formula by which [Berkeley](../philosophers/berkeley.md) sums up his [immaterialism](../glossary/immaterialism.md): the existence of a sensible thing consists in nothing other than its being perceived by a mind. A colour, a sound, a taste has no being apart from the perception one has of it, and to posit a body that would exist while perceived by no one is to contradict oneself. What persists when no human perceives it does not escape the rule: there must then be an [infinite mind](../axes/reality-nature.md) that perceives it always, God.
