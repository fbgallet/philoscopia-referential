# Theory of Forms

*Concept associated with [Plato](../philosophers/plato.md)*

[Plato](../philosophers/plato.md)'s theory of Forms (or Ideas) holds that beyond sensible things, which are changing and imperfect, there exist intelligible realities that are eternal and perfect: the Forms, such as the Beautiful or the Just in itself. The objects of the world are only copies participating in these models, of which reason alone can have true **knowledge**. This twofold reality grounds [recollection](../glossary/anamnesis.md) and gives the philosophical quest its meaning as the search for a [transcendent](../axes/transcendence.md) truth.
