# Positive liberty

Positive liberty defines freedom as autonomy: to be free is not merely to be left alone, but to govern oneself, to be master of one's desires and, as a citizen, the author of the laws one obeys. [Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md) and [Hegel](../philosophers/hegel.md) are its great figures, in the wake of Kantian autonomy. [Berlin](../philosophers/berlin.md) holds it to be the noblest of liberties but also the most exposed to perversion, when "liberating" turns into coercion in the name of the "true self". It stands against [negative liberty](../glossary/negative-liberty.md).
