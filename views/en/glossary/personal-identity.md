# Personal identity

Personal identity is what makes me remain, through time and change, one and the same person, and the question of what this rests on. The answers diverge: an unchanging [thinking substance](../axes/self-nature.md) ([Descartes](../philosophers/descartes.md)), the [continuity of memory](../axes/self-nature.md) ([Locke](../philosophers/locke.md)), the [story one tells of one's life](../axes/self-nature.md) (Ricoeur), or a mere [bundle of states](../axes/self-nature.md) with no stable self ([Hume](../philosophers/hume.md), Buddhism). The stakes are sharpest around survival after death: for there to be the survival of someone, and not the persistence of something, what remains must be tied closely enough to the earlier person to be that person still.
