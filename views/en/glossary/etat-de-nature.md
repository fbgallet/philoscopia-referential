# The state of nature

The state of nature is the hypothetical condition of humanity before or without political society, used by contract theorists to think the legitimacy of power. Its figure varies by author: for [Hobbes](../philosophers/hobbes.md) it is a « war of all against all » that makes the absolute state necessary; for [Locke](../philosophers/locke.md), a state governed by a natural law but lacking an impartial judge; for [Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md), a peaceful state in which solitary man, moved by self-love and [pity](../glossary/pitie.md), is naturally good, and which only society corrupts. Depending on what one places there, one draws opposite conclusions about man and the state.
