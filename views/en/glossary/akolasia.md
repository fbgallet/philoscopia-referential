# Akolasia (intemperance)

Akolasia is intemperance or license: the lack of self-mastery, the surrender to appetites one neither sorts nor restrains. From Greek a- (privative) and kolazein, to restrain or chastise: literally, the uncurbed. It is the exact opposite of sōphrosynē, temperance, which Greek ethics holds to be a cardinal virtue. Where the tradition condemns it, [Callicles](../characters/callicles.md), in the *[Gorgias](../works/gorgias-plato.md)*, makes it the very condition of happiness: letting one's [desires](../axes/desire.md) grow unchecked and having the strength to fill them.
