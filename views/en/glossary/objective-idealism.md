# Objective idealism

*Concept associated with [Peirce](../philosophers/peirce.md)*

[Peirce](../philosophers/peirce.md)'s late metaphysics, according to which matter is but « effete mind », made of habits grown so inveterate as to become physical laws: mind is primary, and the physical is one of its congealed forms. It differs from [Berkeley](../philosophers/berkeley.md)'s subjective idealism, for which things exist only as perceived, because the mind in question is objective, real independently of any particular perception. Backed by [synechism](../glossary/synechism.md), which forbids separating matter and mind into clean-cut substances, this doctrine refuses both [materialism](../axes/reality-nature.md) and dualism.
