# Innate ideas

*Concept associated with [René Descartes](../philosophers/descartes.md)*

Innate ideas are, for [Descartes](../philosophers/descartes.md), ideas the mind holds within itself prior to any experience, such as the idea of God, of the self, or mathematical truths. They come neither from the senses nor from outside, but from the very structure of thought, which anchors knowledge in [reason](../axes/knowledge-source.md). This rationalist claim stands opposed to empiricism, which holds the mind to be a [blank slate](../glossary/tabula-rasa.md) wholly shaped by experience.
