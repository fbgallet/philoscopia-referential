# Śūnyatā (emptiness)

*Concept associated with [Nāgārjuna](../philosophers/nagarjuna.md)*

Emptiness names the absence, in every thing, of an [inherent nature](../glossary/svabhava.md): nothing possesses intrinsic, self-standing existence, since everything arises in dependence on conditions. The central thesis of [Nāgārjuna](../philosophers/nagarjuna.md), it does not claim that nothing exists, but that nothing exists on its own. Properly understood, it coincides with [dependent origination](../glossary/pratityasamutpada.md) and does not deny the conventional reality of the [two truths](../glossary/two-truths.md).
