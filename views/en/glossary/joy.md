# Joy

In ordinary use, joy is an agreeable affect, a feeling of fulfilment. Several philosophers give it a precise technical sense. For [Spinoza](../philosophers/spinoza.md), joy (laetitia) is the passage from a lesser to a greater perfection, the increase of our power to act, bound up with the [conatus](../glossary/conatus.md): as such it is always good, and ethics aims to cultivate it. For Bergson, by contrast, joy signals that life has achieved something, that a creation has occurred, as the mark of a victory of the creative impulse.
