# Free will

Free will is the power of the will to determine itself: faced with an alternative, the agent could have chosen otherwise. It is widely held to be the condition of moral responsibility, and [Kant](../philosophers/kant.md) makes it the very foundation of morality. The question of whether we are [genuinely free in our choices](../axes/freedom.md) sets it in direct opposition to [determinism](../glossary/determinism.md).
