# Individualism

Individualism, in the philosophical sense, holds that society is nothing over and above the individuals who compose it and their interactions: to understand a social phenomenon is to trace it back to persons' choices and reasons. It also carries a moral and political thesis: the individual, with their rights and interests, comes before the demands of the whole, as in the liberalism of [Locke](../philosophers/locke.md) and [Mill](../philosophers/mill.md). It has nothing to do with everyday selfishness: it is a way of explaining and grounding the social order, opposite to [holism](../glossary/holism.md).
