# Akrasia (weakness of will)

Akrasia is acting against one's own judgment: knowing that something is bad and doing it anyway, through weakness before pleasure or passion. From the Greek a- (privative) and kratos, power: the lack of self-mastery. [Socrates](../philosophers/socrates.md) denies that it is possible, since in his eyes no one does evil willingly; [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md), by contrast, sets out to explain how the will can be overcome while judgment stays right.
