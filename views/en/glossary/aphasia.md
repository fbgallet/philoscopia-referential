# Aphasia (non-assertion)

Aphasia is the sceptic's "saying nothing": from the Greek a- privative and phasis, assertion, it names the refusal to utter any assertion about what is not evident. It is the verbal side of [suspension of judgment](../glossary/epoche.md): faced with reasons of equal strength ([isostheneia](../glossary/isostheneia.md)), the sceptic does not fall entirely silent, but he abstains from positing that things are thus rather than otherwise. His own formulas, "no more this than that", are themselves uttered with no claim to truth, like a purge that is voided along with what it expels.
