# Saṃsāra (the cycle of rebirths)

Saṃsāra is the cycle of rebirths and suffering to which one's deeds (karma) bind a being for as long as ignorance lasts. Common to Hinduism, Buddhism and Jainism, it names the wandering through countless existences, rooted in attachment to the world and to the self. To be freed from it is the ultimate aim: [mokṣa](../glossary/moksha.md) in Vedānta, [nirvāṇa](../glossary/nirvana.md) in Buddhism. For [Śaṅkara](../philosophers/shankara.md), it is [ignorance](../glossary/avidya.md) that sustains it, and knowledge of the self alone ([jñāna](../glossary/jnana.md)) that liberates from it.
