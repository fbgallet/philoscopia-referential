# Idols of the mind (idola)

*Concept associated with [Bacon](../philosophers/bacon.md)*

For [Bacon](../philosophers/bacon.md), the idols of the mind are the systematic sources of error that distort knowledge and that the mind must purge before it can practise a true [induction](../glossary/induction.md). He distinguishes four: the idols of the tribe, common to the whole human species, which lends nature more order than it has; the idols of the cave, proper to each individual's history; the idols of the marketplace, slipped into thought by language and ill-made words; and the idols of the theatre, the received systems and dogmas, played out like fables on a stage. It is because a belief is justified by the [reliability of the method that produces it](../axes/epistemic-justification.md), and not by some first intuition, that the mind must first be scoured of its idols.
