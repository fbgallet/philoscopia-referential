# Hylomorphism

*Concept associated with [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md)*

Hylomorphism (from Greek hule, matter, and morphe, form) is [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md)'s thesis that every sensible being is the inseparable union of a matter and a form: bronze is the matter of the statue, its shape is the form. Neither bare matter nor pure form exists separately in the sensible world; it is their composition that constitutes the concrete substance. Form is not a mere arrangement reducible to matter: it organizes and actualizes the matter, bringing out what the thing really is.
