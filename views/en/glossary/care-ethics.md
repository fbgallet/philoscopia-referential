# Ethics of care

The ethics of care grounds morality in attention to vulnerable persons and in the caring relationships that bind us: we are beings who depend on one another, not abstract contracting parties. Framed in the 1980s from the work of the psychologist Carol Gilligan, it reconnects with an older tradition that grounds morality in feeling: [pity](../glossary/pitie.md) in [Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md), compassion in [Schopenhauer](../philosophers/schopenhauer.md). It thus challenges the primacy of [deontologism](../glossary/deontologism.md) and [consequentialism](../glossary/consequentialism.md), which forget that moral life begins in concrete relationships.
