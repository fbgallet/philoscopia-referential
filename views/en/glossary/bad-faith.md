# Bad faith

*Concept associated with [Jean-Paul Sartre](../philosophers/sartre.md)*

Bad faith, in [Sartre](../philosophers/sartre.md), is the lie one tells oneself in order to flee one's radical freedom, by treating oneself as a thing endowed with a fixed nature. The waiter who overplays his role, or the person who claims to be "born that way," reassure themselves by denying that they choose at every moment what they are. It is a flight from the anguish of [existence](../axes/essence.md) without prior **essence**, the illusion of coinciding with a ready-made being.
