# Impartiality

Impartiality is the demand to count every person equally, favoring no one on the grounds of their ties to us. From the impartial moral point of view, a stranger's suffering weighs as much as a loved one's, and favoring our own appears as a leaning that reason must correct. [Kant](../philosophers/kant.md) makes it a condition of the moral law; [Singer](../philosophers/singer.md) extends it to every being capable of suffering. Its flip side is the sometimes crushing demand it imposes, and the distance it can open up with our dearest attachments.
