# Xenia (guest-friendship)

Xenia, in the Homeric world, is the sacred, reciprocal bond uniting host and guest under the guard of Zeus the protector of strangers: to welcome, feed, shelter and give gifts to the stranger, who in turn owes the like one day. To violate it, as the Cyclops does in devouring his guests, or the suitors in plundering [Odysseus](../characters/odysseus.md)'s house, is a grave offense that calls for punishment. The whole world of the *[Odyssey](../works/odyssey-homer.md)* is judged by this duty.
