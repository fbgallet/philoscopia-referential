# Altruism

Altruism makes the disinterested concern for others the mainspring and the end of moral life: to act well is to seek the other's good for their own sake, even placing their interests before one's own. The word is coined by [Auguste Comte](../philosophers/comte.md), who opposes it to egoism and makes it the heart of his morality. [Levinas](../philosophers/levinas.md) radicalizes it into a responsibility for the other that precedes freedom; evolutionary theories, for their part, seek its roots in mutual aid. It stands against [ethical egoism](../glossary/ethical-egoism.md).
