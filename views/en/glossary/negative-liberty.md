# Negative liberty

Negative liberty defines freedom as the absence of hindrance: I am free to the extent that no one prevents me from acting as I see fit. It is measured by the extent of the domain left without interference, and assigns to the law the task of protecting it rather than narrowing it. [Berlin](../philosophers/berlin.md) makes it one of his "two concepts of liberty"; Constant had already tied it to the liberty of the Moderns, [Nozick](../philosophers/nozick.md) and Hayek to the defense of the market. It stands against [positive liberty](../glossary/positive-liberty.md).
