# Pleonexia (the drive to have more)

Pleonexia is the desire to have always more than one's share and more than others: the appetite to prevail, to possess and to dominate beyond what falls to each. Greek thought sees in it a root of injustice, held in check by temperance and equality. In the *[Gorgias](../works/gorgias-plato.md)*, [Callicles](../characters/callicles.md) turns it into a natural right: the superior man is right to want more, and to be content with an equal share would be, in his view, a slave's soul.
