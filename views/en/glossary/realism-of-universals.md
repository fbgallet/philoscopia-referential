# Realism about universals

Realism about universals holds that general properties really exist, and not only in our minds or our words: redness, the circle or justice are realities to be discovered. [Plato](../philosophers/plato.md) places them in a separate intelligible world, the [theory of Forms](../glossary/theory-of-forms.md); [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md) lodges them in things themselves, as their forms. Facing it, [nominalism](../glossary/nominalism.md) sees in them nothing but names.
