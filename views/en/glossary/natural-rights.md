# Natural rights

The doctrine of natural rights holds that certain rights are inherent in the person, prior to the state and to positive law: they are discovered, not granted, and a law that violates them remains unjust even if perfectly legal. [Locke](../philosophers/locke.md) grounds them in natural law to justify the right of resistance; [Kant](../philosophers/kant.md) ties them to the dignity and autonomy of the person. They inspire the declarations of human rights, against legal positivism, for which there are no rights but those an authority institutes.
