# Hinge propositions

*Concept associated with [Wittgenstein](../philosophers/wittgenstein.md)*

Hinge propositions are, in the last [Wittgenstein](../philosophers/wittgenstein.md) (On Certainty), those propositions neither known nor doubted ("here is a hand", "the earth existed long before my birth") but which form the frame that makes knowledge and doubt possible. "If I want the door to turn, the hinges must stay put": they are held fast not by proofs but by our practice, and the skepticism that would revoke them saws off the branch it sits on.
