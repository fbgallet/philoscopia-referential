# Custom

Custom is established usage, the fold of habit a people receives from its place and time and ends up taking for nature itself. [Montaigne](../philosophers/montaigne.md) makes it his "“violent and treacherous schoolmistress”": it shapes what each of us holds to be natural and reasonable, and is thus the great engine of belief against which he turns his [skepticism](../glossary/skepticism.md). Seeing that our moral certainties owe to the [usages of one's country](../axes/moral-scope.md) rather than to reason disarms fanaticism, yet also feeds a wariness of change, since the authority of laws lies in their antiquity more than in their foundation.
