# Rationalism

Rationalism holds that reason has resources of its own, independent of experience: [innate ideas](../glossary/innate-ideas.md), necessary truths, first principles. [Descartes](../philosophers/descartes.md), [Spinoza](../philosophers/spinoza.md) and [Leibniz](../philosophers/leibniz.md) are its great classical figures: the senses can deceive, only reason grounds certain knowledge. It stands against [empiricism](../glossary/empiricism.md); in a broader sense, the word also names the trust placed in reason against authority and revelation.
