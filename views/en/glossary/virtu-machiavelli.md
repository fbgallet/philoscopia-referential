# Virtù

*Concept associated with [Machiavelli](../philosophers/machiavelli.md)*

For [Machiavelli](../philosophers/machiavelli.md), virtù is not moral virtue but the excellence proper to the man of action: the energy, boldness, presence of mind and suppleness by which he seizes the occasion, stands up to [fortune](../glossary/fortuna.md) and imposes his form on events. A founder, a captain, a people can possess it. It is measured by success, not by goodness, and is worth all the more for knowing how to fit the quality of the times. From the Italian virtù, from the Latin virtus, strength and worth, derived from vir, man.
