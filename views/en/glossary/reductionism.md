# Reductionism

Reductionism explains a whole by its simplest parts: to understand is to trace the complex back to its elements and their laws, life to chemistry, thought to neurons, the social to individuals. Inherited from the atomism of Democritus, it is the engine of many scientific successes. Its opponents object that certain properties of a whole, consciousness, the living, cannot be read off its components taken separately. It stands against [emergentism](../glossary/emergentism.md).
