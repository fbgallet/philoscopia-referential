# Atheism

Atheism, from the Greek a- privative and theos, god, denies the existence of God: no divine reality grounds the world, and the very idea of God is explained by human causes. Feuerbach sees in it the projection of our own essence, [Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md) proclaims the "death of God" and draws its consequences for morality, [Russell](../philosophers/russell.md) puts the burden of proof back on the believer. It differs from agnosticism, which suspends judgment.
