# Political realism

Political realism starts from "the effective truth of the matter" rather than imagined republics: to govern is to work with human beings as they are, with their passions and the balance of forces, not as they ought to be. [Machiavelli](../philosophers/machiavelli.md) is its founding figure; [Hobbes](../philosophers/hobbes.md), and then the realist tradition in international relations, extend it. It stands against political utopianism and severs effectiveness from ideal morality.
