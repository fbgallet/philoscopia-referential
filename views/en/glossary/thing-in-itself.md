# The thing-in-itself

*Concept associated with [Kant](../philosophers/kant.md)*

The thing-in-itself, or noumenon, designates in [Kant](../philosophers/kant.md) reality as it is independently of the way we perceive it. We only ever know phenomena, that is, things as they appear to us through the forms of our sensibility, space and time, and the categories of our understanding. The thing-in-itself thus remains thinkable but unknowable, and this limit traces, for Kant, the boundary of knowledge.
