# Mimēsis (imitation)

Mimēsis (imitation) is the classical conception of art, framed by [Plato](../philosophers/plato.md) and [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md): the work represents nature or human actions, and is worth the accuracy of that imitation. From the Greek mimēsis, imitation, representation. Romanticism rejects it in the name of [creation](../glossary/imagination-creatrice.md): art no longer copies a given model, it invents one. This is the stake of the [nature of art](../axes/art-nature.md) axis.
