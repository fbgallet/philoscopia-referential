# Eros (love as desire)

The Greek term for love as desire: to love is to reach for what one does not have and judges beautiful or good. From the Greek erōs, distinct from philia, friendship, and agapē, gift. In the *[Symposium](../works/symposium-plato.md)*, [Plato](../philosophers/plato.md) makes it the child of Penia (Poverty) and Poros (Resource): born of lack, erōs surges from beautiful bodies up to beauty itself. On the [nature of love](../axes/love-nature.md) axis, it gives its name to the pole of lack, facing [agape](../glossary/agape.md) as gift and [philia](../glossary/philia.md) as friendship.
