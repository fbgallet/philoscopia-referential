# The separation of powers

*Concept associated with [Montesquieu](../philosophers/montesquieu.md)*

A principle formulated by [Montesquieu](../philosophers/montesquieu.md) in The Spirit of the Laws: to secure liberty and prevent abuse, the three functions of the state, making the law (legislative), executing it (executive) and judging (judiciary), must be entrusted to distinct bodies that check one another. « It is an eternal experience that every man who has power is led to abuse it »: so it must be that, « by the arrangement of things, power checks power ». A centerpiece of the constitutionalism of the [Enlightenment](../movements/enlightenment.md), this device would inspire the American and French constitutions.
