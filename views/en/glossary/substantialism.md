# Substantialism

Substantialism holds that the self is a substance: a permanent core, distinct from its states, remaining identical beneath the flow of thoughts and experiences. It is the soul of the religious traditions, [Descartes](../philosophers/descartes.md)' thinking thing, or again the [ātman](../glossary/atman.md) of the orthodox Indian schools. [Hume](../philosophers/hume.md)'s bundle of perceptions and the Buddhist [non-self](../glossary/anatta.md) are its most famous negations.
