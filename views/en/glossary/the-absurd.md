# The absurd

*Concept associated with [Camus](../philosophers/camus.md)*

The absurd arises, according to [Camus](../philosophers/camus.md), from the encounter between the human desire for meaning and the unreasonable silence of the world: neither man nor the world is absurd in itself, only their confrontation is. [Camus](../philosophers/camus.md) rejects the two easy ways out, suicide and religious hope, and proposes living the absurd in lucid revolt. [Meursault](../characters/meursault.md), the narrator of *[The Stranger](../works/the-stranger-camus.md)*, is its fictional embodiment.
