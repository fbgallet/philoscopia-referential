# Anattā (non-self)

*Concept associated with [The Buddha](../philosophers/buddha.md)*

Anattā is the claim that there is no permanent self hidden behind the changing aggregates that make up a person: the body, sensations, perceptions, mental formations and consciousness. What we take to be a substantial "I" is only a flux of impersonal, [impermanent](../glossary/anicca.md) processes. For [the Buddha](../philosophers/buddha.md), grasping the absence of self loosens attachment and opens the path to liberation.
