# Sceptical modes (tropes)

The tropes, or modes, are the patterns of argument by which the sceptic produces the equal force of contrary reasons ([isostheneia](../glossary/isostheneia.md)) and brings about [suspension of judgment](../glossary/epoche.md). Tradition distinguishes two sets: the ten modes of Aenesidemus, which play off the variety of perceptions across animals, human beings, the senses, circumstances and customs, and the five, more formal modes of Agrippa, which corner any claim to knowledge into disagreement, infinite regress, relativity, unproven hypothesis or circularity. [Sextus Empiricus](../philosophers/sextus-empiricus.md) preserves and systematizes them as the arsenal of [skepticism](../movements/skepticism.md) against the [dogmatists](../glossary/dogmatism.md).
