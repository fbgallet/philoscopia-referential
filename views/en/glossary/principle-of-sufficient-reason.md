# Principle of sufficient reason

The principle of sufficient reason states that nothing happens without a reason that makes it so rather than otherwise. Formulated by [Leibniz](../philosophers/leibniz.md), it lies at the heart of [determinism](../glossary/determinism.md): if every effect has its reason in its causes, a causeless decision would be an unintelligible exception. To reject it is not to find a freedom, but to give up explaining.
