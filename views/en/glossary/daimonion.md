# Daimonion (divine sign)

*Concept associated with [Socrates](../philosophers/socrates.md)*

The daimonion is the "divine sign" that [Socrates](../philosophers/socrates.md) says he has heard since childhood: an inner voice that never commands him to do anything, but turns him away when he is about to act wrongly. From the Greek daimonion, a diminutive of daimōn, a divine power. Socrates invokes it at his trial as a mark of his piety, and this singular experience fed the charge of introducing "new divinities."
