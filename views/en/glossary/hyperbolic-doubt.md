# Hyperbolic doubt

*Concept associated with [Descartes](../philosophers/descartes.md)*

A method devised by [Descartes](../philosophers/descartes.md): pushing doubt to its extreme, up to the hypothesis of an evil genius deceiving us about everything, so as to keep as true only what withstands it absolutely. This doubt is not [sceptical](../glossary/skepticism.md) but provisional and methodic: it serves to isolate a fixed point, the **cogito**, and to rebuild knowledge. Descartes himself calls these doubts "hyperbolical" in the *[Sixth Meditation](../works/meditations-descartes.md)*, once certainty has been regained.
