# Transhumanism

*1990 – ?*

A contemporary current of thought, born in the 1980s-1990s in the wake of cybernetics and biotechnology, which holds human nature to be not an untouchable given but a starting point to be surpassed: through medicine, genetics, prosthetics and artificial intelligence, the human being could [augment its physical and mental capacities](../axes/human-enhancement.md), extend its life indefinitely, even [defeat death](../axes/death.md). The word was coined by the biologist Julian Huxley in 1957, but the movement took organized form later around figures such as [Max More](../philosophers/max-more.md), [Nick Bostrom](../philosophers/bostrom.md) and Ray Kurzweil, and the idea of the [Singularity](../glossary/singularity.md). Heir to the [Enlightenment](../movements/enlightenment.md) and its faith in progress, it extends the modern project of mastering nature to the human being itself, and stirs sharp ethical controversy over limits, equality of access, and the meaning of a life without end. The movement is itself diverse, from libertarian Extropians to democratic transhumanists: not all give equal weight to capacity enhancement, the end of aging and immortality.

**Key figures** : [Nick Bostrom](../philosophers/bostrom.md) · [Max More](../philosophers/max-more.md)

## Core positions

1. [Enhancing human nature](../axes/human-enhancement.md) : **Augmenting the human being beyond its natural limits is not a transgression but a task: "human nature" is a contingent starting point, not a norm to be preserved.**
2. [Death](../axes/death.md) : **Death is not the fate that gives life its worth, but the greatest of evils and a technical problem: aging is a disease, and defeating it, a goal.**
3. [Essence and existence](../axes/essence.md) : **There is no finished human essence: the human is a being in transition, a bridge toward the [posthuman](../glossary/posthuman.md), defined by what it can become, not by what it is.**
4. [Relation to technology](../axes/technology.md) : **Technology is the engine of human emancipation: what freed us from scarcity can free us from our very biological limits.**

## Positions

### [Enhancing human nature](../axes/human-enhancement.md)

*positioned · explicit in the texts · major*

Transhumanism 100%

Where bioconservatism sees in human nature a limit to be respected, transhumanism treats the body and mind we are given as a contingent starting point, shaped by blind evolution. Nothing sanctifies this inheritance: to forbid medicine, genetics or cognitive enhancement from extending our capacities in the name of "nature" would be a superstition. Enhancement is merely the deliberate continuation of what culture and technology have always done.

*Sources : More, 'Transhumanism: Toward a Futurist Philosophy' · Bostrom, 'In Defense of Posthuman Dignity'*

### [Death](../axes/death.md)

*positioned · explicit in the texts · major*

Immortalism 80% · Revolt / refusal 20%

Transhumanism breaks with the wisdoms of acceptance: death is neither an indifferent nothingness, as for [Epicurus](../philosophers/epicurus.md), nor the horizon that would give life its worth, as for [Heidegger](../philosophers/heidegger.md), but the greatest of harms and a technical problem. Aging is recast as a curable disease. A share of revolt remains: to refuse finitude as an injustice to be fought, not a fate to be tamed.

> “We will no longer tolerate the tyranny of aging and death.” (Max More, 'A Letter to Mother Nature' (1999))

*Sources : Bostrom, 'The Fable of the Dragon-Tyrant' · de Grey, Ending Aging*

### [Essence and existence](../axes/essence.md)

*positioned · explicit in the texts · major*

Existentialism 90% · Essentialism 10%

There is no fixed human essence that enhancement would betray: what we call "human nature" is a transitory state within a process of self-transformation. The human is an unfinished work, a bridge toward the [posthuman](../glossary/posthuman.md) rather than a completed form; its value lies in the capacities and experiences a being can unfold, not in the preservation of a type. The stance remains naturalist, however: it does not deny any given nature, it denies that nature is sacred and final.

*Sources : More, 'On Becoming Posthuman' · Bostrom, 'Why I Want to Be a Posthuman When I Grow Up'*

### [Relation to technology](../axes/technology.md)

*positioned · explicit in the texts · major*

Techno-optimism 93% · Techno-criticism 8%

Technology is neither a neutral tool nor an alienating force, but the chief engine of emancipation from the human condition: what pulled us out of scarcity and disease can free us from our very biological limits. The [existential risks](../glossary/existential-risk.md) it raises do not warrant renunciation, but a prudent, steered acceleration. A desirable future is built by pushing back the frontiers of the technically possible, not by distrusting them.

*Sources : Kurzweil, The Singularity Is Near · Bostrom, Superintelligence*

### [Tradition and transformation](../axes/conservatism-progressism.md)

*positioned · explicit in the texts · major*

Progressivism 98% · Conservatism 3%

Transhumanism carries the [Enlightenment](../movements/enlightenment.md)'s faith in progress to its limit: not only knowledge and institutions, but the human being itself can and must be improved. No tradition, no inherited order holds an authority that would withstand a demonstrable improvement; the past is merely a starting point that reason and technology are meant to surpass.

*Sources : More, 'The Extropian Principles' · Condorcet, Sketch for a Historical Picture of the Progress of the Human Mind (lineage)*

### [Humanity's place in nature](../axes/human-place-nature.md)

*positioned · inferable · major*

Mastery 85% · Stewardship 10% · Member among others 5%

Extending the Cartesian project of making ourselves "masters and possessors of nature", from which it drops even the prudent "as if", transhumanism stretches that mastery to human nature itself. The human is neither a mere member of the natural order to conform to, nor only its steward, but the agent called to reconfigure it and to consciously steer its own evolution: nature has no normativity, it is material to be transformed.

*Sources : Descartes, Discourse on the Method, VI · More, 'A Letter to Mother Nature'*

## Influenced by

- **[Bacon](../philosophers/bacon.md)** (continues) : Transhumanism extends [Bacon](../philosophers/bacon.md)'s program: turning knowledge into effective power over nature, through experimental science, to relieve the human condition. It radicalizes that promise by stretching this command of nature to the human body and mind themselves.
- **[Condorcet](../philosophers/condorcet.md)** (continues) : [Condorcet](../philosophers/condorcet.md) had already written into the Enlightenment idea of progress the indefinite perfectibility of man, up to the possible lengthening of human life. Transhumanism takes up this hope and hands it the means of biotechnology.
- **[Descartes](../philosophers/descartes.md)** (continues) : The Cartesian formula of man as "master and possessor of nature" gives transhumanism its motto, from which it erases the cautious "as if": the mastery sought is no longer analogical but literal, and extends to human nature itself.
- **[Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md)** (reinterprets) : Transhumanism sometimes claims [Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md)'s overman, reinterpreted as a biotechnological posthuman. The lineage is largely an over-reading: the Nietzschean overcoming is spiritual and tragic, not technical, and Nietzsche would likely be deeply critical of this craving for absolute control, in which he would see a refusal of becoming and finitude, a hatred of life contrary to amor fati.

## Influence on

- **[Fukuyama](../philosophers/fukuyama.md)** (reacts against) : [Fukuyama](../philosophers/fukuyama.md) deems transhumanism 'the world's most dangerous idea': by reshaping human nature, enhancement would erode the basis of equal dignity on which rights rest, and risk splitting humanity in two. He counters the project with a defense of human nature as a normative limit.
- **[Habermas](../philosophers/habermas.md)** (reacts against) : [Habermas](../philosophers/habermas.md) challenges the liberal eugenics transhumanism carries: a being genetically programmed by others could no longer experience itself as the sole author of its life, breaking the moral symmetry and reciprocity between persons and between generations.
- **[Sandel](../philosophers/sandel.md)** (reacts against) : [Sandel](../philosophers/sandel.md) sees in enhancement the expression of a Promethean will to mastery: wanting to choose and optimize everything would lose the sense of life's 'giftedness', and with it the humility, solidarity and gratitude rooted in what we did not choose.
