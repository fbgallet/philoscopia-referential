# Movements

- [Aristotelianism](aristotelianism.md) (335 BCE – 1274) : Every being tends toward its end, and humans [flourish](../glossary/eudaimonism.md) by actualizing their proper function, reason.
- [Buddhism](buddhism.md) (500 BCE – ?) : Understanding [suffering (dukkha)](../glossary/dukkha.md) and ending it by releasing the [craving (taṇhā)](../glossary/tanha.md) and the illusion of a self.
- [Confucianism](confucianism.md) (500 BCE – ?) : Cultivating oneself into a person of noble character (junzi), and ordering the world through ritual propriety (li 禮) and humaneness.
- [Cynicism](cynicism.md) (400 BCE – 200 BCE) : Living according to nature rather than convention, free through [self-sufficiency](../glossary/autarkeia.md) and [frank speech](../glossary/parrhesia.md).
- [Empiricism](empiricism.md) (1620 – 1776) : All our ideas come from experience: nothing is in the mind that was not first in the senses.
- [Epicureanism](epicureanism.md) (307 BCE – 200) : [Pleasure as the absence of disturbance](../glossary/hedonism.md): freeing oneself from vain fears to live an [untroubled life](../glossary/ataraxia.md).
- [Existentialism](existentialism.md) (1843 – 1980) : [Existence precedes essence](../axes/essence.md): condemned to be [free](../axes/freedom.md), we invent ourselves.
- [Marxism](marxism.md) (1848 – ?) : The philosophers have only interpreted the world: the point is to **change** it.
- [Phenomenology](phenomenology.md) (1900 – ?) : Returning "to the things themselves": describing lived experience as it gives itself to consciousness.
- [Platonism](platonism.md) (387 BCE – 529) : The truly real is not the sensible but the [Forms](../glossary/theory-of-forms.md), and the soul ascends toward the Good.
- [Pragmatism](pragmatism.md) (1870 – ?) : An idea is worth the practical difference it makes: the true is what gets verified in inquiry.
- [Rationalism](rationalism.md) (1637 – 1716) : [Reason, not the senses](../axes/knowledge-source.md), gives access to the true order of reality.
- [Romanticism](romanticism.md) (1795 – 1850) : Against the cold reason of the Enlightenment, the claims of feeling, imagination and genius, and the mystery of a living nature.
- [Skepticism](skepticism.md) (365 BCE – 200) : Suspending judgment before the equal force of opposing arguments, to find [tranquillity](../glossary/ataraxia.md).
- [Stoicism](stoicism.md) (300 BCE – 180) : Living by nature and reason, serene before [what lies outside our control](../glossary/dichotomy-of-control.md).
- [Taoism](taoism.md) (400 BCE – ?) : Following [the dao 道 (the Way)](../glossary/dao.md) through [wu wei 無為 (non-action, action without forcing)](../glossary/wu-wei.md), and recovering [ziran 自然 (the spontaneity of the natural)](../glossary/ziran.md).
- [The Enlightenment](enlightenment.md) (1685 – 1804) : Dare to use your own understanding: through reason and [enlightenment](../glossary/lumieres.md), humanity can free itself from the tutelage of authority, from superstition and from arbitrary power.
- [Transhumanism](transhumanism.md) (1990 – ?) : Extending, augmenting, even abolishing the human condition through technology: aging, disease and death as problems to be solved.
- [Utilitarianism](utilitarianism.md) (1789 – ?) : The good is the [greatest happiness of the greatest number](../glossary/greatest-happiness-principle.md).
- [Vedānta (Advaita)](vedanta.md) (700 – ?) : The [Self (ātman)](../glossary/atman.md) is identical to the [absolute (brahman)](../glossary/brahman.md): to know this unity is to be free.
