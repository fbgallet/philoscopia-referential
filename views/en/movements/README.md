# Movements

- [Analytic Philosophy](analytic-philosophy.md) (1900 – ?) : Treating philosophical problems as knots to [untangle](../axes/metaphilosophy.md): what exactly are we saying, and what actually follows from our words and our reasons?
- [Aristotelianism](aristotelianism.md) (335 BCE – 1274) : Thinking each being through what it can accomplish: a tradition for which a [flourishing life](../glossary/eudaimonism.md) is built through the exercise of reason and the virtues.
- [Buddhism](buddhism.md) (500 BCE – ?) : Making [suffering](../glossary/dukkha.md) a practical inquiry: seeking out its causes so as to undo them, through ethics, attention and meditation.
- [Confucianism](confucianism.md) (500 BCE – ?) : Cultivating oneself to set the world in order: the rites (li 禮), study and humaneness shape the person who can hold a harmonious society together.
- [Cynicism](cynicism.md) (400 BCE – 200 BCE) : Making one's life a provocation against false needs: voluntary poverty and [frank speech](../glossary/parrhesia.md) put conventions to the test.
- [Empiricism](empiricism.md) (1620 – 1776) : Requiring every idea about the world to measure itself against experience: observation, not speculation, is what puts our beliefs to the test.
- [Environmental Ethics](environmental-ethics.md) (1949 – ?) : Moving the moral question [beyond the human](../axes/moral-circle.md): what do we owe animals, the living world and future generations, and [what place should we hold in nature](../axes/human-place-nature.md)?
- [Epicureanism](epicureanism.md) (307 BCE – 200) : Not multiplying pleasures but defusing the fears that spoil life: happiness as lasting [tranquillity](../glossary/ataraxia.md).
- [Existentialism](existentialism.md) (1843 – 1980) : Starting from lived existence, always in a situation, never finished: a finite [freedom](../axes/freedom.md) shaping itself through its choices, with no instructions provided.
- [German Idealism](german-idealism.md) (1781 – 1831) : Carrying on the work [Kant](../philosophers/kant.md) began, beyond the limits he drew: no longer opposing mind and world, but thinking freedom, nature and history together.
- [Liberalism](liberalism.md) (1689 – ?) : The [individual](../axes/individual-society.md) and their liberties first: power is legitimate only when limited, grounded in consent and bounded by rights.
- [Marxism](marxism.md) (1848 – ?) : Understanding capitalism in order to break free of it: linking the production of wealth to the class conflicts running through society.
- [Phenomenology](phenomenology.md) (1900 – ?) : Returning "to the things themselves": describing lived experience as it gives itself to consciousness.
- [Platonism](platonism.md) (387 BCE – 529) : What changes does not exhaust the real: seeking the [stable forms](../glossary/theory-of-forms.md) that make things intelligible, and that orient thought toward the Good.
- [Pragmatism](pragmatism.md) (1870 – ?) : An idea is worth the practical difference it makes: the true is what gets verified in inquiry.
- [Rationalism](rationalism.md) (1637 – 1716) : Experience informs, but cannot ground necessity: seeking in [reason](../axes/knowledge-source.md) the principles the senses alone cannot guarantee.
- [Romanticism](romanticism.md) (1795 – 1850) : Defending what calculation cannot exhaust: inner experience, creative imagination and a living nature, against a world reduced to mechanism.
- [Skepticism](skepticism.md) (365 BCE – 200) : Suspending judgment before the equal force of opposing arguments, to find [tranquillity](../glossary/ataraxia.md).
- [Stoicism](stoicism.md) (300 BCE – 180) : A discipline of judgment first of all: directing effort at [what is up to us](../glossary/dichotomy-of-control.md), without demanding that the world obey.
- [Taoism](taoism.md) (400 BCE – ?) : What if we acted better by ceasing to force? Attuning to the [dao 道 (the Way)](../glossary/dao.md) rather than imposing our will on the course of things.
- [The Enlightenment](enlightenment.md) (1685 – 1804) : Dare to use your own understanding: through reason and [enlightenment](../glossary/lumieres.md), humanity can free itself from the tutelage of authority, from superstition and from arbitrary power.
- [Transhumanism](transhumanism.md) (1990 – ?) : Extending, augmenting, even abolishing the human condition through technology: aging, disease and death as problems to be solved.
- [Utilitarianism](utilitarianism.md) (1789 – ?) : Can the good be measured? Judging acts and institutions by their consequences for everyone's well-being, privileging no one.
- [Vedānta (Advaita)](vedanta.md) (700 – ?) : Does the self-evidence of a separate self survive scrutiny? Recognizing the [Self (ātman)](../glossary/atman.md) as not distinct from the [absolute (brahman)](../glossary/brahman.md): a knowledge that liberates.
