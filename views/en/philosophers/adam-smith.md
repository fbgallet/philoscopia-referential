# Adam Smith

*1723 – 1790 · Scottish · [The Enlightenment](../movements/enlightenment.md), [Liberalism](../movements/liberalism.md)*

Scottish philosopher and economist of the [Enlightenment](../movements/enlightenment.md), author of The Theory of Moral Sentiments and The Wealth of Nations.

## Influenced by

- **[Hume](../philosophers/hume.md)** (continues) : A close friend of [Hume](../philosophers/hume.md), [Smith](../philosophers/adam-smith.md) extends his morality of sentiments: sympathy is the spring of moral judgment. He recasts it, however, around the impartial spectator, an inner tribunal that corrects our partialities.
- **[Stoicism](../movements/stoicism.md)** (continues) : The Theory of Moral Sentiments makes self-command, [Adam Smith](../philosophers/adam-smith.md)'s name for self-mastery, the virtue that crowns the others, and its impartial spectator takes up the [Stoic](../movements/stoicism.md) movement of [oikeiōsis](../glossary/oikeiosis.md), the widening of self-concern to embrace others. Smith devotes long pages to the Stoics, while judging their apathy too severe for the sympathy that grounds his own morality.
