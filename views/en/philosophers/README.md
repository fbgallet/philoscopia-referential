# Philosophers

## Full profiles (42)

- [Aristotle](aristotle.md) (384 BCE – 322 BCE) : From animals to constitutions, observing, distinguishing and classifying: seeking, on a human scale, what makes a flourishing life.
- [Bacon](bacon.md) (1561 – 1626) : Flushing out the idols that distort the mind and questioning nature through experiment: knowledge as a collective work in the service of the human condition.
- [Beauvoir](beauvoir.md) (1908 – 1986) : Philosophizing from lived experience: womanhood, old age, everything once held to be natural destiny, rethought as a socially shaped situation, and therefore open to change.
- [Bentham](bentham.md) (1748 – 1832) : Judging every institution by its effects: counting the pleasures and pains a law actually produces, rather than appealing to tradition or supposed natural rights.
- [Camus](camus.md) (1913 – 1960) : Refusing consolations and despair alike: facing a world that guarantees no meaning, seeking a lucid, [rebellious](../glossary/revolt.md) way to live.
- [Charles Sanders Peirce](peirce.md) (1839 – 1914) : Asking of every idea what difference it makes in experience: the founder of [pragmatism](../movements/pragmatism.md) makes thought a collective, corrigible inquiry, oriented toward an agreement to come.
- [Confucius](confucius.md) (551 BCE – 479 BCE) : No one becomes human alone: humaneness (ren 仁) is cultivated through study, the rites and benevolence, as jade is polished.
- [Descartes](descartes.md) (1596 – 1650) : Making doubt a method rather than a defeat: searching beneath everything we think we know for one firm point on which to rebuild.
- [Diogenes of Sinope](diogenes.md) (412 BCE – 323 BCE) : Philosophy in deeds rather than doctrines: using provocation and destitution to lay bare our manufactured needs and the weight of convention.
- [Epictetus](epictetus.md) (50 – 135) : Slave turned teacher: inner freedom through [what is up to us](../glossary/dichotomy-of-control.md).
- [Epicurus](epicurus.md) (341 BCE – 270 BCE) : Philosophy as a remedy for the fear of the gods, of death and of want: true [pleasure](../glossary/hedonism.md) is not debauchery but a life [free of pain and trouble](../glossary/ataraxia.md).
- [George Berkeley](berkeley.md) (1685 – 1753) : Refusing the invisible material support supposed to lie behind things: nothing but minds and their ideas, and God to keep the world from vanishing when we stop looking at it.
- [Hegel](hegel.md) (1770 – 1831) : Thinking the real as a rational process that advances through its own contradictions: nothing has truth in isolation, all is understood only within the whole, and spirit grasps itself only after the fact.
- [Hume](hume.md) (1711 – 1776) : The most serene of [skeptics](../glossary/skepticism.md): putting human nature through the sieve of experience, finding habit beneath our surest beliefs and feeling beneath our moral judgments.
- [Husserl](husserl.md) (1859 – 1938) : Describing before explaining: [provisionally bracketing](../glossary/epoche.md) the world we take for granted, to grasp as closely as possible how it appears to consciousness.
- [Kant](kant.md) (1724 – 1804) : Setting reason to examine its own powers: distinguishing what it can know from what it can only think, sacrificing neither science nor freedom.
- [Laozi](laozi.md) (571 BCE – 471 BCE) : [Non-action](../glossary/wu-wei.md): water, which never struggles, wears down the rock.
- [Leibniz](leibniz.md) (1646 – 1716) : Seeking a reason for all that is: even asking why there is something rather than nothing, and why this world among all possible worlds.
- [Locke](locke.md) (1632 – 1704) : Where do our ideas come from, and where does authority come from? Knowledge must trace back to experience, power to the consent of the governed.
- [Lucretius](lucretius.md) (99 BCE – 55 BCE) : Physics as a poem: singing of atoms, the void and the whole of nature, to free us from the fear of the gods and of death.
- [Machiavelli](machiavelli.md) (1469 – 1527) : Wresting politics from morality to look it in the face: by what real springs, up to cunning and force, power is seized, founded and kept.
- [Marcus Aurelius](marcus-aurelius.md) (121 – 180) : The emperor who wrote to himself: at the heart of power, training each day to remain just and serene, attuned to the [reason](../glossary/logos.md) of the world.
- [Marx](marx.md) (1818 – 1883) : Reading ideas through the labour, property and social conflicts that shape them, and making thought a tool for intervening in history.
- [Michel de Montaigne](montaigne.md) (1533 – 1592) : "“What do I know?”": a lived, systemless [skepticism](../glossary/skepticism.md) that takes the self for its matter.
- [Mill](mill.md) (1806 – 1873) : How far may society constrain an individual who harms no one? A [utilitarian](../movements/utilitarianism.md) in love with liberty, for whom the common happiness feeds on singular freedoms rather than crushing them.
- [Nāgārjuna](nagarjuna.md) (150 – 250) : The dialectician who refuses to turn [emptiness](../glossary/sunyata.md) into a doctrine: testing every claim that a thing [exists by itself](../glossary/svabhava.md), and showing that it holds only through dependence.
- [Nietzsche](nietzsche.md) (1844 – 1900) : Where do our values come from, and what are they worth? Philosophizing as a physician of culture: sounding out our moralities to tell what affirms life from what denies it.
- [Plato](plato.md) (428 BCE – 348 BCE) : Writing in dialogues to put our opinions to the test: a movement of ascent from the world that changes toward what does not change, up to the Good.
- [Plotinus](plotinus.md) (204 – 270) : All things flow from the [One](../glossary/the-one.md) beyond being: to philosophize is to ascend back to it, "the flight of the alone to the Alone".
- [Pyrrho](pyrrho.md) (360 BCE – 270 BCE) : Nothing is this any more than that: [decide nothing](../glossary/epoche.md), and the soul rests at peace.
- [Rousseau](rousseau.md) (1712 – 1778) : A man of the [Enlightenment](../movements/enlightenment.md) at odds with it: asking what society has made of man, and on what terms a political order might set him free rather than subject him.
- [Śaṅkara](shankara.md) (700 – 750) : "“Thou art that”": the self and the absolute are one, and to know it liberates.
- [Sartre](sartre.md) (1905 – 1980) : Tracking freedom in the most concrete of things, a gaze, a moment of shame, an act of [bad faith](../glossary/bad-faith.md): with no ready-made human nature, existing remains a responsibility without excuse.
- [Schopenhauer](schopenhauer.md) (1788 – 1860) : Taking suffering seriously rather than justifying it: against the blind will-to-live that drives us, art grants truces, compassion grounds morality, renunciation alone delivers.
- [Seneca](seneca.md) (4 BCE – 65) : A philosopher by letters, close to power: turning each day's trial, disgrace, exile, death itself, into an exercise of virtue.
- [Sextus Empiricus](sextus-empiricus.md) (160 – 210) : To every thesis, an opposing one of equal strength: wisdom [suspends judgment](../glossary/epoche.md).
- [Socrates](socrates.md) (470 BCE – 399 BCE) : The philosopher who wrote nothing: to question relentlessly, hold virtue to be knowledge, and be wise only in knowing oneself ignorant.
- [Spinoza](spinoza.md) (1632 – 1677) : Understanding the passions through their causes rather than judging them: suffering less, acting more, and making of that lucidity a joy.
- [The Buddha](buddha.md) (563 BCE – 483 BCE) : A diagnosis, not a pessimism: [unsatisfactoriness (dukkha)](../glossary/dukkha.md) arises from [craving (taṇhā)](../glossary/tanha.md) and the illusion of a self, and there is a path to extinguish it.
- [Thomas Aquinas](aquinas.md) (1225 – 1274) : Building summas like cathedrals: joining Christian theology to the resources of [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md), convinced that the truths of reason and the truths of faith cannot contradict each other.
- [Wittgenstein](wittgenstein.md) (1889 – 1951) : Twice he thought he had put an end to philosophy: not a doctrine but an activity, which does not solve problems but dissolves them, by showing that they are born of the traps of our language.
- [Zhuangzi](zhuangzi.md) (369 BCE – 286 BCE) : Am I a man who dreamed he was a butterfly, or a butterfly dreaming he is a man?
