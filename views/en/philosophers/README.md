- [Diogenes of Sinope](diogenes.md) (412 BCE – 323 BCE) : "A citizen of the world", free through [self-sufficiency](../glossary/autarkeia.md): whatever you own ends up owning you.

- [George Berkeley](berkeley.md) (1685 – 1753) : “To be is to be perceived”: there is no matter, only minds and their ideas, held in order by God.
- [Hume](hume.md) (1711 – 1776) : “Reason is, and ought only to be, the slave of the passions”: a radical [empiricism](../movements/empiricism.md) dissolving the self, causation and certainty.
- [Husserl](husserl.md) (1859 – 1938) : "To the things themselves!": describing the consciousness that always intends something.
- [Kant](kant.md) (1724 – 1804) : Critical philosophy: bounding knowledge to phenomena to save both science and freedom.
- [Laozi](laozi.md) (571 BCE – 471 BCE) : [Non-action](../glossary/wu-wei.md): water, which never struggles, wears down the rock.
- [Leibniz](leibniz.md) (1646 – 1716) : We live in “the best of all possible worlds”: nothing happens without a sufficient reason.
- [Locke](locke.md) (1632 – 1704) : The mind is first a [blank slate](../glossary/tabula-rasa.md); power, a revocable trust of the people.
- [Lucretius](lucretius.md) (99 BCE – 55 BCE) : “So much wrong could religion prompt”: knowledge of nature, and it alone, frees us from fear.
- [Marcus Aurelius](marcus-aurelius.md) (121 – 180) : Emperor and philosopher: at the heart of power, staying just and serene, in tune with the [reason](../glossary/logos.md) of the world.
- [Marx](marx.md) (1818 – 1883) : “The philosophers have only interpreted the world; the point is to change it.”
- [Michel de Montaigne](montaigne.md) (1533 – 1592) : "“What do I know?”": a lived, systemless [skepticism](../glossary/skepticism.md) that takes the self for its matter.
- [Mill](mill.md) (1806 – 1873) : [The greatest happiness of the greatest number](../glossary/greatest-happiness-principle.md), and over himself the individual remains sovereign.
- [Nāgārjuna](nagarjuna.md) (150 – 250) : Everything is [empty (śūnyatā, emptiness)](../glossary/sunyata.md) of [inherent existence (svabhāva)](../glossary/svabhava.md): [dependent origination](../glossary/pratityasamutpada.md) stands in place of essence.
- [Nietzsche](nietzsche.md) (1844 – 1900) : *[Beyond good and evil](../works/beyond-good-and-evil-nietzsche.md)*: the [will to power](../glossary/will-to-power.md) against the morality of [resentment](../glossary/ressentiment.md).
- [Plato](plato.md) (428 BCE – 348 BCE) : The sensible world is but a shadow: the real and the good are the [Forms](../glossary/theory-of-forms.md).
- [Plotinus](plotinus.md) (204 – 270) : All things flow from the [One](../glossary/the-one.md) beyond being: to philosophize is to ascend back to it, "the flight of the alone to the Alone".
- [Pyrrho](pyrrho.md) (360 BCE – 270 BCE) : Nothing is this any more than that: [decide nothing](../glossary/epoche.md), and the soul rests at peace.
- [Rousseau](rousseau.md) (1712 – 1778)
- [Śaṅkara](shankara.md) (700 – 750) : "“Thou art that”": the self and the absolute are one, and to know it liberates.
- [Sartre](sartre.md) (1905 – 1980) : Existence precedes essence: man is nothing but what he makes of himself.
- [Seneca](seneca.md) (4 BCE – 65) : Counsellor of the powerful and master of wisdom: turning every hardship into an exercise of virtue.
- [Sextus Empiricus](sextus-empiricus.md) (160 – 210) : To every thesis, an opposing one of equal strength: wisdom [suspends judgment](../glossary/epoche.md).
- [Socrates](socrates.md) (470 BCE – 399 BCE) : The philosopher who wrote nothing: to question relentlessly, hold virtue to be knowledge, and be wise only in knowing oneself ignorant.
- [Spinoza](spinoza.md) (1632 – 1677) : [God is Nature](../glossary/immanence.md): knowing necessity as the path to blessedness.
- [The Buddha](buddha.md) (563 BCE – 483 BCE) : All is [suffering (dukkha)](../glossary/dukkha.md): extinguish [craving (taṇhā)](../glossary/tanha.md) and the illusion of a self to be free.
- [Thomas Aquinas](aquinas.md) (1225 – 1274) : [Grace](../glossary/grace.md) does not destroy nature but perfects it: [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md) placed at the service of Christian faith.
- [Zhuangzi](zhuangzi.md) (369 BCE – 286 BCE) : Am I a man who dreamed he was a butterfly, or a butterfly dreaming he is a man?
