# Heidegger

*1889 – 1976 · German · [Phenomenology](../movements/phenomenology.md)*

German philosopher of being: he conceives human existence as a being-toward-death and sees in modern technology an enframing of the world that veils its meaning.

## Influenced by

- **[Husserl](../philosophers/husserl.md)** (reinterprets) : Husserl's assistant and then successor, [Heidegger](../philosophers/heidegger.md) shifts [phenomenology](../movements/phenomenology.md) from transcendental consciousness toward the question of being: no longer describing lived experiences, but existence as being-in-the-world.
- **[Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md)** (reinterprets) : [Heidegger](../philosophers/heidegger.md) devotes extensive lecture courses to [Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md), whom he reads as the last great metaphysician: the will to power and nihilism become the completion and the destiny of Western metaphysics.
- **[Romanticism](../movements/romanticism.md)** (reinterprets) : [Heidegger](../philosophers/heidegger.md) takes up from Romanticism its critique of the world-as-machine and of calculative reason, its listening to nature and its privileging of the poetic as access to being. His reading of Hölderlin, the Romantic poet held to voice the « default of the gods » and the danger of technology, lies at the heart of his late thought: « to dwell poetically » extends the Romantic dream of a re-enchanted world.

## Influence on

- **[Merleau-Ponty](../philosophers/merleau-ponty.md)** (reinterprets) : [Merleau-Ponty](../philosophers/merleau-ponty.md) takes up [Heidegger](../philosophers/heidegger.md)'s being-in-the-world and the pre-theoretical world, but redirects them to the perceiving body: it is the flesh, not the analytic of being, that originally opens the world.
- **[Sartre](../philosophers/sartre.md)** (reinterprets) : [Sartre](../philosophers/sartre.md) existentialises [Heidegger](../philosophers/heidegger.md)'s analyses, being-in-the-world, facticity, anxiety, within a framework centred on freedom and project, which Heidegger would reject as a humanist misreading.
- **[Jonas](../philosophers/jonas.md)** (reacts against) : A pupil of [Heidegger](../philosophers/heidegger.md), [Jonas](../philosophers/jonas.md) breaks with his existentialism, indifferent to nature, to found an ontology of the living and an ethics of responsibility toward future generations.
- **[Emmanuel Levinas](../philosophers/levinas.md)** (reacts against) : [Levinas](../philosophers/levinas.md) builds his thought in close contestation of [Heidegger](../philosophers/heidegger.md): against the primacy of ontology, he posits ethics as first philosophy, the face of the other preceding any understanding of being.
