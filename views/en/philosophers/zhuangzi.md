# Zhuang Zhou 莊周, called Zhuangzi ("Master Zhuang")

*369 BCE – 286 BCE · Chinese · [Taoism](../movements/taoism.md)*

A Chinese [Taoist](../movements/taoism.md) thinker of the Warring States period, traditionally placed in the fourth century BCE and presented as a minor official who is said to have refused a ministerial post to keep his freedom. He gives his name to the *[Zhuangzi](../works/zhuangzi-zhuangzi.md)*, a composite collection whose seven "Inner Chapters" are ascribed to him: a fabric of parables, dialogues and logical provocations in which humor does the work of argument. Where [Laozi](../philosophers/laozi.md) states the [Dao](../glossary/dao.md) in cosmic and political aphorisms, Zhuangzi radicalizes it on the terrain of singular existence and of knowledge: he mounts a critique of distinctions, of language and of usefulness whose emblems are the butterfly dream, Cook Ding and the happiness of fish. Reacting against [Confucian](../philosophers/confucius.md) moralism and against the logician [Hui Shi](../philosophers/hui-shi.md), his favorite foil, his thought shaped Chan (Zen) Buddhism and inspired a whole tradition of poetic and pictorial spontaneity.

## Core positions

1. [Status of norms (metaethics)](../axes/moral-scope.md) : **True and false, beautiful and ugly exist only from a standpoint: "equalize" the perspectives instead of taking yours for the measure of all.**
2. [Relation to certainty](../axes/certainty.md) : **How do I know I am not dreaming at this very moment? The knowing that claims to settle true from false is an illusion to be loosened from in order to breathe.**
3. [Death](../axes/death.md) : **Life and death follow each other like day and night: who knows whether the dead one does not rejoice like an exile come home at last?**
4. [Scope of action](../axes/control-scope.md) : **Like the butcher whose blade follows the openings and never dulls, do not act by forcing: [slip where the real yields](../glossary/wu-wei.md).**
5. [Humanity's place in nature](../axes/human-place-nature.md) : **Am I a man who dreamed the butterfly, or the butterfly dreaming me? The human is not the measure of the living, but one mode among others of [transformation](../glossary/hua.md).**

## Positions

### [Relation to certainty](../axes/certainty.md)

*positioned · explicit in the texts · major*

Skepticism 80% · Dogmatism 20%

Every judgment is made from a standpoint, and each standpoint has its "this" and its "that": what one party holds true the other holds false, and no neutral tribunal can settle them without itself being a party. Zhuangzi draws from this a scepticism about discursive knowledge: the controversies of the [Confucians](../philosophers/confucius.md) and the Mohists cancel out, dream cannot be securely told from waking, and one does not even know whether one knows anything. Yet this doubt is not the Greek suspension aimed at [tranquillity](../glossary/ataraxia.md): it is therapeutic and liberating, dissolving the anxiety of having to be right so as to leave room for a supple knowing attuned to the course of things.

> “How do I know that what I call knowing is not really ignorance, and what I call ignorance not really knowing?”, ch. 2 (On the Equality of Things)

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 2 (On the Equality of Things) · Zhuangzi, ch. 2 (the butterfly dream)*

### [Status of norms (metaethics)](../axes/moral-scope.md)

*positioned · explicit in the texts · major*

Relativism 90% · Universalism 10%

Beautiful and ugly, permitted and forbidden, great and small are not inscribed in things: they are correlative carvings, relative to the scale and the use of whoever judges. An oriole finds delicious what disgusts a human; a beautiful woman makes fish flee: where is the "correct" taste? Zhuangzi generalizes this relativity of norms against the [Confucian](../philosophers/confucius.md) claim to fix the right and the ritual. Unlike [Laozi](../philosophers/laozi.md), who dissolves human distinctions only to replace them with the single norm of the [natural](../glossary/ziran.md), Zhuangzi rebuilds no positive norm: he invites us to "equalize" viewpoints and to move freely among them, which brings him close, without that modern vocabulary, to a relativism of norms.

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 2 (On the Equality of Things)*

### [Death](../axes/death.md)

*positioned · explicit in the texts · major*

Death is nothing 55% · Immortalism 45%

At his wife's death Zhuangzi sings, drumming on a basin: to weep would be to ignore the course of things. Death is neither a nothingness to fear nor a passage toward an immortal soul, but a moment of ceaseless [transformation](../glossary/hua.md) (hua): the vital breaths knot into life as the seasons follow one another, then unknot and reform elsewhere. Who knows whether the dead one is not rejoicing, like an exile come home at last? This serenity through insertion into the great passage of forms meets the Epicurean appeasement, yet it is laced with the idea that living matter endlessly recomposes itself, which is neither quite an extinction nor a personal immortality.

> “Life and death follow each other like day and night. Why should I grieve over it?”, ch. 18 (Perfect Happiness)

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 18 (Perfect Happiness) · Zhuangzi, ch. 6 (The Great Source as Teacher)*

### [Scope of action](../axes/control-scope.md)

*positioned · explicit in the texts · major*

What is up to us 90% · Prometheanism 10%

Cook Ding carves an ox without ever dulling his blade: he does not cut, he follows the openings the carcass offers, and the knife moves through the void. The parable gives [effortless action](../glossary/wu-wei.md) its most concrete sense: not inaction, but action so wedded to the real that it ceases to be effort. Against the voluntarism that bends things to a design, efficacy comes to one who imposes nothing and slips where the situation yields, like the swimmer of the falls who "goes in with the swirl and comes out with the eddy." Where [Laozi](../philosophers/laozi.md) draws non-action toward the art of governing, Zhuangzi unfolds it as a lived, almost bodily mastery of the right gesture.

> “I no longer look with my eyes but with my spirit, and the blade follows of itself the natural openings.”, ch. 3 (The Secret of Caring for Life)

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 3 (Cook Ding) · Zhuangzi, ch. 19 (the swimmer of the falls)*

### [The highest good](../axes/sovereign-good.md)

*positioned · inferable · major*

Eudaimonism 55% · Hedonism 45%

Zhuangzi's sovereign good is ["free and easy wandering"](../glossary/xiaoyao-you.md) (xiaoyao you): a life loosened from honors, offices and rankings, that delights in whatever comes and plays with the world's metamorphoses. Refusing the court that offers him a ministry, choosing to stay "a turtle dragging its tail in the mud," he aims neither at accumulated pleasure nor at the discharge of a function, but at a joy without object, the ease of one who clings to nothing. This felicity is not the Greek [eudaimonism](../glossary/eudaimonism.md) of a life made good by perfecting a nature, yet it shares the idea of a flourishing, tinged with a keen taste for the jubilation of the moment.

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 1 (Free and Easy Wandering) · Zhuangzi, ch. 17 (the turtle in the mud)*

### [Source of knowledge](../axes/knowledge-source.md)

*positioned · inferable · minor*

Empiricism 70% · Rationalism 30%

The knowledge that matters is not the discursive knowing that defines and argues, but an embodied skill acquired through repeated dealings with things: the butcher, the wheelwright, the swimmer could not explain their art, and yet they possess it to their fingertips. This primacy of practical experience and direct contact over abstract reasoning leans to the empirical side, but it is an empiricism of the gesture rather than of the senses, with no theory of perception as a source of true propositions.

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 3 (Cook Ding) · Zhuangzi, ch. 13 (Wheelwright Bian)*

### [Moral circle](../axes/moral-circle.md)

*positioned · inferable · minor*

Ecocentrism 50% · Biocentrism 35% · Pathocentrism 15%

To set the human at the summit of a ladder of beings is for Zhuangzi an illusion of perspective: from the standpoint of the [Dao](../glossary/dao.md), the pillar and the stalk, the ugly and the beautiful, the human and the worm are equally modes of transformation, and none holds a privilege. This anthropological decentering strips the human of being the measure of all things and inscribes him as one being among myriads, brother to the butterfly he may have dreamed he was. The leaning is decidedly ecocentric, even though Zhuangzi does not formulate, as a modern ecologist would, a duty to protect a whole.

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 2 (On the Equality of Things) · Zhuangzi, ch. 17 (Autumn Floods)*

### [Humanity's place in nature](../axes/human-place-nature.md)

*positioned · explicit in the texts · major*

Member among others 85% · Stewardship 15%

The human is neither to master nature nor to manage it as a steward: he must "let things be what they are" and tune his acting to theirs. The happiness of the fish, which Zhuangzi claims to know by watching them swim, and his sparring with [Hui Shi](../philosophers/hui-shi.md) over it, bespeak a lived continuity between the human and the living that makes any vantage of oversight idle. To follow the natural is neither to exploit nor to protect, but to know oneself a member of the same flux, exactly the reverse of Prometheanism.

> “See how the fish come out and dart about at their ease: that is the happiness of fish!”, ch. 17 (Autumn Floods)

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 17 (the happiness of fish) · Zhuangzi, ch. 7 (Fit for Emperors and Kings)*

### [Relation to the past](../axes/past-relation.md)

*positioned · explicit in the texts · minor*

Amor fati 85% · Revolt 15%

Whatever comes, disgrace as much as deformity, sickness as much as death, is welcomed as a deal of transformation to be embraced without revolt: a character who grows a hump examines it laughing, ready to see his arm turned into a rooster or his hip into a wheel. This acquiescence to one's lot meets the Stoic amor fati, but without the spring of a rational [providence](../glossary/providence.md): one consents not to a willed order but lets oneself be carried by a course that has neither design nor cruelty.

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 6 (The Great Source as Teacher)*

### [Relation to desire](../axes/desire.md)

*positioned · inferable · minor*

Extinction and detachment 40% · Affirmation 30% · Reasoned selection 30%

Zhuangzi does not preach the extinction of desire as a discipline, but the lightening of the craving soul that clutches at gains, ranks and duration. The sage ["fasts the heart"](../glossary/xinzhai.md) (xinzhai), empties himself of his preferences so as to espouse whatever comes, without however mortifying the body or condemning the simple joys he gladly celebrates. The leaning is toward detachment, but a playful detachment that lets desires pass like clouds rather than tearing them out, which sets him apart from Stoic asceticism as much as from the affirmation of desire.

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 4 (the fasting of the heart) · Zhuangzi, ch. 6 (The Great Source as Teacher)*

### [Essence and existence](../axes/essence.md)

*positioned · inferable · minor*

Essentialism 75% · Existentialism 25%

Each being has a "way of being so of itself," the [ziran](../glossary/ziran.md) that is to be let unfold: the sea bird dies if treated like a man, and one cripples the duck by trying to lengthen its legs. To live well is therefore not to create oneself freely but to espouse one's own native cast. Yet this nature is no fixed essence defined once and for all: it is fluid, open to metamorphoses, which holds Zhuangzi back from a clear-cut essentialism and places him more moderately on the side of the given against self-invention.

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 18 (the sea bird) · Zhuangzi, ch. 8 (the duck's legs)*

### [Nature of the self](../axes/self-nature.md)

*positioned · inferable · minor*

Bundle theory 100%

The butterfly dream undoes the assurance of a stable self: on waking, Zhuangzi no longer knows whether he is the man who dreamed the butterfly or the butterfly dreaming the man. The "self" is not a substance enduring beneath change, but a provisional knot in the flux of transformations, a boundary that one must "forget" (wuwo) so as to merge into the course of things. This dissolution of the substantial self, close to a bundle of states without a bearer, echoes Buddhist [anatta](../glossary/anatta.md) even before any contact between the two traditions.

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 2 (the butterfly dream) · Zhuangzi, ch. 6 (sitting in forgetfulness)*

### [Where the sacred resides](../axes/transcendence.md)

*positioned · inferable · minor*

Immanence 88% · Transcendence 13%

The [Dao](../glossary/dao.md) is neither a god nor a world-behind: it is everywhere, in the humblest things, even, Zhuangzi says to provoke, "in the piss and the dung." The sacred does not reside above the world but coincides with the immanent course of all transformations, accessible to whoever attunes to it rather than to whoever worships it. This radical immanence leaves only a thin residue of the inaccessible, the obscurity of the Dao before distinctions, which holds the score back from the utmost extreme.

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 22 (the Dao in the piss and dung) · Zhuangzi, ch. 6 (The Great Source as Teacher)*

### [Interiority](../axes/interiority.md)

*positioned · inferable · minor*

The examined life 75% · Primacy of action 25%

Wisdom is won by a turn inward, the ["fasting of the heart"](../glossary/xinzhai.md) and the "sitting in forgetfulness" that empty the mind of its calculations and preferences so as to make it clear as a mirror. The sage "uses his heart like a mirror": he receives without retaining, welcomes without pursuing. This contemplative quietude takes precedence over active engagement in public affairs, which Zhuangzi shuns; yet it is prolonged in an effortless action, so that the inward withdrawal is not pure inaction.

> “The perfect sage uses his heart like a mirror: he goes out to meet nothing and welcomes nothing, he responds and does not retain.”, ch. 7

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 4 (the fasting of the heart) · Zhuangzi, ch. 7 (the mirror-heart)*

### [Status of emotions](../axes/emotions.md)

*positioned · inferable · minor*

Intellectualism 70% · Sentimentalism 30%

The sage is not unfeeling but does not let himself be "wounded within" by his affects: emotions pass through him like the seasons, with no clinging and no feeding of them. Disturbance arises from the judgment that posits an event as a loss, and it dissolves when one stops sorting the real into gains and losses, as Zhuangzi stops weeping for his wife. The leaning is toward mastery of the passions, but by dissolving distinctions rather than by a rational government in the Stoic manner, which keeps it short of a clear-cut intellectualism.

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 5 (not to be wounded within) · Zhuangzi, ch. 18 (Perfect Happiness)*

### [Letter or spirit of the rule](../axes/rule-spirit.md)

*positioned · inferable · minor*

Equity-phronesis 90% · Legalism 10%

No rule can anticipate the singularity of each situation: Wheelwright Bian cannot pass on his knack through precepts, and Cook Ding follows no method but the very contours of the real. Right action is a living, formulaless adjustment, lost the moment one fixes it into a code. Zhuangzi thus stands resolutely on the side of the spirit against the letter, and more than that: he refuses the very idea that rightness could ever be put into letter.

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 13 (Wheelwright Bian) · Zhuangzi, ch. 3 (Cook Ding)*

### [Criterion of right action](../axes/ethics-foundation.md)

*positioned · inferable · minor*

Virtue ethics 100%

Zhuangzi mocks the "benevolence" and "righteousness" of the [Confucians](../philosophers/confucius.md) as paraded virtues that maim the natural, like the duck whose legs are stretched. Good conduct is neither obedience to a rule nor a reckoning of consequences, but the expression of an inner state attuned to the [Dao](../glossary/dao.md), a spontaneous excellence (de) that does not know itself virtuous. For want of any alternative, it is a virtue ethics, but of a virtue that denies itself as a code and holds every proclaimed morality to be a falling-off.

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 8 and 9*

### [The meaning of words](../axes/meaning.md)

*positioned · inferable · minor*

Use theory 70% · Speaker's intention 30%

Words do not picture things: they serve to catch them, as the fish trap serves to catch the fish, and "the fish caught, one forgets the trap." Their meaning lies in their use and in what they let one do, not in a fixed correspondence with a referent, so that the logicians' disputes about names spin idly. This instrumental, pragmatic conception of language, in which one may "forget the words" once their office is done, leans toward a use theory with a tint of intention.

> “The trap is for catching fish: once the fish is caught, the trap is forgotten. Words are for grasping the idea: once the idea is grasped, the words are forgotten.”, ch. 26

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 26 (the trap and the fish) · Zhuangzi, ch. 2*

### [The meaning of suffering](../axes/suffering-meaning.md)

*positioned · inferable · minor*

Abolitionism 65% · Fecundity and redemption 35%

Suffering is neither an evil to be abolished nor a redemptive trial that would forge character: it is a moment of the flux to be welcomed without dramatizing it, like the sickness that may turn the arm into a rooster. Zhuangzi lends it no moral fecundity in the manner of Pascal or Nietzsche, but neither does he campaign for its abolition: he dissolves its weight by ceasing to judge it a misfortune. The position leans slightly toward the median indifference, through release from the judgment that constitutes the hurt.

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 6 (The Great Source as Teacher)*

### [Relation to technology](../axes/technology.md)

*positioned · inferable · minor*

Techno-criticism 80% · Techno-optimism 20%

An old gardener refuses the water-drawing machine he is offered: "whoever uses machines acquires a machine heart," and loses the native purity of the spirit. Zhuangzi's distrust bears not on the tool itself, which he gladly wields in his artisan parables, but on the calculating mind and the cunning the instrument breeds at the expense of the [natural](../glossary/ziran.md). This critique of the "machine heart" ranks him, like [Laozi](../philosophers/laozi.md), among the distant ancestors of techno-criticism, but from the inner self rather than the political order.

> “Whoever uses machines does all his work mechanically; whoever does his work mechanically grows a machine heart.”, ch. 12

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 12 (the gardener and the machine)*

### [Beauty in life](../axes/aesthetic-life.md)

*positioned · extrapolated · minor*

Aestheticism 70% · Primacy of the ethical 30%

Zhuangzi's ideal is not moral duty but a light, played existence whose grace is akin to the artist's: the butcher whose blade dances "to the rhythm of the Mulberry Forest," the painter who strips naked to paint at his ease. The successful life is less a matter of rightness than of ease and beauty of gesture, which makes it an aestheticization of existence, even though the word art is anachronistic here and the aim remains a liberation rather than a cult of the beautiful.

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 3 (Cook Ding) · Zhuangzi, ch. 21 (the naked painter)*

### [Temporal orientation](../axes/time-orientation.md)

*positioned · inferable · minor*

Presentism 80% · Memory and transmission 20%

The sage dwells in what arises at the instant and "travels in the single breath" of the present, without projecting himself into designs for the future, which belong to the forced action he rejects. Unlike [Laozi](../philosophers/laozi.md), more turned toward restoring an original state, Zhuangzi stresses the mobility of the flux and adherence to the metamorphosis under way: a thin ground of return to the [Dao](../glossary/dao.md) before distinctions remains, but the accent falls on the espousal of the present.

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 6 (The Great Source as Teacher) · Zhuangzi, ch. 2*

### [Vision of humanity](../axes/human-vision.md)

*positioned · inferable · minor*

Anthropological optimism 73% · Anthropological pessimism 28%

Evil lies not in a corrupt human nature but in what denatures it: affected knowledge, ambition, hierarchies and moral codes that turn each one away from his native cast. Left to his [naturalness](../glossary/ziran.md), the human is neither good nor wicked in the moral sense, but spontaneously attuned to the course of things; it is civilization that tears him apart. This trust in the pre-cultural natural sets Zhuangzi apart from anthropological pessimism, without making him the theorist of an original goodness as sharply drawn as in Rousseau.

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 9 (Horses' Hooves)*

### [Tonality of reality](../axes/cosmic-outlook.md)

*positioned · inferable · minor*

Metaphysical optimism 70% · Tragic thought 30%

The world is not ruled by a benevolent providence and has no meaning given in advance: it is an impersonal flux of transformations without design. Yet this course is in no way tragic for Zhuangzi: whoever accords with it finds in it a causeless joy and a freedom of play, for death itself is but a turn of the wheel. The tonality is serene and carefree rather than optimistic or tragic, which leans slightly toward a happy welcome of the real.

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 18 (Perfect Happiness) · Zhuangzi, ch. 6 (The Great Source as Teacher)*

### [Individual and society](../axes/individual-society.md)

*positioned · inferable · minor*

Individualism 80% · Holism 20%

Zhuangzi's wisdom is that of an individual who loosens himself from roles, offices and social recognition to preserve his inner freedom: better a free turtle in the mud than a revered shell in the temple. Society and its institutions, far from constituting the human, deform him by imposing distinctions and ambitions foreign to his nature. Priority clearly goes to the singular individual against the social whole, but through withdrawal and flight rather than a claim of rights.

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 17 (the turtle in the mud) · Zhuangzi, ch. 4 (the useless tree)*

### [Bodily pleasures](../axes/bodily-pleasures.md)

*positioned · inferable · minor*

Asceticism 50% · Celebration 50%

Zhuangzi neither mortifies the body as a prison of the soul nor erects pleasure into an end: he takes it for a moment of the flux to be welcomed with ease, without greed or disgust. "Caring for the vital principle" is neither asceticism nor debauchery but the care not to wear out one's life by exhausting it in desires or fears. The position is properly median, a playful temperance that follows the natural of the body without making it either an enemy or an idol.

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 3 (The Secret of Caring for Life)*

### [Meaning of work](../axes/work-meaning.md)

*positioned · extrapolated · minor*

Fulfillment 65% · Necessity, leisure as the end 35%

The figures of accomplished craftsmen, the butcher, the wheelwright, the bell-stand carver, suggest that well-made work can be a site of flourishing and even of communion with the [Dao](../glossary/dao.md), when the gesture becomes effortless. But this accomplishment owes to recovered spontaneity, not to toil or useful production: the gnarled, "useless" tree is precisely the one that, serving no purpose, lives out its full life. The praise thus wavers between art as a way and the prizing of free leisure, against busy usefulness.

*Sources : Zhuangzi, ch. 3 (Cook Ding) · Zhuangzi, ch. 4 (the useless tree)*

### [Freedom of the will](../axes/freedom.md)

*rejects the alternative*

The opposition between free will and determinism, forged by Greek and then Christian metaphysics of the will, has no equivalent in Zhuangzi: he thinks not a willing-subject asking whether it is free, but a freedom of another order, the "free and easy wandering" of one who dissolves forced willing to espouse the course of transformations. To place him on this axis would betray his point.

### [Can a machine think](../axes/machine-mind.md)

*not applicable*

An anachronistic question: nothing in Zhuangzi's corpus allows positioning him on machine thought without hazardous extrapolation, even though his "machine heart" parable targets the calculating mind, not artificial cognition.

## Influenced by

- **[Hui Shi](../philosophers/hui-shi.md)** (reinterprets) : Friend and chief sparring partner of [Zhuangzi](../philosophers/zhuangzi.md), [Hui Shi](../philosophers/hui-shi.md) supplies, through his paradoxes of the School of Names, the logical material that Zhuangzi turns into a philosophy of the relative and of the equivalence of viewpoints.
- **[Laozi](../philosophers/laozi.md)** (reinterprets) : [Zhuangzi](../philosophers/zhuangzi.md) radicalises the themes associated with [Laozi](../philosophers/laozi.md): the dao, non-action and the relativity of distinctions, which he unfolds through parables and paradoxes rather than maxims.
