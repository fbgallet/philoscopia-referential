# Hobbes

*1588 – 1679 · English*

English political philosopher: starting from a dark view of humanity, he founds the state on a contract by which each person renounces their own violence to escape the war of all against all.

## Influenced by

- **[Aristotelianism](../movements/aristotelianism.md)** (reacts against) : [Hobbes](../philosophers/hobbes.md) builds his mechanism against scholastic [Aristotelianism](../movements/aristotelianism.md), whose “Aristotelity” he mocks: no more essences or final causes, only bodies in motion, right into politics.
- **[Descartes](../philosophers/descartes.md)** (reacts against) : In the Third Objections to the Meditations, [Hobbes](../philosophers/hobbes.md) directly contests [Descartes](../philosophers/descartes.md): the thing that thinks may well be corporeal. On this central point of dualism, the confrontation crystallises his materialism against the Cartesian immaterial soul.
- **[Epicureanism](../movements/epicureanism.md)** (reinterprets) : The materialism of [Epicureanism](../movements/epicureanism.md) reaches [Hobbes](../philosophers/hobbes.md) through a documented but indirect relay, his friend Gassendi, restorer of Epicurus in Mersenne's circle: atoms, fear and covenant there prefigure his physics and his contract.

## Influence on

- **[Locke](../philosophers/locke.md)** (reacts against) : Without ever naming [Hobbes](../philosophers/hobbes.md), [Locke](../philosophers/locke.md) builds his contract against Hobbesian absolutism: the same materials, the state of nature and the covenant, but turned into a limited and revocable power. A filiation reconstructed by historians.
- **[Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md)** (reacts against) : [Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md) reverses [Hobbes](../philosophers/hobbes.md)'s [state of nature](../glossary/etat-de-nature.md): far from a « war of all against all », it is peaceful, and man within it is naturally good, moved by self-love and [pity](../glossary/pitie.md), not by fear and pride. Hobbes, Rousseau charges, projected onto natural man the passions of already-social man; it is not nature but society that makes man wicked.
