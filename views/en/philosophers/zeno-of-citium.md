# Zeno of Citium

*334 BCE – 262 BCE · Greek · [Stoicism](../movements/stoicism.md)*

Founder of [Stoicism](../movements/stoicism.md), who taught under the Painted Porch of Athens an ethics of living according to nature and reason.

## Influenced by

- **[Crates](../philosophers/crates.md)** (continues) : Before founding the Stoa, [Zeno](../philosophers/zeno-of-citium.md) studied under the Cynic [Crates](../philosophers/crates.md): from him he takes the ideal of self-sufficiency and life “according to nature”, an ethical core he would recast into a system.

## Influence on

- **[Chrysippus](../philosophers/chrysippus.md)** (continues) : Passed on through Cleanthes, [Zeno](../philosophers/zeno-of-citium.md)'s doctrine was systematised by Chrysippus across logic, physics and ethics, so thoroughly that antiquity said “without Chrysippus, no Stoa”.
