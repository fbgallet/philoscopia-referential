# Avicenna

*980 – 1037 · Persian · [Aristotelianism](../movements/aristotelianism.md)*

Persian philosopher and physician of the Islamic Golden Age, the foremost interpreter of Aristotle in the Islamic world; his distinction between essence and existence and his proof of the necessary being deeply shaped Latin Scholasticism, Aquinas included.

## Influenced by

- **[Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md)** (reinterprets) : [Avicenna](../philosophers/avicenna.md) recasts [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md)'s metaphysics by Neoplatonising it: the distinction of essence and existence, the necessary being, the hierarchy of intellects.

## Influence on

- **[Thomas Aquinas](../philosophers/aquinas.md)** (reinterprets) : [Aquinas](../philosophers/aquinas.md) takes up from [Avicenna](../philosophers/avicenna.md) the real distinction of essence and existence, but recasts it: where Avicenna treats existence as an accident added to essence, Aquinas thinks it as the act of being ([esse](../glossary/esse.md)) that God confers, thereby correcting his model within his own metaphysics of being.
