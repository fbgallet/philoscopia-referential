# Antonio Gramsci

*1891 – 1937 · Italian · [Marxism](../movements/marxism.md)*

Italian [Marxist](../movements/marxism.md) theorist, known for the concept of cultural hegemony, developed in his Prison Notebooks.

## Influenced by

- **[Machiavelli](../philosophers/machiavelli.md)** (reinterprets) : [Gramsci](../philosophers/gramsci.md) takes up Machiavelli in his Prison Notebooks: the "Modern Prince" is no longer a man but the revolutionary party, a collective prince that, like the Machiavellian myth, crystallizes and organizes the collective will. The founder's virtù becomes hegemony built through organization.
- **[Marx](../philosophers/marx.md)** (reinterprets) : [Gramsci](../philosophers/gramsci.md) rereads [Marx](../philosophers/marx.md) by shifting the analysis from the economic base toward culture: the concept of hegemony explains how a class rules through consent and civil society, not by force alone.
