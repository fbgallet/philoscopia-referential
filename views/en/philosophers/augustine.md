# Augustine

*354 – 430 · Roman African · [Platonism](../movements/platonism.md)*

Church Father and philosopher: he makes free will the source of moral evil and grace the mainspring of salvation, wedding the Platonic heritage to Christianity.

## Influenced by

- **[Plotinus](../philosophers/plotinus.md)** (reinterprets) : [Augustine](../philosophers/augustine.md) receives from the Neoplatonists, [Plotinus](../philosophers/plotinus.md) foremost, a metaphysics of inwardness and of the soul's ascent toward the immaterial, which he reconfigures within Christian theology.

## Influence on

- **[Anselm of Canterbury](../philosophers/anselm.md)** (continues) : [Anselm](../philosophers/anselm.md) extends the Augustinian method of “credo ut intelligam”, faith seeking understanding, all the way to the so-called ontological argument of the Proslogion.
- **[Thomas Aquinas](../philosophers/aquinas.md)** (continues) : [Aquinas](../philosophers/aquinas.md) inherits from [Augustine](../philosophers/augustine.md), whom he cites tirelessly, the conception of evil as a [privation](../glossary/privation.md) of good and the free-will defence against the problem of evil, along with the broad Christian-Platonist frame. Yet he re-grounds this legacy on the metaphysics of [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md), shifting Augustinianism toward a theology of being.
- **[Hannah Arendt](../philosophers/arendt.md)** (reinterprets) : [Arendt](../philosophers/arendt.md) devotes her dissertation to love in [Augustine](../philosophers/augustine.md), where she draws the motif that will run through her work: the human being as a "beginning" who, in being born, introduces something new into the world.
- **[Descartes](../philosophers/descartes.md)** (reinterprets) : [Descartes](../philosophers/descartes.md)'s “cogito” has a famous Augustinian precedent, the “si fallor, sum”: [Augustine](../philosophers/augustine.md) bequeaths him the self-certainty drawn from doubt itself and the turn toward interiority, which Descartes recasts as the foundation of knowledge, acknowledging no borrowing.
