# Boethius

*477 – 524 · Roman*

Roman philosopher of late antiquity, author of "The Consolation of Philosophy", who sought to reconcile divine foreknowledge with human freedom by placing God outside time.

## Influenced by

- **[Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md)** (transmits) : [Boethius](../philosophers/boethius.md) set himself the task of translating and commenting on [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md)'s Organon: he thereby became the only logical Aristotle known to the early Latin Middle Ages.
- **[Platonism](../movements/platonism.md)** (reinterprets) : [Boethius](../philosophers/boethius.md)'s Consolation of Philosophy breathes late [Platonism](../movements/platonism.md): the soul's ascent toward the Good, an ordering providence, evil deprived of being, in a prose that Christianises the heritage without citing it.

## Influence on

- **[Thomas Aquinas](../philosophers/aquinas.md)** (transmits) : [Boethius](../philosophers/boethius.md) hands the Latin world its logical and metaphysical vocabulary (person, nature, eternity) and the method of the theological tractates that [Aquinas](../philosophers/aquinas.md) takes up and comments on.
