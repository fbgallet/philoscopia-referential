# Schopenhauer

*1788 – 1860 · German*

German pessimist philosopher: he sees the world as the expression of a blind will-to-live, of which love is merely the ruse to perpetuate the species, and art an escape from desire.

## Influenced by

- **[Buddhism](../movements/buddhism.md)** (reinterprets) : [Schopenhauer](../philosophers/schopenhauer.md) finds in [Buddhism](../movements/buddhism.md) an Eastern confirmation of his thought: the centrality of suffering, detachment, and the denial of the will to live as a path of deliverance.
- **[Kant](../philosophers/kant.md)** (reinterprets) : [Schopenhauer](../philosophers/schopenhauer.md) adopts the Kantian division between appearance and thing-in-itself, but identifies the latter with the will: a blind, endless striving of which the world of representations is only the manifestation.
- **[Vedānta (Advaita)](../movements/vedanta.md)** (reinterprets) : [Schopenhauer](../philosophers/schopenhauer.md) draws from the Upaniṣads and [Vedānta](../movements/vedanta.md), which he revered, the motif of the veil of appearances (Māyā) and the metaphysical unity behind plurality, which he retranslates into his philosophy of the will.

## Influence on

- **[Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md)** (reacts against) : As a young man, [Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md) hails [Schopenhauer](../philosophers/schopenhauer.md) as his educator and inherits his tragic reading of existence and the centrality of the will. He then turns the inheritance around: against pessimism and the denial of the will to live, he sets the affirmation of life and the will to power.
