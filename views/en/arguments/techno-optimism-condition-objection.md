# Progress of means

*Objection to [Relation to technology](../axes/technology.md) : Techno-optimism*

**Technology multiplies our means, but a surplus of means is not by itself a better life.**

## The argument

1. Grant the optimist his starting point: our means of acting, producing, communicating have grown as never before. But he at once concludes that our condition has improved, and it is that step which must be examined.
2. For a means is worth only the end it serves: having more tools makes life better only if those tools serve what makes life good. To multiply means without questioning ends is to gain in power, not necessarily in happiness or freedom.
3. And the very power that removes old ills gives rise to new ones: it creates needs we never had, dependencies, unprecedented forms of fatigue and solitude. The reckoning is thus not the simple addition the optimist assumes, and nothing guarantees it tips the right way for life itself.

**⇒** So from the growth of our means one cannot conclude the progress of our condition: it is a leap from power to the good life that nothing secures.

## Deepenings

### Rousseau's suspicion

*The historical example of the gap between progress in means and progress in morals.*

1. As early as the eighteenth century, [Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md) argued, in his first discourse, that the progress of the sciences and arts had polished manners without making people better or happier: the first striking illustration of the gap between the advance of means and that of the condition.

*[Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md), *Discourse on the Sciences and Arts*, 1750*

## Ways out of the objection

- **That is decisive: I had taken the growth of our means for the growth of our lives.** : You don't deny technology's power; you stop reading it automatically as a better way of living.
- **I qualify: technology does not make happiness, but it removes great obstacles, pain, hunger, ignorance.** : You now distinguish what technology can do, remove ills, from what it cannot, give meaning.
- **I refute it: removing suffering and ignorance already makes life better; the rest is up to us.** : You hold the reduction of ills to be a real progress of the condition, and refer meaning to each person's freedom.
