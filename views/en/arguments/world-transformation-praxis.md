# Transform, not merely interpret

*Argument for [The point of philosophy](../axes/metaphilosophy.md) : Changing the world*

**A philosophy that leaves the world as it is betrays its task: to think truly is to work to change what must be changed.**

*[Marx](../philosophers/marx.md), *Theses on Feuerbach, XI*, 1845*

## The argument

1. No thought floats outside the world: it is born of social conditions and, in turn, acts upon them, shoring them up or shaking them. There is no neutral standpoint above the fray.
2. So contenting oneself with understanding leaves intact the dominations one describes: professed neutrality always benefits the order in place. Interpreting the world in a thousand ways has left it as it was.
3. The soundness of an idea is proven not in books but in [practice](../glossary/praxis.md): it is by changing the world that it is tested, and that one frees those whom an unjust order crushes.

**⇒** So philosophy's task is not to contemplate but to transform: a thought is judged by what it frees, and to think without acting is to consent.
