# The endless demand

*Objection to [Partiality](../axes/partiality.md) : Impartialism*

**If everyone counts equally, your whole life becomes a failing.**

## The argument

1. If a stranger's pain weighs as much as a loved one's, every euro spent on a pleasure I could do without is a euro owed to someone dying for want of care.
2. Strict impartiality grants no respite: giving my child a gift rather than saving a distant child becomes, at every moment, a dereliction.
3. A morality that no human being can keep, and that condemns even the love we bear our own, may have mistaken what morality is.

**⇒** A demand that no one can live by, and that turns attachment into a fault, may refute itself.

## Ways out of the objection

- **You are right: a morality that condemns all particular care and makes ordinary life a permanent fault has mistaken what it can demand.** : You restore the bonds' legitimacy: the impartial demand has a limit.
- **I maintain the equal worth of each, but I recognize limits to what it can demand of us: partiality has its place.** : You keep the impartial foundation while giving it a livable form: the concentric circles.
- **I refute it: that a demand is heavy does not make it false; better an ideal we fall short of than a comfort we christen morality.** : You accept the demand: the difficulty of a duty does not excuse us from it.
