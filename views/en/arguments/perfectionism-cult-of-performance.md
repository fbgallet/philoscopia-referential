# The cult of self-overcoming

*Objection to [The highest good](../axes/sovereign-good.md) : Virtue / moral perfectionism — attacks the position*

**To make self-overcoming the supreme value is to turn yourself into your own torturer.**

*Alain Ehrenberg, *The Cult of Performance; The Weariness of the Self*, 1998*

## The argument

1. The path of self-overcoming makes effort on oneself the seat of the good: to conquer oneself, to be disciplined, to go further than yesterday would be worth more than to enjoy or to succeed.
2. But to make self-overcoming the supreme value is to give oneself a measure with no end: there is always someone stronger, faster, a record to break, a yesterday-self to humble. The [cult of performance](../glossary/culte-de-la-performance.md) installs at the heart of oneself a judge that never says "that is enough".
3. The athlete offers its image: whoever is worth only as much as they surpass themselves wears out proving it, and lives each limit not as a fact, but as a fault. Alain Ehrenberg named this underside "the weariness of the self": depression as the shadow cast by the injunction to keep performing.
4. What was meant to forge character therefore gnaws at it: discipline turns into inner tyranny, demandingness into violence against oneself.

**⇒** So to make self-overcoming the highest good is not to rise, it is to condemn oneself: a value with no measure becomes a master with no mercy.

## Deepenings

### Where the injunction to surpass oneself comes from

*We are said to have passed from a society of discipline, ruled by the permitted and the forbidden, to a society of performance, where only the possible counts: the self then becomes its own task, with no limit to rest it.*

1. Ehrenberg locates the origin of this ill in a shift: yesterday, the individual measured themselves against what was permitted or forbidden; today, against what is possible, against what they could achieve.
2. But nothing outside comes any longer to say "you have reached your place": freed from prohibitions, the self becomes its own endless task, alone responsible for its success as for its failure, and exhaustion replaces the guilt of former times.

*Alain Ehrenberg, *The Weariness of the Self*, 1998*

## Ways out of the objection

- **True: self-overcoming without measure becomes a trap. I would rather seek to realize myself, to unfold what I can, than to punish myself for never being enough.** : You slide from perfectionism toward [flourishing](../glossary/eudaimonism.md): fulfilling yourself rather than forever outbidding yourself.
- **I still hold self-overcoming to be a good, but I grant it: it must not become that inner judge that forgives nothing.** : You keep the virtue of effort while stripping it of its excess: to surpass oneself, without mistreating oneself.
- **What you call torturing oneself, I call demanding of oneself. That the cult of performance perverts effort does not condemn effort: it is the abuse that is at issue, not the value.** : You distinguish the virtue of self-overcoming from its modern caricature: chosen askesis is not performance endured.
