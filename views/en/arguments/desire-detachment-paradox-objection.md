# The desire to desire no more

*Objection to [Relation to desire](../axes/desire.md) : Extinction and detachment*

**To want to free oneself from desire is still a desire: one does not so easily leave what one claims to quit.**

## The argument

1. Detachment aims to extinguish desire in order to reach peace. But this peace, one wants it: to aspire to serenity, to work at freeing oneself, is still to desire something, and strongly.
2. Desire, then, cannot be removed from outside: one displaces it, from the world toward deliverance itself. To want to want nothing more is still a willing, one that would take itself as its target.
3. And if one truly managed to let go of all, what would remain? Whoever holds to nothing no longer holds to justice, to their own, to the fate of the world: in making oneself invulnerable, one risks making oneself indifferent.

**⇒** So detachment cannot be total without contradicting itself or growing cold: it too must keep certain desires, those worth holding to.

## Deepenings

### To detach without closing off

1. [Buddhism](../movements/buddhism.md) does not preach indifference: to the extinction of selfish thirst answers compassion for all that suffers; detachment there aims at craving, not at the care for others.
2. But the objection remains: why keep that care, which is still an attachment, and set aside the rest? One needs, then, within detachment itself, a sorting between the desires one holds just and those one rejects.

## Ways out of the objection

- **One does not remove all desire, and one should not. Better to choose those worth keeping than to claim to have none.** : You move from extinction to discernment: no longer « no desire », but « the right desires », which is already the path of sorting.
- **I keep detachment as a direction, while granting it cannot be perfected: one lightens most of one's desires, without the illusion of abolishing them all.** : You hold detachment as a path more than a state: one loosens the grip without ever claiming to have wholly let go.
- **The aspiration to peace is not a desire like the others, it is the one that quiets them. As for the care for others, detachment does not extinguish it, it purifies it of craving.** : You hold to the path: there is a desire that undoes desires, like a fire that goes out from having consumed everything.
