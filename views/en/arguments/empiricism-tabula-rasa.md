# The blank slate

*Argument for [Source of knowledge](../axes/knowledge-source.md) : Empiricism*

**The mind is born empty; all our ideas, even the most abstract, come from the senses.**

*[Locke](../philosophers/locke.md), [Hume](../philosophers/hume.md), *An Essay concerning Human Understanding, Books I–II*, 1689*

## The argument

1. Let us seek an idea that would be in us before any experience. The [supposedly innate ideas](../glossary/innate-ideas.md), God, identity, first principles, ought to be recognized by all; yet the child has none of them, and whole peoples have no notion of them.
2. Every idea one examines can be traced back to a received impression: take from a man born blind the experience of colors, and the very idea of color is missing. The mind forms nothing that it has not first drawn from the senses, or from reflection on its own operations.
3. Nothing, then, is in the understanding that was not first in the senses: the mind is at the start like a [blank slate](../glossary/tabula-rasa.md), an empty cabinet that experience alone comes to furnish.

**⇒** So all knowledge derives from experience: our ideas are no innate treasure, but the deposit of what we have perceived.
