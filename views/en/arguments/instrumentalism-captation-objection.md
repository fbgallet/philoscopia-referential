# An unequal contest

*Objection to [Relation to technology](../axes/technology.md) : Instrumentalism*

**Pitting your will alone against an industry that studies your weaknesses is a contest lost in advance.**

## The argument

1. Grant that the use depends on you. But is that use decided on equal terms? On one side, your will of an evening, tired, distracted. On the other, thousands of engineers whose job is to catch it off guard.
2. The feed is tested, measured, tuned on millions of people to find what keeps you there: unpredictable reward, borderless scrolling, flattering numbers. This is the [attention economy](../glossary/attention-economy.md): your viewing time is the commodity, and everything is set to extract more of it.
3. To say then « one need only use it well » is to expect your will alone to prevail over vast means, studied and ceaselessly adjusted to catch it out. The freedom of use remains, but it does not carry the weight ascribed to it.

**⇒** So to refer the problem to use alone is to burden the individual with a contest that the object, for its part, did not fight fairly.

## Ways out of the objection

- **That is decisive: my will is no match for a device built to outwit it; use alone is not enough.** : You stop expecting everything from personal discipline, and grant the object a force that must be met by other means.
- **I qualify: the contest is unequal, all the more reason to arm oneself, with rules, limit-setting tools, screen-free times.** : You keep responsibility for use, but you stop believing it sufficient on its own.
- **I refute it: I have been taught to resist many solicitations, advertising, sugar, gambling; this one is no different in kind.** : You place captation among ordinary temptations, which upbringing and habit teach us to hold in check.
