# The subterfuge

*Objection to [Freedom of the will](../axes/freedom.md) : Compatibilism*

**Redefining freedom, is that not dodging the real question?**

*[Kant](../philosophers/kant.md), [William James](../philosophers/william-james.md)*

## The argument

1. The question was: could we have acted otherwise? The compatibilist answers it by changing the meaning of the word "free", not by answering it.
2. [Kant](../philosophers/kant.md) called it a "wretched subterfuge"; William James, a "quagmire of evasion". The freedom that truly matters, they say, is the power to [do otherwise](../glossary/alternate-possibilities.md), which determinism removes.
3. To call an entirely determined act "free" would be to save the word while losing the thing: responsibility, merit, remorse presuppose more than the mere absence of external constraint.

**⇒** So the compatibilist compromise may be a word over a problem: either freedom is real, or it is not.

## Ways out of the objection

- **You are right: if compatibilist freedom is only a word, what we need is a real freedom, the power to do otherwise.** : You refuse the compromise from above: you demand a genuine [free will](../glossary/free-will.md), able to escape causes.
- **The other way: if neither compatibilist freedom nor the power to do otherwise holds, better admit it, we are not free.** : You refuse the compromise from below: rather than a redefined freedom, you lean toward an avowed [determinism](../glossary/determinism.md).
- **I refute it: the freedom that matters is not a magic power to escape causes, but the absence of constraint. That is no word, it is what matters.** : You hold [compatibilism](../glossary/compatibilism.md) to be a thesis, not an evasion: acting from one's reasons suffices to be free.
