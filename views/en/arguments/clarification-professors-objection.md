# Professors of philosophy, not philosophers

*Objection to [The point of philosophy](../axes/metaphilosophy.md) : Clarifying thought — attacks the position*

**Turned into a university trade, the philosophy that promised to teach thinking now speaks mostly to its own specialists: papers answering papers, in a language the outside world does not read.**

*[Thoreau](../philosophers/thoreau.md), *Walden*, 1854*

## The argument

1. Critical examination has become a profession: careers, specialized journals, conferences, sectarian disputes. Rigor gains by it, but in a form so technical, and on problems so finely carved, that the outside reader hardly recognizes their own questions in it anymore.
2. “There are nowadays professors of philosophy, but not philosophers”, wrote [Thoreau](../philosophers/thoreau.md), who granted that professing is admirable, provided one lives what one teaches: to philosophize is “to solve some of the problems of life, not only theoretically, but practically”. The objection extends of itself to today's university: a clarification that promised to untie everyone's confusions devotes much of its strength to knots it tied itself.
3. While the specialists grow ever more specialized, the demand for meaning does not go away: it shifts to others, popularizers, coaches, ideologues, who do speak everyone's language. A clarity that circulates so little leaves the field open to public confusions.

**⇒** So, to the extent that it speaks only to its specialists, philosophy would fail its own task: a clarity that does not circulate clarifies nothing for anyone, and the examination of ideas, turned into a career, would risk ceasing to be the examination of life.

## Ways out of the objection

- **True: clarity that serves only the initiated has lost its point. Philosophy must again become an exercise each person practices on their own life.** : You move philosophy over to practice: fewer papers, more lived examination. The move of [Thoreau](../philosophers/thoreau.md) himself, who went to test it in the woods.
- **Technicality is the price of rigor, here as elsewhere; but it is only half the work, the other half being to bring clarity back down into the common language. A demanding discipline and a public voice: either without the other fails.** : Clarification owns both its faces: the laboratory and the agora. The ideal of the [Enlightenment](../glossary/lumieres.md): dare to know, and put knowledge back into circulation.
- **To reproach philosophy for its specialists is to reproach it for having become serious: no one blames physics for its equations. Yesterday's clarifications, logic, the analysis of language, now irrigate law, computing, medical ethics; technical work most often ends up equipping common thought.** : The defense of the trade: the division of intellectual labor is no betrayal, and its products spread, late but surely.
