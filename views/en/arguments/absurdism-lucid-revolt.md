# Holding out without cheating

*Argument for [The meaning of life](../axes/life-meaning.md) : Absurdism*

**A life can be fully worth living without a meaning beyond it: to recognize the absurd and hold it in lucid revolt is to reclaim one's life without cheating.**

*[Camus](../philosophers/camus.md), *[The Myth of Sisyphus](../works/the-myth-of-sisyphus-camus.md)**

## The argument

1. Our reason demands a meaning, a reason to be; the world, for its part, stays silent. From this confrontation between our demand and the muteness of things arises the [absurd](../glossary/the-absurd.md).
2. Two escapes present themselves, equally dishonest: hope, which invents a hidden meaning so as not to see the silence; and renunciation, up to suicide, which removes the demand so as to suffer the silence no longer.
3. There remains a third path, the only lucid one: to hold both terms without reconciling them, to keep the demand for meaning while looking the silence in the face. This is [revolt](../glossary/revolt.md), paid for by neither illusion nor abdication.

**⇒** So a life can be fully worth living without a meaning beyond it: its worth lies in the lucidity and intensity of the revolt, not in an answer that will not come.
