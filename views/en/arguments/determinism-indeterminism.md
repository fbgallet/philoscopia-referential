# The indeterminism objection

*Objection to [Freedom of the will](../axes/freedom.md) : Hard determinism*

**If matter itself is not determined, the future is not written in advance.**

*Indeterminist tradition*

## The argument

1. The argument assumes every effect is wholly fixed by its causes. Yet quantum physics describes fundamental events that are merely **probable**, not determined.
2. If [indeterminism](../glossary/indeterminism.md) holds at the finest level, "everything has a cause" no longer means "everything is settled in advance": one and the same cause may leave several outcomes open.
3. [Causal closure](../glossary/causal-closure.md) survives, nothing springs from nothing; but its determinist conclusion no longer follows: the web of causes leaves play.

**⇒** So causal closure does not prove strict [determinism](../glossary/determinism.md): the future may not be wholly fixed.

## Ways out of the objection

- **The objection lands: if matter has play, the future is not written, and strict determinism fails.** : You leave strict determinism without reaching free will: since chance is not freedom, you stop at a middle position.
- **I keep determinism at our scale: quantum chance averages out, and the brain remains a predictable system.** : You distinguish scales: the indeterminacy of the tiny does not, in practice, reach up to deliberation.
- **I refute it: chance is not freedom. Replacing necessity with a die does not make me any more the author of my choices.** : A decisive point against libertarianism: [indeterminism](../glossary/indeterminism.md) dissolves necessity but offers no foothold for a genuine power to choose.
