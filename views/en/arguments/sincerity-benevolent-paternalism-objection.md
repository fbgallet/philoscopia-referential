# The lie that infantilizes

*Objection to [Sincerity](../axes/sincerity.md) : Benevolent lying permitted*

**Lying "for the good" of another is deciding in their place what they can bear.**

## The argument

1. The benevolent lie assumes that I know, better than the other, what is good for them: I judge them too fragile for the truth.
2. It denies them the respect owed to a being capable of reason, treating them as a **child** to be handled rather than an adult to be informed.
3. Often, besides, the lie spares first the one who tells it: he spares himself a hard conversation and calls it kindness.

**⇒** Under cover of protecting them, the benevolent lie takes from the other what is theirs by right: the truth, and the freedom to judge it.

## Ways out of the objection

- **The objection lands: by lying to spare them, I deprive the other of a choice that is theirs to make.** : You grant that respect for the other comes before the wish to spare them: the truth is owed to them.
- **I maintain that a lie can be good, but only to spare a truly pointless suffering, never out of convenience.** : You narrow the permission: not to spare yourself discomfort, but to protect someone who can gain nothing from the truth.
- **I refute it: to recognize that a truth would be devastating and useless is not to disdain the other, it is to care for them.** : You hold kindness to be a form of regard, not of contempt: to spare someone is not to infantilize them.
