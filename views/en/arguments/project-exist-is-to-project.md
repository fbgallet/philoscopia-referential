# To exist is to project oneself

*Argument for [Temporal orientation](../axes/time-orientation.md) : Project*

**Man is not a given thing: he is only what he makes of himself by projecting toward the future.**

*[Sartre](../philosophers/sartre.md), *Existentialism Is a Humanism**

## The argument

1. A stone is what it is, wholly in the present. Man, by contrast, is never quite given: he relates ceaselessly to what he is not yet, what he wants to become, to do, to accomplish.
2. This is what [Sartre](../philosophers/sartre.md) means: [existence precedes essence](../axes/essence.md). No one is born with a ready-made nature; we are our acts and our commitments to come, and it is by projecting ourselves that we give ourselves a shape.
3. From then on, to fall back on the instant alone, to refuse to project oneself, would be to treat oneself as a thing, to give up what makes us subjects. This retreat has a name, [bad faith](../glossary/bad-faith.md): hiding one's own freedom from oneself by passing for a being with no future.

**⇒** So to live, for a human being, is to strain toward what is not yet: not to lose oneself in the future, but to recognize oneself there as the author of what one becomes.

## Deepenings

### Why we are not things

1. A thing coincides with itself; a consciousness, never. I am not my occupation the way the inkwell is an inkwell: I am it in the mode of having-to-be it, that is, by projecting myself into it.
2. This distance from oneself is our very freedom: because nothing fixes me once and for all, I am responsible for what I make of myself, and that « what I make » is turned toward tomorrow.

*[Sartre](../philosophers/sartre.md), *Being and Nothingness**
