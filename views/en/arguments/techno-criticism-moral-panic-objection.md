# The charge of nostalgia

*Objection to [Relation to technology](../axes/technology.md) : Techno-criticism*

**In every age, the new technology was said to be our undoing; every time, we made our peace with it.**

## The argument

1. Mistrust of technology has a long history, and a poor record of predictions. [Plato](../philosophers/plato.md) feared writing, others the novel, the railway, television: each time, the announcement of a softened mind, a broken bond, a lost generation.
2. Yet we learned to live with all of it. What was taken for the end of a world was only the discomfort of a habit to be formed. The **moral panic** often says less about the object it accuses than about the fear of those growing old alongside it.
3. Worse, this mistrust treats people as passive victims, incapable of judging and conducting themselves. By seeing manipulated dupes everywhere, one comes to scorn the ordinary freedom of users, and offers, for want of better, only a refusal no one will follow.

**⇒** So to mistrust technology may be to repeat an old fear that has always been wrong, and to take people for less free than they are.

## Ways out of the objection

- **The objection lands: I was perhaps replaying an old fear, and underestimating our capacity to adapt.** : You loosen your mistrust: what worries us today might, as before, become a mastered habit.
- **I refine it: to be wary is not to reject everything; I distinguish technologies by what they do to us, without condemning technology wholesale.** : You keep your wariness, but make it selective: not all technologies are alike before this charge.
- **I refute it: that we were wrong before proves nothing for today; a device built to capture attention is not just one more novelty.** : You reject the conflation: fear of the railway is nothing like an industry that measures and exploits your attention.
