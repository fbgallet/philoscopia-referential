# The lie comes back

*Objection to [Sincerity](../axes/sincerity.md) : Benevolent lying permitted*

**The benevolent lie destroys, in the end, what it claims to protect.**

## The argument

1. A lie seldom stays secret: one day, the other learns that they were lied to "for their own good".
2. From then on, each of your reassuring words becomes suspect: they no longer know when you are sparing them and when you are sincere.
3. A trust damaged this way is not mended with a word; the lie that meant to spare a pain sows a more lasting one.

**⇒** In deceiving to protect, one trades a present pain for a mistrust that, unlike it, does not heal.

## Ways out of the objection

- **You are right: the price paid in trust is too heavy; better a hard truth than a lie that eats away at the bond.** : You place trust above immediate relief: this is the rigorist's intuition.
- **I grant it for those close to me, whose trust extends over time; but a lie to a stranger, with no tomorrow, destroys no bond.** : You restrict the permission to situations with no shared future, where trust is not at stake.
- **I refute it: not all lies come to light, and some protect a peace that was worth more than the truth.** : You maintain that a well-kept lie can spare lastingly, without the supposed backlash.
