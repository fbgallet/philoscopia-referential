# You will not be there

*Objection to [Temporal orientation](../axes/time-orientation.md) : Memory and transmission*

**To console oneself with what one transmits is to hide from oneself that one will no longer be there to enjoy it.**

## The argument

1. Grant that we receive everything from the past. It remains to ask what it is worth, for the life one has to live, to turn first toward what precedes and follows us.
2. For of transmission I will not see the fruit: when my legacy bears, I will no longer be there. To devote myself to it is to live for a time from which I am absent, and to entrust my worth to what will happen without me.
3. One may see in it a last ruse against death: failing to endure, one dreams of surviving in one's children, one's work, one's line. But to face one's own end, [being-toward-death](../glossary/being-toward-death.md), is to understand that no one dies in my place, and that this time allotted to me is mine to live, not my heirs'.

**⇒** So to devote oneself to transmission can soften the thought of vanishing, but at the cost of a detour: one busies oneself with the afterward so as not to have to inhabit the now, the only time still ours.

## Deepenings

### Living for the afterward

1. Care for posterity is ancient and noble, one plants trees whose shade one will not see, and no one scorns it. The only question is whether it should govern a whole life.
2. For it can serve as an alibi: one leaves it to the inheritance to give meaning to days one did not know how to live oneself, and transmission then becomes what it claimed to avoid, a way of passing one's own life by.

## Ways out of the objection

- **That is right: I will not see what I transmit, and I have only one life, mine, to live now. It is that life, first, that I do not want to miss.** : The objection brings you back to the present: before living for the afterward, to live the only time you are sure of, the one that is here.
- **I hold to transmitting, but without forgetting myself along the way: caring for what follows me does not require deserting my own life.** : You keep the care for the afterward without sacrificing the present to it: one can transmit and live, the one does not exclude the other.
- **To live only for oneself and one's own time is the true smallness. What does it matter that I do not see the fruit: what exceeds me is worth more than what stops at me.** : You accept living for something greater than yourself: the value of a legacy is not measured by what one gets from it, and it is that going-beyond that makes its greatness.
