# The instant that slips by

*Objection to [Temporal orientation](../axes/time-orientation.md) : Living in the present*

**By living only the instant, one skims over life without ever inhabiting it.**

## The argument

1. Living in the present, we are told, frees us from regret and expectation. But the instant, by nature, does not last: barely tasted, one must already move on to the next.
2. This race from pleasure to pleasure, from one high point to the next, looks all but identical to what [Pascal](../philosophers/pascal.md) called [divertissement](../glossary/divertissement.md): not rest, but a restlessness that keeps us from being alone with ourselves.
3. Lacking a thread that ties its days together, the presentist risks a life without depth: a succession of vivid, quickly forgotten instants that gather into nothing, no work, no lasting bond, no memory.

**⇒** So to inhabit the present alone may not be peace, but another flight, a gentler one: the flight that dodges depth instead of dodging boredom.

## Deepenings

### A tourist in one's own days

1. One recognizes this life by a sign: everything in it is consumed, nothing is kept. Experiences pile up like images on a screen, leaving no trace.
2. The reproach is not enjoying the present, but doing only that: by letting nothing ripen, one passes through one's years as a visitor, never as a dweller.

## Ways out of the objection

- **It is true: living only the instant, I build nothing that endures. Perhaps I need a thread longer than the day.** : You are after a depth the instant alone does not give, on the side of what lasts and is passed on.
- **I keep the present, but I want it to leave a trace: to savor, yes, without settling for flitting about.** : You distinguish two presents, the one that stuns itself and the one that takes root; you hold to the second.
- **To confuse attention to the present with restlessness gets it backwards: the instant fully lived is no flight, it is the only true presence.** : You overturn the reproach: it is not presence that dilutes, it is the care for past and future that absents us from the only real time.
