# To act without understanding is to act blind

*Objection to [The point of philosophy](../axes/metaphilosophy.md) : Changing the world — attacks the position*

**A thought judged by what it changes bows to effectiveness: it risks bending truth to the cause, and acting before it has understood.**

*[Arendt](../philosophers/arendt.md), *The Human Condition*, 1958*

## The argument

1. Transformation wants to measure an idea by its effects: true if it emancipates, vain if it leaves the world as it is.
2. But 'what changes the world' and 'what is true' are two things: a false belief can raise crowds, an inconvenient truth remain without effect. To judge an idea by its effectiveness is to risk subordinating the true to the useful, and thought to propaganda.
3. And one transforms well only what one has first understood: to act without analysis is to reproduce blindly the ills one thought one was fighting. Theory does not follow action, it precedes it.

**⇒** So a philosophy's worth is not measured by what it changes: subordinating understanding to action is to risk acting wrongly and sacrificing truth to the cause.

## Ways out of the objection

- **True: understand first, else one acts wrongly. The true does not bend to the useful.** : You return to contemplation: truth comes before effectiveness, and analysis before action.
- **I keep the concern to transform, but under the control of truth: act, yes, but after understanding.** : An enlightened praxis: action remains the aim, but understanding precedes and corrects it.
- **This 'understanding first' is an alibi for inaction: one always understands from a position, and to wait for full clarity is to let injustice endure. Practice is itself a source of truth.** : The reply of praxis: there is no prior neutral understanding; it is in acting that one tests and learns.
