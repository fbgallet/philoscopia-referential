# What no progress reaches

*Objection to [Scope of action](../axes/control-scope.md) : Prometheanism*

**Progress pushes back the limits of humanity, not those of your life.**

## The argument

1. Grant that knowledge has undone many an inevitability. But these victories are the species', and most often statistical: medicine pushes back mortality, it does not abolish your death.
2. Yet the ordeal, you, you live it in the singular: this grief, this illness, this time that will not return. No collective progress gives back the one who is lost, nor will it give you back the age you were. Before this, the ambition to transform stands empty-handed.
3. The Promethean then risks forever deferring peace: as long as a limit remains to be pushed back, he grants himself no rest, and one always remains. By measuring his serenity against his mastery, he condemns himself never to be at peace just where he can do nothing.

**⇒** So even an endless progress leaves intact a share of the irreducible, the one where each of us, alone, must come to terms with what he will not change.

## Ways out of the objection

- **The objection reaches the heart: over what is essential in my own life, I cannot do everything. I must also learn to accept what I will not change.** : You make room for the irreducible without giving up on acting: the path of discernment, where action and acceptance share the task.
- **I keep the drive, but set my peace apart from it: to act with all my strength, without hanging my serenity on the result alone.** : You keep the ambition while loosening what it demands: to transform the world, without expecting everything from that transformation.
- **I refute it: finitude itself is a challenge, not a fate. To lengthen life, to ease loss, to push back death, this too is work to carry on.** : You extend the project to finitude itself: what seems insurmountable today is, for you, tomorrow's work site.
