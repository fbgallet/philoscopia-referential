# The great diversion

*Objection to [The highest good](../axes/sovereign-good.md) : Hedonism — attacks the position*

**The pleasure of sport, playing it or watching it, is a diversion in Pascal's sense: a noise that turns us away from ourselves.**

*[Pascal](../philosophers/pascal.md), *Pensées*, 1670*

## The argument

1. The path of pleasure holds enjoyment to be the good: the joy of running, the endorphins, the excitement of the match would be, on their own, enough to make a happy life.
2. But [Pascal](../philosophers/pascal.md) warns us: a human being cannot stay quietly in a room. They need noise, stir, a hare to chase, not for the catch, but so as not to see their condition. This is what he calls [diversion](../glossary/divertissement.md).
3. And sport is its finished form: the effort that empties the head, the stadium that makes crowds roar, the ranking one keeps refreshing. The fan's pleasure is even a pleasure by proxy, the emotion of a feat that is not their own.
4. This pleasure is therefore not an evil, but a flight: it fills time so that we do not feel it empty, and a whole life handed over to these emotions passes by what, within it, called to be faced.

**⇒** So to set up this pleasure as the highest good is to take dizziness for happiness: diversion soothes our unease for a moment, it never resolves it.

## Deepenings

### Why we flee rest

*Pascal observes that we love the hunt more than the catch, the stir more than the goal: it is not the game we seek, it is not having to stop.*

1. [Pascal](../philosophers/pascal.md) shows it by a paradox: offer the hunter the hare without the hunt, and he refuses it. It is not the catch he wants, then, but the pursuit, all that keeps him from thinking.
2. Left with nothing to do, a person feels boredom rise, and behind it the dread of their finitude. Diversion is that veil thrown over rest, precisely because rest would return us to ourselves.

*[Pascal](../philosophers/pascal.md), *Pensées*, 1670*

## Ways out of the objection

- **True: strong sensations alone end up ringing hollow. A good life asks of me something I build, not only something I feel.** : You move from pleasure to [fulfillment](../glossary/eudaimonism.md): what one builds holds better than a thrill that fades.
- **I hold the joy of play to be a real good, but I grant it: not every pleasure is worth taking, and some are only a flight.** : You are already sorting your pleasures: the living joy of a body at play is not the spectator's dizziness.
- **The joy of a body that runs and plays is not a flight, it is life itself. Pascal's suspicion targets empty agitation, not a pleasure that makes me present to the world and to myself.** : You distinguish the diversion that dulls from the pleasure that wakes: to play can be a presence, not a flight.
