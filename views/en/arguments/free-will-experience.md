# The argument from the experience of deliberation

*Argument for [Freedom of the will](../axes/freedom.md) : Free will*

**The very experience of deliberating attests a freedom no theory can erase.**

## The argument

1. When I deliberate, I directly undergo a power: holding several possibilities open, then settling.
2. This experience is primary: every theory, determinism included, must lean on it merely to be thought and argued.
3. To dismiss that immediate testimony in the name of a theory would be to saw off the branch on which the argument itself sits.

**⇒** [Free will](../glossary/free-will.md) is therefore a primary given, which any theory must respect before denying it.
