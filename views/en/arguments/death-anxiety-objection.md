# The anxiety objection

*Objection to [Death](../axes/death.md) : Freeing ourselves from the fear*

**An argument, however sound, is not enough to dispel the fear of dying.**

*[Heidegger](../philosophers/heidegger.md)*

## The argument

1. The Epicurean argument treats the fear of death as an error of reasoning: whoever grasps that "death is nothing to us" should see it vanish of its own accord.
2. But this fear is not a mere opinion: it is the living being's attachment to its own existence, older than any reasoning and more obstinate than it. One can grant the conclusion and tremble still.
3. Experience confirms it: no one contemplates their own vanishing as a truth of geometry. What we dread is not a painful state, it is no longer being at all, and no demonstration has any hold on that vertigo.

**⇒** So the promised serenity may be only a victory of words: reasoned [ataraxia](../glossary/ataraxia.md) sometimes leaves intact the very anguish it claims to cure.

## Deepenings

### Fear and anguish

1. One distinguishes fear, which always has an object one can face or flee, from anguish, which has none: before death, it is nothing definite that we fear, but the sheer "no longer being."
2. This is why an argument, which always addresses an object, misses anguish: it refutes a reason for fearing, where what would need calming is one's whole relation to oneself.

*[Heidegger](../philosophers/heidegger.md), *Being and Time*, 1927*

## Ways out of the objection

- **True: no reasoning really calms this fear. Better to face it than to believe it dissolved.** : You move from dissolving the fear to facing it: this is the gesture of [being-toward-death](../glossary/being-toward-death.md).
- **I keep the serenity, but as an exercise to sustain, not as a fear already conquered.** : You hold [ataraxia](../glossary/ataraxia.md) to be a never-finished conquest, more honest than a peace decreed.
- **I refute it: fear yields to reason, provided one practices. The argument is not a word, it is a remedy.** : This is, from the start, the [Epicurean](../axes/death.md) reply: to join a daily exercise to the reasoning.
