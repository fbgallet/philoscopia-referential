# A meaning you give yourself does not bind

*Argument for [The meaning of life](../axes/life-meaning.md) : Teleology and providence*

**A meaning I give myself I can just as well take back; only a meaning found, inscribed in an order that surpasses me, can truly orient a life.**

*[the Stoics](../movements/stoicism.md), [Marcus Aurelius](../philosophers/marcus-aurelius.md), *[Meditations](../works/meditations-marcus-aurelius.md)**

## The argument

1. A meaning I give myself I can just as well take back: nothing holds me to it but my decision, and what depends only on my decision cannot truly bind me.
2. Yet a life needs a meaning that surpasses and orients it: something in whose name it is worth acting even when it costs, and that no whim can undo.
3. Such a meaning cannot therefore be an invention: it must be found, inscribed in an order that precedes me, nature, the Logos, a design, whose assigned place for me it is my task to decipher.

**⇒** So meaning is not to be created but discovered: existence is inscribed in an order that surpasses it, and to live well is to learn to attune oneself to it.
