# The suspicion of ressentiment

*Objection to [Death](../axes/death.md) : Refusing death*

**Revolt against death may be nothing but a revenge against life itself, disguised as love of life.**

*[Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md), *Thus Spoke Zarathustra; The Gay Science**

## The argument

1. [Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md) saw in the revolt against time and the irreversible the "spirit of revenge": the rancorous refusal of what was and of what must end. To revolt against death would be to hold existence against its very condition.
2. But mortality cannot be detached from the rest: it is the same finitude that bounds our days and gives them their savor, their urgency, their weight. To say no to death is to say no to life as it is, the only one there is.
3. The refusal then takes on the face of what it fought: no longer a love of life, but a grudge against it. Strength, says Nietzsche, is not to curse what ends, but to love it enough to will it eternally: this is [amor fati](../glossary/amor-fati.md).

**⇒** So your revolt calls for self-examination: is it the height of the love of living, or a [ressentiment](../glossary/ressentiment.md) against life that, in refusing death, also refuses the worth death gives to our days?

## Deepenings

### Would you want this life an infinity of times?

1. [Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md) offers a test: a demon tells you that you will live this life again, down to the smallest detail, an infinite number of times. Would you curse him, or would it be "the most divine" thing you ever heard?
2. Whoever could will the eternal return has nothing left to refuse, death included: he has made finitude not a scandal, but the very form of his consent to living.

*[Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md), *The Gay Science*, 1882*

## Ways out of the objection

- **He is right: my refusal was a "no" to the very condition of living. I would rather learn to say yes, death included.** : You move from refusal to [amor fati](../glossary/amor-fati.md): consenting to death as to the very course of life, not out of resignation but out of love for the real.
- **I can love life and still refuse to bless death: my "no" is aimed at what destroys life, not at the life that includes it. A lucid revolt, not a vengeful one.** : This is [Camus](../philosophers/camus.md)'s revolt rather than ressentiment: a refusal without hope or hatred, one that loves life too much to bless what snuffs it out.
- **I refute it: to call the love of life "ressentiment" is to disarm it. My refusal of death is the very form of my "yes" to living.** : You turn the objection around: far from denying life, revolt is its highest passion, the one that yields nothing of what it loves.
