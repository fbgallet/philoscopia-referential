# Philosophy as a way of life

*Argument for [The point of philosophy](../axes/metaphilosophy.md) : Transforming oneself*

**Philosophy is first of all an exercise that transforms the one who practices it, not a system of propositions: 'empty is the word of the philosopher that heals no human suffering'.**

*[Hadot](../philosophers/hadot.md), [Epicurus](../philosophers/epicurus.md), [the Stoics](../movements/stoicism.md), *[What Is Ancient Philosophy?](../works/what-is-ancient-philosophy-hadot.md)*, 1995*

## The argument

1. For the Ancients, philosophy was not a body of doctrines to learn but a way of life: meditation, self-examination, preparation for hardship, so many **exercises** that worked on desires, fears, judgments.
2. For what troubles us is not so much ignorance of theories as our ill-ordered passions and opinions: knowing that death is nothing is of no use if one goes on fearing it; one must train oneself to live it.
3. A doctrine that does not change the one who holds it stays a dead letter: the true measure of a philosophy is the transformation it works, the passage from a troubled life to a freer and more lucid one.

**⇒** So philosophy is worth something as medicine for the soul and an art of living: not a discourse added to one's life, but an exercise that remakes it.
