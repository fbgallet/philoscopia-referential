# Neither one nor the other

*Objection to [Partiality](../axes/partiality.md) : Concentric circles*

**In wanting both, you risk holding neither equality nor the bond.**

## The argument

1. The position of the circles looks wise, but it may be merely unstable: either equality truly commands, and your partiality is only a tolerated weakness.
2. Or it is the bond that commands, and the "impartial foundation" is only a convenient dressing for your preference for your own.
3. In between, one leans now on one, now on the other, as it suits: which may be leaning on nothing at all.

**⇒** The middle way might therefore be only a polite name for a hesitation between two theses that are, themselves, clear.

## Ways out of the objection

- **Deep down, it is equality that commands; my partiality is only a tolerated weakness, not a principle.** : You settle it from above: equality is the true foundation, the rest a makeshift.
- **Deep down, it is the bond that commands; the impartial demand is only a dressing for my preference for my own.** : You settle it from below: the bond is the true foundation, impartiality a backdrop.
- **I refute it: holding equality at the foundation and gradation in action is not a contradiction, it describes moral life as it is lived.** : You hold the circles to be a thesis, not a hesitation: an order, not a wavering.
