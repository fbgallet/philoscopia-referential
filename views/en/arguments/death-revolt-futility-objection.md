# The objection of impotent revolt

*Objection to [Death](../axes/death.md) : Refusing death*

**Revolting against death changes nothing about death, and spoils the one thing that remains: the present.**

*[Epictetus](../philosophers/epictetus.md), [Spinoza](../philosophers/spinoza.md)*

## The argument

1. Revolt presupposes an adversary that can be swayed. But death is not an injustice a court could overturn: it is a law of nature, as deaf to our anger as the rain or old age.
2. To rise up against the inevitable is to wage a battle lost in advance. [Epictetus](../philosophers/epictetus.md) said it: it is not things that trouble us but our judgments about them; to demand that nature be other than it is condemns us to suffer.
3. Worse: by fixing on death as an enemy, we let it devour every present moment. The rebel suffers twice, from having to die and from his rage at having to die, when wisdom would be to consent in order to live.

**⇒** So revolt may be no more than a sterile agitation: it does not push death back by a single hour, and it robs the present of the serenity that alone depends on us.

## Deepenings

### "A free man thinks of nothing less than of death"

1. [Spinoza](../philosophers/spinoza.md) writes that "a free man thinks of nothing less than of death, and his wisdom is a meditation not on death but on life."
2. To be free is not to defeat death but to stop placing it at the center: understanding that it follows from the order of things disarms revolt without denying anything of life.

*[Spinoza](../philosophers/spinoza.md), *Ethics*, 1677*

## Ways out of the objection

- **True: railing against what I cannot change poisons my days. A lucid serenity is better.** : You lean toward [freeing yourself from the fear](../axes/death.md): consenting to what does not depend on us in order to live in peace.
- **I hold to my refusal, but I turn it toward this death here, the avoidable, unjust, premature death, not toward mortality as such.** : You distinguish two refusals: revolt against unjust death, consent to finitude, a position many hold without knowing it.
- **I refute it: to consent to what crushes us is not wisdom but resignation; lucidity commands us to say no.** : This is the heart of [revolt](../axes/death.md): refusing to mistake appeasement for truth.
