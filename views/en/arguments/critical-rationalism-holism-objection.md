# No refutation is clean

*Objection to [Place of science](../axes/science-place.md) : Critical rationalism — attacks the position*

**One never tests a hypothesis alone: a disproof says a piece is at fault, not which one. Falsifiability does not suffice to govern how the sciences move.**

*Kuhn, *The Structure of Scientific Revolutions*, 1962*

## The argument

1. Critical rationalism makes refutation the heart of science: a theory falls as soon as a fact contradicts it.
2. But one never tests a hypothesis alone, always with a bundle of auxiliary hypotheses. A disproof says that **one** piece is at fault, not which; one can always save the theory by correcting another piece.
3. In fact, no great theory has been dropped over a single anomaly: scientific communities change frameworks for historical, social, aesthetic reasons as much as logical ones. 'Falsifiability' does not by itself govern how the sciences move.

**⇒** So clean demarcation by refutation is a reassuring myth: the boundary of knowledge is blurrier, more historical, than critical rationalism claims.

## Ways out of the objection

- **True: the choice of theories depends on history and on communities, not on a single rule. Method is looser, more plural, than one thinks.** : You slide toward pluralism: with Kuhn and [Feyerabend](../philosophers/feyerabend.md), scientific rationality is historical, not an algorithm.
- **I keep the idea of a science that corrects itself, but I grant that it does so more collectively and historically than pure refutation claims.** : A softened critical rationalism, alert to the social dimension of research, without giving up the idea of progress.
- **Saving a theory with patches has a cost: in the long run, one that no longer risks anything is abandoned. Refutation remains the rule, even if it plays out over time, not on a single fact.** : [Popper](../philosophers/popper.md)'s reply: ad hoc stratagems impoverish a theory, and the community eventually penalizes them.
