# The limits you already accept

*Objection to [Partiality](../axes/partiality.md) : Partialism*

**You already admit that partiality has limits coming from elsewhere.**

## The argument

1. You would give the last seat in the lifeboat to your child without remorse. But you would condemn the judge who acquits his son, or the official who reserves the public contract for his nephew.
2. These limits, you accept: there are situations where putting your own first becomes a wrong, and you know it.
3. Yet if the bond were by itself the foundation, nothing could stand against it; that you recognize limits shows that partiality answers, at bottom, to a demand for equality that goes beyond it.

**⇒** The limits you place on partiality therefore show that it answers, in the last resort, to an impartial justice.

## Ways out of the objection

- **You are right: if I admit a judge must not save his son, it is because an impartial demand prevails, even over the bonds.** : You grant impartiality the last word, where it conflicts with the bond.
- **I grant that my bonds must reckon with a wider rule, without thereby vanishing: they have their place within it.** : You hold together the impartial limit and priority to those close to us: the concentric circles.
- **I refute it: the limits I accept come not from an impartial morality, but from other particular duties, those of my office or my role.** : You explain the limits by other bonds, to the public, to justice, not by abstract equality.
