# The difficulty smuggled away

*Objection to [Scope of action](../axes/control-scope.md) : Knowing the difference*

**To say we must tell the possible from the inevitable, is that to answer the question, or to ask it again?**

## The argument

1. The path of discernment holds in a single formula: act on what depends on us, accept the rest, and know how to tell the difference. Who would not subscribe to it?
2. But it is precisely this knowing how to tell the difference that was the whole question. Where does the boundary of the possible fall? The Stoic draws it close to himself, the Promethean keeps pushing it back: their disagreement is about just this, and discernment settles nothing, it sends each back to his own judgment.
3. A wisdom that contents itself with saying « it depends » risks being no third path, but the polite name for hesitation: one avoids choosing by calling it moderation.

**⇒** So discernment, as long as it does not say how to draw the boundary, might be only a question rephrased as an answer.

## Ways out of the objection

- **You are right, and I lean to the safe side: since the boundary is uncertain, better to draw it close in, on what truly depends on me.** : Lacking a sure rule, you choose Stoic caution: to count on the inner self alone.
- **The other way around: if we never know in advance where the limit lies, better to assume it further off and try, rather than give up too soon.** : You pull the uncertainty toward action: when in doubt, try rather than yield.
- **I refute it: that a wisdom gives no recipe does not condemn it. Discernment is not a formula, it is a judgment to be exercised, and it is what both extremes lack.** : You hold discernment to be a task, not an evasion: judging case by case is more demanding, not less, than following a fixed rule.
