# The line that cannot be found

*Objection to [Relation to desire](../axes/desire.md) : Reasoned selection*

**The division between necessary and empty desires has no assured measure.**

## The argument

1. Sorting assumes a stable criterion: on one side the natural and necessary desires, on the other the empty ones to be dismissed.
2. But this boundary shifts endlessly. Yesterday's comfort becomes today's need; what a hermit judges superfluous, another holds vital. The division seems to depend less on the nature of the desire than on the habits one lives by.
3. Sorting one's desires then risks being merely a way of justifying the standard of living one is used to: we call « necessary » what we do not want to lose, and « empty » what we have already managed to do without.

**⇒** So the wisdom of sorting might be only an arrangement with oneself, disguised as a measure of nature.

## Deepenings

### Natural, necessary, empty

1. [Epicurus](../philosophers/epicurus.md) distinguishes three kinds of desire: the natural and necessary (to eat, to be sheltered, to be free of pain), the natural but unnecessary (fine dishes rather than bread), and the empty ones, with no natural limit (wealth, fame).
2. The objection does not deny the list, it denies that it is given in advance: where to place the desire for knowledge, for beauty, for recognition? Nature, here, does not decide alone.

*[Epicurus](../philosophers/epicurus.md), *[Letter to Menoeceus](../works/letter-to-menoeceus-epicurus.md)**

## Ways out of the objection

- **I cannot draw this line with certainty. Rather than endlessly classifying my desires, better to learn to depend on them less.** : Lacking an assured boundary, you lean toward detachment: if one cannot tell which desire to keep, perhaps one should loosen one's grip on all.
- **I keep sorting, but without claiming to read nature: I draw the line to my own measure, knowing it is revisable.** : You keep the sorting, stripped of its pretension: no longer a boundary inscribed in things, but a lucid examination, always to be redone.
- **The boundary moves at the margins, but the core does not. No one ever needed to be famous in order not to go hungry.** : You hold the heart of the Epicurean criterion: a few desires are enough to live well, and recognizing them calls for no exact science, only lucidity.
