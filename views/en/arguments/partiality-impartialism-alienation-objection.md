# One thought too many

*Objection to [Partiality](../axes/partiality.md) : Impartialism*

**A morality that puts your attachments before an impartial tribunal cuts you off from what gives your life meaning.**

*[Bernard Williams](../philosophers/williams.md), *Moral Luck*, 1981*

## The argument

1. Seeing your spouse and a stranger drowning, if you first ask whether impartiality permits you to save your own, you have already had, as it has been said, one thought too many.
2. Our loves, our loyalties, our gratitudes are not preferences one justifies: they are what make a life worth living.
3. To demand that they answer to an abstract equality is to make oneself a stranger to oneself in the name of morality.

**⇒** An impartiality that condemns attachment thus turns against what it claimed to serve: a human life worth living.

## Ways out of the objection

- **The objection lands: my attachments are not a bias to be corrected, they have a worth of their own.** : You grant the bonds a dignity that strict impartiality denied them.
- **I keep equality as a principle, but I admit that moral life begins in caring for those close to us.** : You make room for the bonds without giving up equality: the path of concentric circles.
- **I refute it: that an attachment is dear to me does not make it just; moral reason must be able to correct it.** : You maintain that the worth of a person does not depend on the place they hold in your heart.
