# The future is not yours

*Objection to [Temporal orientation](../axes/time-orientation.md) : Project*

**To stake your life on the future is to hang it on what does not depend on us.**

## The argument

1. To define oneself by one's projects is to place the meaning of one's life in what is not yet: a goal to reach, a work to finish, a future to build.
2. Yet of all this, almost nothing [truly depends on us](../glossary/dichotomy-of-control.md). An illness, an accident, a setback, and the patiently raised edifice collapses. Whoever is happy only once the goal is reached entrusts his happiness to fortune, which owes him nothing.
3. Worse: the goal once reached, another rises at once. Always straining toward the next stage, the man of projects never enjoys arriving; he lives on borrowed time, awaiting a rest he himself keeps pushing back.

**⇒** So to rest one's life on the future is to build on ground that is not one's own: it remains to be seen whether it is wise to lodge there the whole worth of one's days.

## Deepenings

### The share of fortune

1. The ancients called « fortune » all that, in a life, does not rest on our will: health, success, the time we are granted. The project depends on it through and through.
2. This is why the wisdoms of acceptance advise not to fasten one's peace to what one does not control: not to give up acting, but so as not to place one's happiness in hands that are not one's own.

*[Epictetus](../philosophers/epictetus.md), *[Enchiridion](../works/enchiridion-epictetus.md)**

## Ways out of the objection

- **It is true: by staking everything on tomorrow, I risk never living. Better to hold to the present, the only sure thing.** : You bring the weight of life back toward the instant: since the future escapes, you might as well inhabit the only time you have.
- **I keep my projects, but I stop hanging all my happiness on them: to act toward a goal, without promising myself I will reach it.** : You keep the momentum while loosening your grip: to aim, without making your peace depend on arrival.
- **To risk nothing because all can fail would be to undertake nothing. A life without a project, sheltered from fortune, is no longer a life.** : You accept the fragility of the project: the worth of a life strained toward a goal is worth the uncertainty that comes with it.
