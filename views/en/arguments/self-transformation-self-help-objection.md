# Self-help does it better

*Objection to [The point of philosophy](../axes/metaphilosophy.md) : Transforming oneself — attacks the position*

**If philosophy is an art of living, it has heirs that outdo it on its own ground: for many concrete difficulties, evaluated psychotherapies and personal development do better, faster, for more people.**

*Seligman (positive psychology), *Authentic Happiness*, 2002*

## The argument

1. The ambition to live better has changed address, and it has spread across very unequal standards: proven psychotherapies for diagnosed disorders, largely unregulated coaching for performance, self-help of variable quality for the everyday run of life. They are not all worth the same, but each opens a door the schools of wisdom no longer open.
2. The most rigorous is **cognitive-behavioral therapy**, and its case is striking: [Epictetus](../philosophers/epictetus.md) already wrote “men are disturbed not by things, but by the judgments they pass on things”, and its founders, Albert Ellis and then Aaron Beck, claimed him explicitly. The idea, once isolated and equipped, let itself be turned into a protocol one evaluates, compares and teaches: what ancient wisdom offered to a few, this lineage has made testable.
3. Philosophy has its exercises and even its handbooks, but they commit you to an entire reorientation of existence, backed by texts that demand time and background. Whoever seeks targeted relief will find shorter routes: graduated methods, fitted to a precise difficulty, anxiety, grief, confidence, attention, and whose effect can at least be measured.

**⇒** So, for whoever wants above all to feel better on a precise point, the medicine of the soul has found more convenient, and sometimes better-tested, practitioners: keeping the word 'wisdom' then looks like nostalgia, and seeking what works seems to lead out of philosophy.

## Ways out of the objection

- **True: to feel better, evaluated methods win. If philosophy keeps any worth, it is as knowledge and examination, not as therapy.** : You separate care from thought: to the manual, well-being; to philosophy, lucidity. The Ancients would have refused that split; yours to see whether it holds.
- **I gladly borrow these tools, but they optimize a life whose ends they never question: philosophy begins where the manual stops, when I ask what deserves to be pursued.** : The sharpest dividing line: personal development helps you reach your goals; philosophy examines whether they are the right ones.
- **The comparison misses the essential: even the therapies that touch on values and life projects adjust the individual to their own ends, without putting them in question, and that is their office; the spiritual exercises of the Ancients aimed at something else, an entire conversion of one's gaze, grounded in an idea of the true and the good. One relieves, the other questions the ends; and the extracted recipes lose part of what grounded them.** : The defense of the art of living: without an examination of ends, techniques serve any master, the injunctions of the moment included.
