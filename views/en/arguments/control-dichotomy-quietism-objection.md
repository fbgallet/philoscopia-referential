# The charge of resignation

*Objection to [Scope of action](../axes/control-scope.md) : What is up to us*

**Focusing on oneself can lead to accepting what ought to be changed.**

## The argument

1. The [dichotomy of control](../glossary/dichotomy-of-control.md) places our judgments on one side and almost everything else on the other: illness, poverty, injustice, the social order. All of that, it invites us to take in without letting it disturb our peace.
2. Yet many of these so-called inevitabilities are nothing of the sort: illness recedes before medicine, destitution before action, injustice before those who fight it. To deem them beyond our power in advance is sometimes to be mistaken about that power.
3. The risk is then moral as much as practical: a wisdom that soothes by turning its gaze from what could be corrected looks like a consolation for those who give up. [Marx](../philosophers/marx.md) put it in a single stroke: philosophers have only interpreted the world; the point is to change it.

**⇒** So the dichotomy of control, by drawing the line of the inevitable too soon, risks christening as wisdom what is only resignation.

## Ways out of the objection

- **The objection lands: many limits I thought I had to endure can in fact be pushed back by effort.** : You shift the boundary: what you filed under the inevitable, you hand back to action.
- **I keep the dichotomy, but I widen what depends on us: to act where it is possible, and accept only the irremediable.** : From a fixed boundary you move to a shifting one: already the path of discernment.
- **I refute it: focusing on oneself does not forbid acting; the Stoic plays his part, but without hanging his peace on the outcome.** : You separate effort from attachment to the result: one can act fully without leaving one's serenity to chance.
