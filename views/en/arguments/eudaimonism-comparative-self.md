# Realizing yourself against others

*Objection to [The highest good](../axes/sovereign-good.md) : Eudaimonism — attacks the position*

**To fulfill yourself through success is to never be enough: you no longer realize yourself except in the ranking, against the defeat of others.**

*[Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md), Alain Ehrenberg, *Discourse on the Origin of Inequality; The Weariness of the Self**

## The argument

1. The path of fulfillment makes the good the full unfolding of oneself: developing one's talents, carrying out one's projects, becoming all one can be.
2. But in sport as elsewhere, this fulfillment is soon measured by success: by the time, the rank, the medal. And rank knows no rest, the record broken becomes the new floor, and there will always be someone stronger. You no longer realize yourself, you compare yourself.
3. To compare yourself is to hang your worth on the gaze and the defeat of others. [Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md) named [amour-propre](../glossary/amour-de-soi-et-amour-propre.md) this passion to be worth more than one's neighbor, which he set against amour de soi, the simple concern to live. It knows no satiety, for its object is not a good to reach, but a gap to maintain.
4. Fulfillment then turns into an [endless race](../glossary/culte-de-la-performance.md): no longer unfolding what one is, but forever proving one's worth, under the gaze of a judge, oneself or others, whom nothing contents.

**⇒** So to make success into self-fulfillment is to trade an end one reaches for a comparison that always recedes: you do not realize yourself, you wear yourself out not to fall behind.

## Deepenings

### Amour de soi, amour-propre

*Rousseau distinguishes two loves of self: one wants to live and preserve itself, the other wants to prevail over others; the first can be contented, the second, always comparative, never.*

1. Amour de soi, says [Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md), is the natural concern for one's own preservation: it is governed by one's needs and settles once they are met.
2. Amour-propre, by contrast, is born of life in society: it compares itself only to others and enjoys only surpassing them. That is why it is insatiable, for there is no rank so high that another cannot contest it.

*[Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md), *Discourse on the Origin of Inequality*, 1755*

## Ways out of the objection

- **True: measuring my fulfillment by my rank never ends. I might as well run for the joy of running, and stop comparing myself.** : You leave success for the [pleasure](../glossary/hedonism.md) of the activity itself: the worth is in the doing, not in the result.
- **I still aim at fulfilling myself, but I grant it: it is worth something only once it stops depending on the ranking and the gaze of others.** : You distinguish realizing yourself from comparing yourself: unfolding your powers for yourself, not against others.
- **Comparing yourself is not a disease, it is a spur. Without rivals of my measure, I would know neither what I am worth nor how far I can go.** : You hold for the [agôn](../glossary/agon.md): the ruled rivalry that reveals the best in oneself, rather than the amour-propre that estranges it.
