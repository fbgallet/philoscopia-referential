# The impossibility-of-self-creation argument

*Argument for [Freedom of the will](../axes/freedom.md) : Hard determinism*

**To be the ultimate author of my choices, I would have to be the [cause of myself](../glossary/causa-sui.md).**

*[Galen Strawson](../philosophers/galen-strawson.md), *The Impossibility of Moral Responsibility*, 1994*

## The argument

1. My choices flow from what I am: my character, my desires, the reasons that weigh on me.
2. But what I am, I did not posit out of nothing: it comes from my heredity, my history, circumstances I did not choose.
3. To be its ultimate author, I would have had to create myself, hence to exist before existing: the regress has no end.

**⇒** Since no one is the cause of themselves, no one is the ultimate author of their choices.
