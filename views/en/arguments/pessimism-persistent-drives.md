# The crooked timber of humanity

*Argument for [Vision of humanity](../axes/human-vision.md) : Anthropological pessimism*

**Selfishness, amour-propre, and aggression never quite fade: laws and morality are dams, not cures.**

*[Freud](../philosophers/freud.md), [Hobbes](../philosophers/hobbes.md), [Pascal](../philosophers/pascal.md), *[Civilization and Its Discontents](../works/civilization-and-its-discontents-freud.md)*, 1930*

## The argument

1. Look at what men do the moment constraint and the gaze of others fall away: pillage, cruelty, war reappear at every collapse of order. What resurfaces so constantly is no accident of history.
2. Even our best conduct is undermined from within: beneath generosity, the need to be loved or admired; beneath displayed virtue, an [amour-propre](../axes/self-interest.md) seeking itself. The dark part is not outside, it inhabits us.
3. So the dams that hold us, laws, mores, institutions, do not cure this nature: they contain it. Let them loosen, and what they held back returns.

**⇒** So man is not an angel whom society brought low, but a 'crooked timber' that nothing quite straightens: a livable common life demands reckoning with this depth, not betting on a primal goodness.
