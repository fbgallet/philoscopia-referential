# Science has taken over

*Objection to [The point of philosophy](../axes/metaphilosophy.md) : Understanding the world — attacks the position*

**Philosophy set out to understand the world; the sciences now do that in its place, domain after domain, and all that is left to it is what no method has yet conquered.**

*Hawking and Mlodinow, *The Grand Design*, 2010*

## The argument

1. Philosophy began as the **whole of knowledge**: nature, the soul, the city all fell to it. Yet every time one of its questions found a method of empirical inquiry, it became a science and left its fold: physics in the seventeenth century, then chemistry, biology, psychology, sociology.
2. The movement continues before our eyes: the mind, once questioned by metaphysics, passes to neuroscience and cognitive science; human nature, to anthropology and evolutionary biology; morality itself becomes a matter of empirical inquiry. The 'great questions' are carved up into problems that laboratories investigate.
3. What remains to philosophy are the questions that have no method yet, or never will: less a domain than a remainder. “Philosophy is dead”, a contemporary physicist rules; the phrase is polemical, but the observation it leans on is simple: philosophy has allegedly not kept up with modern developments in science, and on knowledge of the world, the torch has changed hands.

**⇒** So, for the **explanation of the world**, whoever wants to understand does science, or reads it: the history of philosophy's claim to knowledge would be one of a long dispossession, and to cling to it would be to mistake a discipline for its past.

## Ways out of the objection

- **True: understanding the world has become the business of the sciences. If philosophy still serves, it is elsewhere: clarifying our ideas, or learning how to live.** : You cede the terrain of knowledge: the move of much of twentieth-century philosophy, falling back on analysis or on existence.
- **The sciences explain each thing, but none of them says what its results mean or how they hold together: gathering, interpreting, connecting remains a work of thought.** : Understanding then changes meaning: not competing with the sciences, but seeking the overview that none of them, taken alone, provides.
- **The objection describes a real history, but draws too much from it: that the sciences have conquered empirical explanation does not say who will examine their presuppositions, their concepts and their ends. When a physicist debates what makes a good theory, he is already doing epistemology; when he declares philosophy dead, he is doing metaphysics without knowing it. Philosophy does not rival the sciences: it works where experiment does not settle things, foundations, meaning, norms.** : [Husserl](../philosophers/husserl.md)'s reply, and his heirs', to [scientism](../glossary/scientism.md): science does not ground itself, and the physicist who buries philosophy is doing philosophy as he speaks.
