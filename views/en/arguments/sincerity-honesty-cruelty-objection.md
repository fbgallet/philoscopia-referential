# Honesty without regard

*Objection to [Sincerity](../axes/sincerity.md) : Truthful rigorism*

**A truth that spares no one is not a virtue, but a hardness.**

## The argument

1. Telling the whole truth in every circumstance can inflict a pain that nothing warrants: on someone dying, on a child, on anyone who can no longer change their lot.
2. To hold to the principle when frankness only wounds is to prefer the purity of a rule to the very person it is meant to serve.
3. Yet **compassion** ranks among our duties as much as **truthfulness**; to set them against each other without remainder is to sacrifice one to the other without admitting it.

**⇒** An honesty that allows itself no tact ends up confusing uprightness with indifference.

## Ways out of the objection

- **The objection lands: out of regard for others, I may at times soften a truth, or leave it unsaid.** : You grant that truthfulness is not the only duty: kindness can, on occasion, take precedence.
- **I keep the duty of truth, but I choose the moment and the words, without ever lying for all that.** : You separate the lie, which is ruled out, from mere tact: one can say everything without hammering everything home.
- **I refute it: to spare the truth is already to begin betraying it; a frankness that wounds is worth more than a kindness that deceives.** : This is [Kant](../philosophers/kant.md)'s firmness: the first exception allowed opens the door to all the rest.
