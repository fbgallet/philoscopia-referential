# When they collide

*Objection to [Partiality](../axes/partiality.md) : Concentric circles*

**When principle and bond collide, your position must say which prevails.**

## The argument

1. A scholarship: impartial justice gives it to the most deserving stranger, your heart wants it for your niece.
2. There, the middle way must decide. If equality always prevails, you are in truth an impartialist, and your circles mere landmarks.
3. If the bond always prevails, you are in truth a partialist. The position of the circles seems to dissolve at the very place it was expected to hold: at the point of conflict.

**⇒** At the moment of choice, the middle way should therefore tip to one side, and so reveal the camp it belonged to all along.

## Ways out of the objection

- **You are right: at the point of conflict, equality should prevail; my circles are only convenient landmarks.** : The conflict places you on the impartialist side: the rule always prevails.
- **On the contrary: when it comes to deciding, it is my own that I choose; the impartial foundation does not weigh much.** : The conflict places you on the partialist side: the bond always prevails.
- **I refute it: the conflict does not dissolve the position, it sharpens it; the rule sets the bounds, and within them the bond decides.** : You answer that impartiality draws uncrossable limits, and that within them partiality has its rights.
