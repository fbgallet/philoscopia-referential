# Why revolt?

*Objection to [The meaning of life](../axes/life-meaning.md) : Absurdism — attacks the position*

**If nothing really has value, the rebel's lucidity and pride have none either: to make them the 'right' answer is already to reintroduce a meaning.**

*[Nagel](../philosophers/nagel.md), *The Absurd*, 1971*

## The argument

1. The absurd arises, we are told, from the world's silence before our thirst for meaning; and the right response would be lucid revolt, without hope or resignation.
2. But why 'revolt' rather than indifference, or renunciation? If nothing really has value, the rebel's lucidity and pride have no more of it: to set them up as the right response is already to hold something better, and so to reintroduce a meaning.
3. The absurdist is thus caught between two terms: either these values hold, and the world is not the desert of meaning he claimed; or they do not, and his revolt is as futile as what it claims to defy.

**⇒** So absurdism contradicts itself: it proclaims the absence of meaning while making revolt a meaning. One must either recognize values, and leave the absurd, or renounce even revolt itself.

## Ways out of the objection

- **The objection lands: if I care about lucidity, about revolt, then these things matter to me. So better to own it and build a meaning, rather than deny one while living by it.** : You leave the absurd upward, toward constructed meaning: what the revolt affirmed by implication, better to affirm outright.
- **I keep the absurd, but lower the emphasis on revolt: no heroism, just the quiet lucidity of one who lives without an answer, without making it a virtue.** : You hold [Nagel](../philosophers/nagel.md)'s reply: to the absurd, a touch of irony suits better than the pathos of revolt, without leaving the absurd for all that.
- **I refute it: revolt is not one more value, it is the only honest attitude once one has seen both the demand and the silence. To hold it is not to deny the absurd, it is to live it without cheating.** : You hold [Camus](../philosophers/camus.md)'s reply: revolt is not a hidden meaning but the very consciousness of the absurd, kept up without rest.
