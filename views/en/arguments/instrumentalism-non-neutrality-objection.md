# No tool is neutral

*Objection to [Relation to technology](../axes/technology.md) : Instrumentalism*

**An object designed for a certain behaviour does not wait for use: it already inclines it.**

## The argument

1. To say « everything depends on the use » assumes the tool is an indifferent means, equally available for better or worse. That is true of a hammer, which lies in the drawer asking nothing of you.
2. But the feed does not wait in the drawer: endless scrolling, notifications, unpredictable rewards, everything in it is calculated to keep you there. It is not a neutral means you pick up, it is a device that solicits, steers, and makes one behaviour easier than the rest.
3. An object imposes nothing, granted, but it disposes. The slide invites you to slide down, the bench to sit, the feed to keep scrolling. To believe use entirely free is to forget that the very form of the tool has already steered behaviour before one uses it.

**⇒** So « it's not the tool, it's the use » is too quick: the line between tool and use is precisely what the object is built to move.

## Ways out of the objection

- **The objection lands: a device built to hold me is not a neutral instrument among others.** : You grant the object a share of initiative that the idea of a neutral tool denied it.
- **I refine my position: the tool inclines, but it is still up to me to yield or not; knowing its slope does not excuse me from answering for it.** : You keep use at the centre, taking the object's slope as a fact to be reckoned with.
- **I refute it: a slide, a bench, a feed, none of these decides in my place; the object proposes, I dispose.** : You maintain the user's sovereignty: an object's slope is never more than an invitation one can decline.
