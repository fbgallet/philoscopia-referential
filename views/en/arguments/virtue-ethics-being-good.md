# Who to be, before what to do

*Argument for [Criterion of right action](../axes/ethics-foundation.md) : Virtue ethics*

**What makes an action truly good is not having chosen to follow a rule, but having become the kind of person who sees and does what the situation calls for.**

*[Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md), *[Nicomachean Ethics](../works/nicomachean-ethics-aristotle.md)**

## The argument

1. Following a rule does not suffice to make an action good for certain: one can know it and apply it grudgingly, out of interest, or at the wrong moment. The occasionally just man is not yet the just man.
2. What makes a person act well consistently is a **lasting disposition** of character: the one who has become just does the just thing regularly, gladly, and for its own sake, without consulting a rule each time.
3. And this disposition, unlike a rule fixed in advance, stays attentive to what is particular in each situation: the person endowed with [practical wisdom](../glossary/phronesis.md) perceives what this precise case demands, where a general rule, unable to foresee everything, must sometimes betray it.

**⇒** So the ground of right action is a good character, not the observance of a rule or the calculation of effects: to act well is to have become the one who sees and does what the moment demands.
