# The slope of favoritism

*Objection to [Partiality](../axes/partiality.md) : Partialism*

**If the bond is enough to justify, it also excuses the clan against the outsider.**

## The argument

1. The impulse that makes me put my own first feeds, on a larger scale, nepotism, the clan spirit, the closing of the door to the outsider.
2. If the bond is by itself a sufficient reason, what stops me from preferring my tribe to humanity, and shutting my door on whoever does not belong to it?
3. Many injustices come not from neglecting one's own, but from the opposite excess: loyalty to one's own raised into the only law.

**⇒** A partiality with no counterweight thus opens the way to the most unjust preferences, for lack of a principle to bound it.

## Ways out of the objection

- **The objection lands: if the bond excuses everything, there must be a principle of equality above it.** : You acknowledge that above the bonds an impartial demand must keep watch.
- **I hold to my duties toward my own, but I submit them to a wider demand for equality.** : You house partiality within an impartial frame: the path of concentric circles.
- **I refute it: loving one's own is not favoritism; injustice is nepotism, the abuse of a public power, not love of those close to us.** : You distinguish legitimate loyalty from the misuse of a power that is not ours to give.
