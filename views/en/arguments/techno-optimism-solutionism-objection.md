# Solutionism

*Objection to [Relation to technology](../axes/technology.md) : Techno-optimism*

**Expecting technology to repair what it damages forgets that it sometimes lives off the damage.**

## The argument

1. The optimist treats technology's ills as passing defects: to every problem, more ingenuity, a better setting, a smarter app. This is **solutionism**, the faith that every technical problem has a technical solution.
2. But the feed that held you tonight is not a clumsy tool to be perfected: it does exactly what it was designed to do. Holding you is not its flaw, it is its trade, for it is on your attention that those who build it make their living.
3. To hope, then, that the industry thriving on this mechanism will correct it of its own accord is to set the fox to guard the henhouse. Improvement may come, but not from refining the tool alone.

**⇒** So the optimist is mistaken in filing under malfunctions what is, in truth, the machine's very purpose.

## Ways out of the objection

- **The objection lands: when a device is built to hold me, expecting it to fix itself is naive.** : You don't give up on technology's promises, but you stop expecting it to cure its own excesses.
- **I keep my confidence, but I shift it: progress will come from rules and countervailing powers, not from the makers' goodwill alone.** : You keep hoping for better, but you entrust it to law and to collective action rather than to the tool alone.
- **I refute it: every new tool frightened people at first, we learn to use it, and the balance stays largely positive.** : You take the present trouble for a time of learning, as there was for the printing press or the telephone.
