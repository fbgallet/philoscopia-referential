# An opinion on everything, knowledge of nothing

*Objection to [The point of philosophy](../axes/metaphilosophy.md) : Changing the world — attacks the position*

**If philosophy is judged by its action on the world, its hour has passed: those who truly transform it are elsewhere, and the engaged philosopher has become a commentator.**

*[Plato](../philosophers/plato.md), *[Gorgias](../works/gorgias-plato.md)**

## The argument

1. Who transforms the world today, and through which lever? **Technical expertise** designs the means, **institutional power** decides, **organized collective action** shifts the balance of forces. The philosopher may be learned, in history, in logic, even in law; but he masters none of these three levers, where things are actually decided.
2. When he nevertheless wants to bear on public life, he becomes a media figure: war on Monday, school on Wednesday, artificial intelligence on Friday. It is the charge [Plato](../philosophers/plato.md) brought against rhetoric, producing persuasion without knowledge; the modern commentator falls back into it, reigning over [opinion](../glossary/doxa.md) while celebrity does the work of competence.
3. Whoever refuses that role fares no better: his social critique stays in journals read by his peers. Between the TV set and the seminar room, where is the promised transformation? The weapons of criticism have, on their own, almost never sufficed to overturn an order.

**⇒** So judging philosophy by what it changes would amount to condemning it: the world would transform itself without it, and its 'engagement' would swing between commentary and inaudibility.

## Ways out of the objection

- **True: change comes through specialized knowledge and concrete struggles. Philosophy serves better upstream, understanding and clarifying, than pretending to guide action.** : You leave the leading role to others: thought prepares, others transform. It remains not to leave the upstream without effect downstream.
- **To transform is not to comment: philosophy acts by arming public debate, by forming those who act, by carrying the question of ends where every expert sees only means. Its action is slow, but real: philosophical ideas have become rights and institutions.** : Engagement redefined: not an opinion on everything, but the work of concepts that end up, at the scale of generations, in constitutions.
- **The objection mistakes the caricature for the thing: the TV philosopher is to philosophy what the astrologer is to astronomy. It is precisely because each expert sees only his own domain that a thinking of common ends is needed: justice, the use of our knowledge, the city. No one else does that work.** : The defense of engagement: the media scattering is a pathology, not the essence; the thinking of ends has no replacement specialist.
