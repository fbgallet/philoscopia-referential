# Care can be unjust

*Objection to [Criterion of right action](../axes/ethics-foundation.md) : Ethics of care — attacks the position*

**Care favors those close to us: with no impartial safeguard, it can turn into favoritism, blind to the stranger and the distant.**

## The argument

1. Care attends to those close to us, to the one in our charge: that is its strength, but also its natural slope.
2. With no impartial criterion to correct it, care can become favoritism: I look after my own, and the stranger, the distant, the one I do not love cease to count. Many injustices thus dress themselves in the colors of love.
3. Yet justice requires precisely that we take into account also those who do not move us: it is impartiality, which care rejects, that ensures we do not sacrifice the absent to the present.

**⇒** So care alone does not suffice for morality: it needs the impartial standpoint that duty or calculation provide, on pain of being merely a preference for one's own.

## Ways out of the objection

- **True: my concern for those close to me cannot be the whole of morality, or the distant would never count. There must be an impartial principle that weighs those I do not see too.** : You bring back the impartiality of duty or calculation as a corrective: care orients, but justice arbitrates.
- **I keep the primacy of care, but give it an internal demand: to care well is also to widen the circle of one's concern, not to shut oneself in with one's own.** : You hold the reply of a matured care: rightly understood, care extends, from the near to the distant, without denying itself into an abstract principle.
- **I refute it: cold impartiality is precisely what has permitted so many injustices 'on principle'. It is not care that must be corrected by the rule, but the rule that must be brought back to care.** : You reverse the charge: great injustices have often been done in the name of impartial principles; care is the safeguard, not the danger.
