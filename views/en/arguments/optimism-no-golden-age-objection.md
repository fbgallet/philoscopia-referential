# No golden age

*Objection to [Vision of humanity](../axes/human-vision.md) : Anthropological optimism — attacks the reasoning*

**The state of nature is not a lost paradise but a war: scarcity and the desire to dominate set men against one another before any society.**

*[Hobbes](../philosophers/hobbes.md), *[Leviathan](../works/leviathan-hobbes.md)*, 1651*

## The argument

1. The argument assumes a peaceful state of nature, where man has only self-love and pity.
2. But two men who desire the same scarce thing, with no judge above them, become enemies: distrust, the pursuit of glory, and fear suffice for war, without any corrupting society having to intervene.
3. And the child, before any upbringing, already wants everything for himself, with no pity for the brother he bites: greed and comparison are there from the cradle, not laid down by institutions.

**⇒** So man had no golden age to lose: society does not invent conflict, it contains it. Remove it, and it is the war of all against all that returns, not goodness.

## Ways out of the objection

- **True: without laws or judge, men make war on one another. Conflict is in us, society is only a dam.** : You join [Hobbes](../philosophers/hobbes.md): peace is not a gift of nature but the work of a power that contains war.
- **I grant neither a golden age nor an evil nature: man is capable of both; everything depends on what, in him, is cultivated.** : You slide toward plasticity: neither given goodness nor given wickedness, but a being formed by its conditions.
- **This 'desire to dominate' is nothing natural: it is already amour-propre, a need for comparison that presupposes social life. Hobbes projects onto the state of nature the man of his own time.** : [Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md)'s reply: Hobbes describes social man, not natural man, and takes the effect for the cause.
