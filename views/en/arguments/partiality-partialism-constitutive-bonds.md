# The bonds that bind

*Argument for [Partiality](../axes/partiality.md) : Partialism*

**The bond itself is a reason: I owe my own what I owe to no one else.**

## The argument

1. Moral life is not born in the abstract, but in concrete relationships: a mother's care, a friend's loyalty, the debt to those who raised us.
2. These bonds are not arbitrary preferences; they constitute [real obligations](../glossary/special-obligations.md) that no impartial calculation created and none can erase.
3. A "morality" that treated my child like any child would have lost touch with moral life as it is lived: the [graded concern](../movements/confucianism.md) that [Confucius](../philosophers/confucius.md) placed at the heart of virtue, or the ethics of care, attentive first to those who depend on us.

**⇒** Favoring one's own is therefore not a flaw in morality: it is an essential part of it, where it takes flesh.

## Deepenings

### Graded love against equal love

1. In ancient China, Mozi preached an equal love for all, with no distinction of closeness.
2. Mencius replies that a love without degrees would amount to "disowning one's father", and that virtue begins by honoring one's own before extending, step by step, to the rest of humankind.
