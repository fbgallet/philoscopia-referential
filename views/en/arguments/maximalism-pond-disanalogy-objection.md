# The pond is not the world

*Objection to [Moral demandingness](../axes/moral-demand.md) : Maximalism — attacks the reasoning*

**The argument extends the duty of rescue from the emergency case to the world's misery; but what made the duty obvious, uniqueness, urgency, certainty, vanishes in the global case.**

*Pogge, *World Poverty and Human Rights*, 2002*

## The argument

1. The argument draws from an emergency case, the child drowning before me, a general duty to rescue all the world's destitute.
2. But the two situations differ on what gave the first case its force: at the pond I alone can save, the need is here and now, the act one-off and its effect certain. The world's misery is permanent, the business of billions of agents, and the effect of my donation uncertain.
3. What made the duty obvious, uniqueness, urgency, certainty, vanishes in the global case: to carry the same obligation over is to treat a lasting condition as a string of emergencies, and to lay on the individual what belongs to collective action.

**⇒** So from the pond one cannot conclude to an unlimited individual duty: the true answer to the world's misery is a matter of justice and institutions, not each person's endless sacrifice.

## Ways out of the objection

- **True: the global case is not the pond. My strict duty remains not to harm and to rescue in emergencies; the rest belongs to institutions.** : You return toward minimalism, completed by a duty of justice: reforming structures rather than requiring individual sacrifice.
- **I keep a personal duty of aid, my fair share, but the bulk of the task falls to collective action and institutions.** : The 'fair share' path: each owes their contribution, no one owes to carry the world alone.
- **These differences worsen the obligation rather than lessen it: that others fail to help does not excuse me, and uncertainty is corrected by giving to the most effective causes.** : The maximalist reply, that of effective altruism: others' inaction and uncertainty are parameters of the calculation, not exemptions.
