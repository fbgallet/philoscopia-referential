# Prisoners of our own forms?

*Objection to [Source of knowledge](../axes/knowledge-source.md) : Criticism — attacks the position*

**If the mind gives form to everything that appears to it, we never know things as they are, but only as they appear to us.**

## The argument

1. Criticism grants that space, time and causality are not in things but are forms the mind imposes on every possible experience.
2. But then everything we know has already passed through that filter: we reach the phenomenon, the thing as it appears to us, never the thing in itself, as it would be independently of our forms.
3. Knowledge gains in rigor what it loses in reach: it holds for the world of our experience, but locks us within our own frames, without ever giving us the real as it is.

**⇒** So the synthesis has a price: either one accepts knowing only appearances, or one seeks to reach the real otherwise, on the side of what the senses give or of what reason grasps.

## Ways out of the objection

- **The objection convinces me: these 'forms of the mind' may be one construction too many. Better to hold to what experience gives, without adding a priori frames.** : You lean toward empiricism: trusting the sensory given rather than imposed forms, even at the cost of the necessity Kant meant to save.
- **I keep the synthesis, but lower its ambition: let us say the mind organizes experience, without claiming to settle how things stand beyond our forms.** : You hold a modest criticism: keeping the frames as conditions of experience, without making them a metaphysics of the thing in itself.
- **I refute it: knowing phenomena is no fallback. 'Things as they are, independently of any possible experience' is an empty idea; the only real worth speaking of is the one the mind and the senses build together.** : You hold [Kant](../philosophers/kant.md)'s reply: the thing in itself is not a missed object but a limit of thought; knowledge of phenomena is genuine knowledge.
