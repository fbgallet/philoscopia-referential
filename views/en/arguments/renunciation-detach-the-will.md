# Drying up the source of torment

*Argument for [The meaning of life](../axes/life-meaning.md) : Renunciation*

**We suffer not because life has no meaning, but from demanding one of it: peace comes when we cease to require of it what it cannot give.**

*[Schopenhauer](../philosophers/schopenhauer.md), *The World as Will and Representation, Book IV*, 1818*

## The argument

1. The torment comes not from life lacking meaning, but from our demanding of it a meaning and a happiness it does not contain: it is insatiable willing that suffers, never satisfied.
2. For the pursuit produces only an endless swing between lack and boredom: scarcely is one desire satisfied than another is born, and satisfaction is but a respite before the next torment.
3. True peace is therefore gained not by satisfying one more desire, but by quieting the will itself: ceasing to demand of existence what it cannot give, and letting that thirst, which otherwise is reborn without end, come undone.

**⇒** So, faced with the enigma, the lucid response is neither to decipher a meaning, nor to create one, nor to revolt, but to renounce: not to extinguish this or that desire, but to loosen the grip of the will itself, for a peace without illusion.
