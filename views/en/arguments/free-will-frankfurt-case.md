# The Frankfurt case

*Objection to [Freedom of the will](../axes/freedom.md) : Free will*

**One can be responsible for an act one could not have failed to do.**

*[Harry Frankfurt](../philosophers/frankfurt.md), *Alternate Possibilities and Moral Responsibility*, 1969*

## The argument

1. Imagine a neurosurgeon who wants you to make a certain choice. He implants in your brain a device able to force that choice, but set to fire only if you were about to choose otherwise.
2. When the moment comes, you make that choice on your own: the device stays inert, it never intervenes.
3. Yet you could not have done otherwise, the device would have prevented it. And nonetheless, having acted of your own accord, you are indeed the responsible author of your act.

**⇒** So responsibility does not require the power to [do otherwise](../glossary/alternate-possibilities.md): the argument that grounded free will on responsibility no longer holds.

## Deepenings

### The "flicker" of freedom

1. It has been replied that an alternative remains: you still kept at least the power to begin to choose otherwise, which would have triggered the device.
2. But this "flicker" is very thin to bear the whole of responsibility: can a flutter at once short-circuited really be said to ground our merit or our fault?

## Ways out of the objection

- **You grant it: if responsibility needs no alternatives, my freedom need not be that power.** : This is the path of [compatibilism](../glossary/compatibilism.md): to be free would be to act of one's own accord, without needing the power to do otherwise.
- **I keep free will, but I admit responsibility is not its proof.** : You decouple the two: one may believe in a real freedom without resting it on the responsibility argument.
- **I refute it: the device that stayed inactive proves nothing; an alternative, however slight, remained open.** : This is the "flicker" reply: as long as a genuine power to [act otherwise](../glossary/alternate-possibilities.md) survives, the case does not bite.
