# Nothing to discover, everything to make

*Argument for [The meaning of life](../axes/life-meaning.md) : Constructed meaning*

**No meaning is inscribed in things in advance: it is for each of us to create our own, through our choices and commitments.**

*[Sartre](../philosophers/sartre.md), *Existentialism Is a Humanism*, 1946*

## The argument

1. No meaning is inscribed in things in advance: the world is there, with no instructions; we exist first, with no essence or end given to us.
2. But this absence is not a lack: it is our freedom. Since nothing dictates what my life should be, it is for me to decide, through my choices and commitments.
3. Meaning is therefore not found but made: it arises from what I devote myself to, and this commitment makes me fully responsible for what I become, with no received order to unload it onto.

**⇒** So there is nothing to discover and everything to make: the meaning of a life is what each creates through their projects, not an order they undergo.
