# A life is not a heap of instants

*Objection to [Temporal orientation](../axes/time-orientation.md) : Living in the present*

**A human life is not a sequence of instants but a story: without past or future, it comes undone.**

## The argument

1. Grant that only the instant is real. It remains that a human life is not lived as a series of unconnected points: it is understood as a story, with a past it answers for and a future toward which it strains.
2. Everything that makes an existence worthwhile presupposes it. A promise binds a future self; love is measured by its duration; a character, a work, a friendship are built over time. To reduce life to the instant is to dissolve the [person](../glossary/personal-identity.md) herself, who is someone only because she holds together across time.
3. The consistent presentist would have to give up promising anything, counting on tomorrow, recognizing himself in yesterday. But no one lives that way: even the one who preaches living in the moment makes plans, keeps loyalties, remembers.

**⇒** So the pure present is not enough to carry a life: there must be a thread linking the instants, and that thread is already to project oneself and to remember.

## Deepenings

### Who keeps a promise?

1. To promise is to bind in advance a self I am not yet: today I answer for what I will do tomorrow. [Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md) saw in man the animal « entitled to make promises », not because he lives in the instant, but because he goes beyond it, holding himself the same from one end of his word to the other.
2. Without this continuity, neither promise nor commitment would have any meaning: what makes a word worth anything is precisely that it spans time.

*[Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md), *[On the Genealogy of Morality](../works/on-the-genealogy-of-morality-nietzsche.md)**

## Ways out of the objection

- **That is right: a life is a story, not a dust of instants. What matters is built over time, and so it projects ahead.** : You pass from the present to the project: if a life holds together by a thread, then to live is to strain toward what one wants to make of it.
- **I keep the present as the place of life, but I give it back a thread: one can inhabit the instant without giving up a story.** : The present remains the only time lived, but run through by a memory and a purpose that keep it from coming undone.
- **The story I tell myself is itself made of present instants: remembering, promising, these are acts of today. The thread never leaves the present.** : Past and future exist only as presents, the ones in which we remember and foresee. To live the instant is also to hold one's story within it.
