# Man is good, society corrupts him

*Argument for [Vision of humanity](../axes/human-vision.md) : Anthropological optimism*

**Man is born good; it is social inequalities and rivalries that awaken wickedness in him.**

*[Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md), *[Discourse on the Origin of Inequality](../works/discourse-on-inequality-rousseau.md)*, 1755*

## The argument

1. In the state of nature, before society, man has only two springs: self-love, the care of his own preservation, and pity, the reluctance to see a fellow creature suffer. Neither of the two makes him wicked: pity, on the contrary, holds him back from harming others.
2. Wickedness, envy, cruelty, the thirst to dominate, presupposes comparison: one must measure oneself against others, wish to be preferred, in order to wish them harm. Now this comparison, **amour-propre**, arises with social life, property, inequality.
3. Where one thinks one sees an evil nature, one is really seeing the effect of a history: unjust institutions have set men against one another.

**⇒** So evil is not in the depths of man but in the society that has disfigured him: to make men better is to [reform the conditions](../axes/political-ideal.md) that corrupt them, not to tame a perverse nature.
