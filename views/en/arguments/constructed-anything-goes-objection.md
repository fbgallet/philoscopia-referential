# Will any meaning do?

*Objection to [The meaning of life](../axes/life-meaning.md) : Constructed meaning — attacks the position*

**If meaning is only my decision, then a life devoted to collecting bottle caps would be 'worth' as much as any other. Yet we refuse to believe it.**

*[Susan Wolf](../philosophers/susan-wolf.md), *Meaning in Life and Why It Matters*, 2010*

## The argument

1. If the meaning of a life is what each decides, then any project I devote myself to gives it, by that choice alone, as much meaning as any other.
2. One would then have to say that a life spent collecting bottle caps, or devoted to a hateful cause, is as full of meaning as a life of love or creation, provided it was chosen. Yet we refuse to believe it.
3. This refusal reveals a demand that decision alone does not meet: for a life to have meaning, it is not enough to be attached to something, the thing must also be worth it, independently of what one has decided.

**⇒** So meaning does not reduce to what one decides: it requires that what one devotes oneself to be objectively worth it, and that worth one does not give oneself, one recognizes it.

## Ways out of the objection

- **True: not all lives are equal by what one devotes them to. Meaning presupposes things that are genuinely worth it, not only things one has chosen.** : You reintroduce a worth to be recognized, not decreed: meaning lies in a meeting between what engages us and what, in itself, deserves engagement.
- **I keep that meaning is constructed, but grant that it is not constructed just anyhow: one must still commit to things that matter, not to just anything.** : You hold a constructed but non-arbitrary meaning: the making of meaning meets values that guide it, in [Susan Wolf](../philosophers/susan-wolf.md)'s manner.
- **I refute it: this 'objective worth' that nothing grounds is only a last ghost of given meaning. What counts is the intensity and sincerity of the commitment, not a merit inscribed in things.** : You hold the existentialist line: no value waits to be recognized; it is commitment itself that makes a thing matter.
