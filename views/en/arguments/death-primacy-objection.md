# The primacy-of-death objection

*Objection to [Death](../axes/death.md) : Being-toward-death*

**It is not death, but life and others, that return us to ourselves.**

*[Emmanuel Levinas](../philosophers/levinas.md), *Totality and Infinity*, 1961*

## The argument

1. That the thought of the end can rouse a slumbering existence, granted. But it does not have this power alone: a love, a calling, the birth of a child draw us out of indifference just as much, and put us under summons to answer.
2. Levinas goes further: what truly calls me out of myself is the face of the other, its frailty that makes me responsible, and not my death, the most solitary and most silent of relations.
3. By making death alone the source of [authenticity](../glossary/authenticity.md), one risks the opposite of the goal sought: a withdrawal onto one's own end, where it may be attention to the world that opens a life.

**⇒** So nothing compels us to take death as our only master: life has other paths, perhaps surer ones, to return us to ourselves.

## Deepenings

### The face rather than death

1. For Levinas, the philosophy of being-toward-death remains centered on the self: even in anguish, it brings me back to my own existence.
2. The true departure from self comes from the other, whose call precedes and exceeds me: I find myself responsible even before having chosen to be.

*[Emmanuel Levinas](../philosophers/levinas.md), *Totality and Infinity*, 1961*

## Ways out of the objection

- **True: it is life, those I love, what commits me, that return me to myself, more than the thought of my end.** : You turn your gaze from the end toward the present and those around you: this too is what [Epicurus](../philosophers/epicurus.md) recommends, for whom it is better to cultivate life than to meditate on death.
- **I maintain that death lights up life, but I grant it is not alone in doing so.** : You keep finitude's power to awaken, without granting it a monopoly.
- **I refute it: if these experiences wake us, it is already because our time is counted. Death remains the horizon that gives them their worth.** : A classic reply: finitude is not one revelation among others, it is the condition of them all. This is the heart of [being-toward-death](../glossary/being-toward-death.md).
