# The land ethic

*Argument for [Moral circle](../axes/moral-circle.md) : Ecocentrism*

**A thing is right when it tends to preserve the integrity, stability, and beauty of the community of life; wrong when it tends otherwise.**

*[Leopold](../philosophers/leopold.md), [Næss](../philosophers/naess.md), *A Sand County Almanac, 'The Land Ethic'*, 1949*

## The argument

1. No living thing exists alone: each is what it is only through the whole, soils, waters, plants, animals, on which it depends and which it in turn helps hold together, a **biotic community**.
2. An ethics centered on individuals alone misses this level: it can judge an act toward this animal, not toward a species, a river, an ecosystem, which can flourish or collapse without being sentient individuals.
3. Now it is these wholes that carry all individual life: their integrity is the condition of every particular good, and on that ground holds a worth that the sum of individuals does not capture.

**⇒** So the human is not the master of nature but a [member of the biotic community](../axes/human-place-nature.md): the ultimate good is the health of that whole, of which every being, humans included, is but a part.
