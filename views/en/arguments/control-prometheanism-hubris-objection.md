# Overreach and the world's recoil

*Objection to [Scope of action](../axes/control-scope.md) : Prometheanism*

**By striving to extend your mastery without end, you multiply the occasions of being defeated.**

## The argument

1. The Promethean project measures wisdom by what one manages to change. But there is always more to change: each limit pushed back uncovers another, and the effort never knows rest.
2. Above all, the real has the last word. Illness, grief, aging, death end up foiling the best-laid plans; and the nature we thought we had tamed sometimes reminds, harshly, those who force it, like those antibiotics whose overuse has bred bacteria they no longer cure.
3. Whoever has placed all his peace in the power to transform finds himself without recourse the day he runs up against the untransformable. **Overreach**, what the Greeks called hubris, called down a punishment in their tragedies: not out of superstition, but because it forgets the measure of things.

**⇒** So the ambition to master everything, for want of acknowledging a share of the irreducible, prepares its own defeats and the bitterness that follows them.

## Ways out of the objection

- **The objection lands: it is wiser to focus my efforts on myself than to wear myself out bending the world.** : You reverse the priority: mastery begins within, where it is sure.
- **I keep the drive, but grant it a limit: to act tirelessly on the possible, without denying that there is an irreducible.** : You keep the ambition while making room for the inevitable: the path of discernment.
- **I refute it: what we call « irreducible » has nearly always given way before those who refused to believe in it. Today's measure was yesterday's overreach.** : You hold the limit to be provisional: the history of science and of struggles often proves you right.
