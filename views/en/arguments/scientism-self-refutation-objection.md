# Sawing off the branch

*Objection to [Place of science](../axes/science-place.md) : Scientism — attacks the reasoning*

**'Only science gives knowledge' is not a scientific statement: it is a philosophical claim about science, one its own rule ought to disqualify.**

*[Husserl](../philosophers/husserl.md), *The Crisis of European Sciences*, 1936*

## The argument

1. The argument sets a criterion: only what exposes itself to the public test of facts is knowledge.
2. But the statement 'only science gives knowledge' is itself neither an observation nor a testable theory: it is a philosophical claim about science.
3. By its own criterion, then, it fails: it would have to be filed among the 'opinions' it claims to disqualify. It moreover presupposes a lifeworld, subjects, norms of proof, which science assumes without grounding.

**⇒** So scientism saws off its own branch: to assert it is already to do a philosophy its own rule forbids, hence to acknowledge a knowledge that is not science.

## Ways out of the objection

- **True: stating the worth of science is not science. There is, then, a knowledge, philosophical, that precedes and judges it.** : You join [Husserl](../philosophers/husserl.md): science floats on a 'lifeworld' it presupposes and that another kind of thought must elucidate.
- **I grant that scientism overrates itself, but I maintain that science remains our most reliable knowledge, provided it admits its limits.** : You slide toward critical rationalism: science keeps its authority, but stops claiming to say everything.
- **This 'philosophy' is only a summary of the success of the sciences: an empirical generalization from their record, not a knowledge of another order.** : The naturalist reply: the scientistic thesis means to be the extension of science, not a metaphysics apart.
