# Concepts and intuitions

*Argument for [Source of knowledge](../axes/knowledge-source.md) : Criticism*

**The senses give the matter, the mind the forms that order it: without concepts perceptions are blind, without perceptions concepts are empty.**

*[Kant](../philosophers/kant.md), *[Critique of Pure Reason](../works/critique-of-pure-reason-kant.md)**

## The argument

1. Empiricism is right on one point: without data received from the senses, the mind has nothing to work on. Concepts that no intuition comes to fill remain empty, mere words that know nothing.
2. Rationalism is right on the other: these raw data, without form, would be but a flux without order. It is the mind that supplies space, time, causality, the frames under which alone anything can appear to us. Intuitions that no concept orders remain blind.
3. Knowledge is therefore neither received ready-made from without, nor drawn from reason alone: it arises from the meeting of the two, the matter from the senses, the form from the mind that orders it.

**⇒** So neither experience alone nor reason alone grounds knowledge: to know is to give form, through the mind's frames, to what experience gives us.
