# Proving too much

*Objection to [Relation to technology](../axes/technology.md) : Techno-criticism*

**If no technology is neutral, the charge applies to the book as well, and no longer distinguishes anything.**

## The argument

1. You say no tool is neutral, that every device shapes us. Granted. But then the book shapes us too, and writing, and the cathedral, and conversation governed by politeness: all of it steers attention and transforms our lives.
2. If « not neutral » is said of everything, the word no longer separates the feed that captures you from the library that elevates you. A critique that condemns all technology gives no means of choosing among them.
3. To tell the good device from the bad, one must after all return to what it does to us, to the end it serves, hence to its use and its effects. What mistrust wished to avoid, the examination of uses, it is now forced to return to.

**⇒** So the critique holds only if it becomes discerning: not « all technology alienates us », but « this one, for this reason, does us a disservice ».

## Ways out of the objection

- **That is decisive: condemning technology wholesale distinguishes nothing; one must return to uses and ends.** : You bring the critique back to the ground of use: no longer technology in general, but this device and its effects.
- **I refine it: all technology shapes us, but not equally; some serve our ends, others impose theirs on us.** : You keep your wariness while grading it: non-neutrality admits of degrees, from the book to the feed.
- **I refute it: this is a difference in kind, not in degree; a device built to capture is not a book that asks to be read.** : You draw a clear line: between the tool that demands our effort and the one that exploits our weaknesses, the gap is not a matter of more or less.
