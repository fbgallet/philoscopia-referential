# God as human projection

*Argument for [Existence of God](../axes/god-existence.md) : Atheism*

**It is not God who made man in his image: it is man who made God in his.**

*[Feuerbach](../philosophers/feuerbach.md), [Freud](../philosophers/freud.md), *The Essence of Christianity, chs. I–II*, 1841*

## The argument

1. Consider God's attributes: power, knowledge, goodness, justice, raised to the infinite. They are, trait for trait, human qualities stripped of our limits: man contemplates himself in them, magnified.
2. Now this movement of **projection** is fully explained by human causes: the fear of death, the desire that justice be done, the need for consolation and for a protecting father. No God is needed to understand why humans believe in God.
3. A hypothesis that can be accounted for without supposing it true, and that answers our desires so exactly, loses its credit: what we would have invented from scratch would look precisely like this.

**⇒** So God is not in heaven but in us: a projection of humanity, whose making the history of religions describes.
