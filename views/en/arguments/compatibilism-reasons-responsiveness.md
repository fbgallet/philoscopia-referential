# Freedom as the absence of constraint

*Argument for [Freedom of the will](../axes/freedom.md) : Compatibilism*

**To be free is to act from one's own reasons, not without a cause.**

*[Hume](../philosophers/hume.md), *[An Enquiry Concerning Human Understanding](../works/enquiry-concerning-human-understanding-hume.md)*, 1748*

## The argument

1. Compare the addict, driven by a craving he disowns, and the person who weighs her reasons and then acts accordingly: both have causes, but only the latter acts freely.
2. Freedom is therefore opposed not to the **cause**, but to **constraint**: free is the act that flows from my own will, even if that will is itself determined.
3. Thus freedom and [determinism](../glossary/determinism.md) are reconciled: I am the author of my acts so long as they pass through my deliberation, with no need for a power to escape causes.

**⇒** So one can be free and responsible in a determined world: this is the thesis of [compatibilism](../glossary/compatibilism.md).
