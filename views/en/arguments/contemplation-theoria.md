# Theoria, the highest activity

*Argument for [The point of philosophy](../axes/metaphilosophy.md) : Understanding the world*

**Understanding for its own sake is no luxury: it is the highest activity of which man is capable, and it need serve nothing beyond itself.**

*[Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md), *[Nicomachean Ethics](../works/nicomachean-ethics-aristotle.md)**

## The argument

1. Every other activity aims at an end beyond itself: one heals for the sake of health, one governs for the sake of the city. But each time one can ask 'and that, for the sake of what?'; the chain must stop at what is wanted for its own sake.
2. Now understanding, grasping why things are as they are, is desired for itself alone: 'all men by nature desire to know', and the knowledge that serves nothing else is the one entirely free.
3. It is also the most continuous and most self-sufficient activity, the one that exercises what is highest in us, thought, upon what is highest to be known.

**⇒** So philosophy fulfills its vocation by understanding: its theoria is not an escape but the end to which the rest is a means, the [life according to what is highest](../axes/sovereign-good.md) in us.
