# The great lever

*Argument for [Relation to technology](../axes/technology.md) : Techno-optimism*

**No wisdom has ever pushed back hunger, pain, and death the way our technologies have.**

## The argument

1. Before our tools, human life was short, exposed, weighed down by toil. The plough, the printing press, the vaccine, the engine: each technology widened what a human being can know, do, and hope for. This is the meaning of [Descartes](../philosophers/descartes.md)'s project, which charged knowledge with making us “as masters and possessors of nature”.
2. The feed you were scrolling tonight belongs to this lineage: never has so vast a share of knowledge, of works, of distant voices been within arm's reach. What we call distraction is the flip side of an unheard-of abundance.
3. And every novelty, in its day, caused fear: [Plato](../philosophers/plato.md) already dreaded that writing would ruin memory. Yet we would not trade writing for forgetfulness. The present anxiety is surely, too, the discomfort of a threshold.

**⇒** So to bet on technology is not naivety: it is to recognize the lever that, alone, has kept its promises of emancipation.

## Deepenings

### Writing and memory

1. In the *[Phaedrus](../works/phaedrus-plato.md)*, [Plato](../philosophers/plato.md) has King Thamus say that writing, far from strengthening memory, will let it wither: people will believe they know because they have written it down. The fear was not absurd, yet it turned into an immense gain.
