# The world does not answer

*Objection to [The meaning of life](../axes/life-meaning.md) : Teleology and providence — attacks the position*

**The order we think we decipher, we project onto the world: it gives no sign of an end intended for us.**

*[Camus](../philosophers/camus.md), *[The Myth of Sisyphus](../works/the-myth-of-sisyphus-camus.md)**

## The argument

1. Teleology claims to read, in the order of the world, a design that precedes us and that would give our lives their direction.
2. But that order, the laws, the fitting of living things, is fully explained by blind processes, selection, physics, without any intention having to intervene. One explains the same world without supposing an end.
3. And nowhere does the world show that it has a meaning intended for us: to our demand for meaning it answers with silence. To read a design into things is to project onto them our own need to be awaited somewhere.

**⇒** So the meaning teleology claims to find might well be only a projected one: nothing, in the order of the world, attests to an end destined for us.

## Ways out of the objection

- **The objection lands: I find no sign of a meaning inscribed in things. If there is to be one, I will have to make it, or hold out without it.** : You leave providence for constructed meaning or the lucidity of the absurd: nothing being given, everything is to be made or faced.
- **I keep my trust in an order, but stop claiming to decipher it clearly: it surpasses me, and I trust it without reading its detail.** : You hold a teleology of faith: the order is believed more than read, trust in a meaning that exceeds what we can show of it.
- **I refute it: that science explains the mechanism says nothing about the end. An order so intelligible, in which reason finds itself reflected, still argues for a meaning that precedes us.** : You distinguish the how from the why: explaining the mechanism does not exhaust the question of meaning, and the world's intelligibility remains a clue.
