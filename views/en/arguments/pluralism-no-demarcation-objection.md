# If all are equal, nothing is known

*Objection to [Place of science](../axes/science-place.md) : Epistemic pluralism — attacks the position*

**If no method holds the privilege of telling the truth, what distinguishes knowledge from a shared illusion? Astrology too claims its 'other way of knowing'.**

*[Popper](../philosophers/popper.md), *Conjectures and Refutations*, 1963*

## The argument

1. Pluralism refuses to grant any method the privilege of telling the truth: all accesses to the real would be equal in principle.
2. But then what distinguishes knowledge from a shared illusion? Astrology, conspiracy thinking, quackery also claim their 'other way of knowing'.
3. Without a criterion that exposes a claim to the verdict of the facts, one can no longer correct an error or decide between rival theses: 'all are equal' comes down to 'nothing is known'.

**⇒** So refusing any privilege to the method that exposes itself to the facts threatens to dissolve the very difference between knowledge and belief: one needs at least a criterion that distinguishes what exposes itself to the real from what shields itself from it.

## Ways out of the objection

- **True: without a criterion, anything calls itself 'knowledge'. The method that exposes itself to disproof keeps a privilege nothing equals.** : You return toward scientism, or at least toward the primacy of method: demarcation becomes necessary again.
- **I maintain the plurality of accesses, but grant that a safeguard is needed: what claims truth must be open to being tested.** : You move closer to critical rationalism: plurality remains, under the condition of exposure to the real.
- **Distinguishing knowledge from illusion does not require ONE single method: each domain has its own criteria, art, history, clinical judgment, without bending to that of physics.** : The pluralist reply: standards of rigor are internal to each practice, not imported from natural science alone.
