# The authenticity argument

*Argument for [Death](../axes/death.md) : Living in view of one's death*

**It is by taking on my death that I truly become myself.**

*[Heidegger](../philosophers/heidegger.md), *Being and Time*, 1927*

## The argument

1. In everyday busyness, I mostly live as "one" lives: scattered among tasks, rumors and everyone's opinions, I keep postponing the question of what I truly want.
2. Death, however, cannot be delegated: no one can die in my place, and no one knows when. It is my ownmost possibility, the one that sets me apart and that nothing surpasses.
3. Facing it lucidly, instead of fleeing it, brings me at once back to the essential: this finite time is mine, and it is for me to answer for it, here, now.

**⇒** Therefore facing death is not morbid: it is the condition of an [authentic](../glossary/authenticity.md) existence, chosen rather than undergone.

## Deepenings

### Fleeing into the "they"

1. Heidegger notes that death is everywhere softened: "one dies," we say, as if it were an anonymous and distant fact, never my own death.
2. This flight is reassuring but costly: by hiding my finitude from me, it also robs me of the urgency that could make my life truly mine.

*[Heidegger](../philosophers/heidegger.md), *Being and Time*, 1927*
