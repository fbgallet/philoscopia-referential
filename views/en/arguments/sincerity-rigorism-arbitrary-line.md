# The arbitrary line

*Objection to [Sincerity](../axes/sincerity.md) : Truthful rigorism*

**Between lying and letting someone believe, the line you draw is less clear than it seems.**

## The argument

1. Rigorism forbids the lie, yet permits staying silent, being evasive, letting a false impression settle in.
2. But a calculated silence or a half-truth can deceive just as surely as a lie, and with the same intent to mislead.
3. To save the word this way while absolving the manoeuvre is to preserve the letter of sincerity while sacrificing its spirit.

**⇒** If deceiving by silence is allowed and deceiving by words forbidden, then it is the intent to deceive, and not the lie alone, that ought to be at issue.

## Ways out of the objection

- **You are right: what matters is not wanting to deceive, not the form the deception takes.** : If only the intent weighs, then withholding to spare and lying to spare are on a par: your position moves toward benevolent lying.
- **I grant it in part: I remain opposed to the lie, but I admit a deceptive silence is not innocent.** : You tighten your demand: to be sincere is not only to refrain from lying, it is to refrain from misleading.
- **I refute it: there is a real difference between actively deceiving and not telling all; no one has the right to demand my every thought.** : You hold the line: to stay silent is a matter of discretion, to lie a matter of fault; the two are not equivalent.
