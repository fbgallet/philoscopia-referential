# The self-refutation objection

*Objection to [Relation to certainty](../axes/certainty.md) : Skepticism*

**To assert that nothing can be asserted contradicts itself.**

*Aristotelian tradition*

## The argument

1. The skeptic holds a thesis: "no thesis is certain." But this very thesis, does he hold it as certain?
2. If he holds it as certain, he contradicts himself: here is at least one certainty. If he does not hold it as certain, then it binds us to nothing, and we may go on affirming.
3. Moreover, no one lives their skepticism: to act, to speak, to eat, the skeptic trusts at every instant a thousand beliefs he claims to suspend.

**⇒** Therefore universal doubt cannot be carried through: something is always already taken to be true.

## Deepenings

### The Pyrrhonian reply

1. The consistent skeptic replies that his formula is not one more thesis but a report that "purges itself," like a drug expelled along with the humours it drives out.
2. The practical objection remains: even self-purged, his suspension does not say how to act, and life decides for him what he refuses to decide in thought.

## Ways out of the objection

- **The objection is decisive: if total doubt bites its own tail, then some things must after all be taken as settled.** : You grant reason a foothold it cannot revoke: your position moves toward [dogmatism](../glossary/dogmatism.md).
- **I trade radical doubt for fallibilism: I can know, but always revisably, with no closed certainty.** : This is the middle path of [fallibilism](../glossary/fallibilism.md): giving up general suspension without claiming definitive truths.
- **I refute it: I do not erect skepticism into a dogma, I suspend judgment about it too. The formula purges itself.** : This is Pyrrho's reply: skepticism is not a thesis but a practice of doubt, which does not exempt itself.
