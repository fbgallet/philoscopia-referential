# Explaining nature

**How should natural phenomena be explained: by mechanisms, by purposes, or by a vital impulse?**

*The world · descriptive (theory) · triangular*

Depending on whether we see blind causes, ends being pursued, or a life unfolding, nature becomes a mechanism with no purpose or a world reaching toward something. Behind the method of explanation, what is decided is the presence or absence of meaning in the living world.

## Poles

### Mechanism

Nature is fully explained by material causes and laws of motion, like an immense machine. Living beings themselves are complex mechanisms, with no need to invoke purposes or mysterious forces.

*[Descartes](../philosophers/descartes.md) · [La Mettrie](../philosophers/la-mettrie.md)*

### Finalism

A natural phenomenon is fully understood only by grasping what it occurs for: the organ has a function, the organism tends toward its completed form. Nature is ordered by ends, not merely pushed by causes.

*[Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md) · [Leibniz](../philosophers/leibniz.md)*

### Vitalism

Life cannot be reduced either to mechanics or to a pre-established plan: it is a creative, unpredictable impulse that invents new forms. Understanding the living requires a different approach from that of the physical sciences.

*[Bergson](../philosophers/bergson.md)*

Finalism supports bioconservatism: for [Jonas](../philosophers/jonas.md), finitude and vulnerability are not flaws to be corrected, but conditions of a properly human life.
- *[Explaining nature](../axes/causality.md) : Finalism* grounds *[Essence and existence](../axes/essence.md) : Essentialism* : If each being tends toward an end of its own, it is because a form, an essence, defines it and guides its development. Finalism grounds essentialism: in [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md), the [final cause](../glossary/four-causes.md) and the form coincide, and the nature of a thing is what it is meant to become.
- *[Explaining nature](../axes/causality.md) : Finalism* grounds *[Humanity's place in nature](../axes/human-place-nature.md) : Stewardship* : If nature and the living have their own ends and a worth that our power can destroy, then that very power obliges us to preserve them. Finalism grounds stewardship: [Jonas](../philosophers/jonas.md) draws from nature's vulnerability an unprecedented responsibility, that of keeping the world for those to come.
- *[Explaining nature](../axes/causality.md) : Finalism* grounds *[Relation to technology](../axes/technology.md) : Techno-criticism* : If nature and the living are bearers of their own ends, then the technological will to master and reconfigure everything threatens an order that surpasses us. Finalism grounds techno-criticism: [Jonas](../philosophers/jonas.md) sets against the excessive power of technology a principle of responsibility toward life and future generations.
- *[Nature of reality](../axes/reality-nature.md) : Materialism* grounds *[Explaining nature](../axes/causality.md) : Mechanism* : A reality made only of matter acts solely through contact and the transfer of motion, with no ends or vital forces. Materialism therefore calls for a mechanistic reading of causality, where every effect is explained by its physical antecedents.
- *[Explaining nature](../axes/causality.md) : Mechanism* grounds *[Freedom of the will](../axes/freedom.md) : Hard determinism* : If every physical event is wholly produced by prior causes, then our choices, as events in this world, are too. Mechanism extended to the mind thus grounds [determinism](../glossary/determinism.md) and leaves [free will](../glossary/free-will.md) without an object.
- *[Explaining nature](../axes/causality.md) : Mechanism* grounds *[Humanity's place in nature](../axes/human-place-nature.md) : Mastery* : If nature is but a vast mechanism governed by laws, then to know it is to be able to master it and put it at our service. Mechanism grounds the ideal of mastery: [Descartes](../philosophers/descartes.md) charges science with making us "as masters and possessors of nature", and Bacon makes knowledge a power.
- *[Explaining nature](../axes/causality.md) : Mechanism* grounds *[Enhancing human nature](../axes/human-enhancement.md) : Transhumanism* : If the living body is a machine governed by physical laws, it can in principle be repaired, augmented and reconfigured like any mechanism. The mechanistic view of the living, which [La Mettrie](../philosophers/la-mettrie.md) once turned into "man a machine", feeds the transhumanism of [Bostrom](../philosophers/bostrom.md), for which human nature is not a sacred given but a perfectible state. The link remains a contestable inference: classical mechanism did not aim at enhancement, but it removes the objection to it in principle.
