# Status of emotions

**Are emotions an obstacle to thought or a source of knowledge?**

*The world · descriptive (theory) · bipolar*

Should we distrust what we feel, a disturbance that clouds judgement, or hear in it a fine perception of what really matters? To take a position is to say how far to follow our emotions when they contradict cold reason.

## Poles

### Intellectualism

Emotions cloud judgment: hasty assents or confused stirrings, they distort more often than they reveal what matters. Only reason, by examining them, can sort out what truth they carry.

*the Stoics · [Descartes](../philosophers/descartes.md)*

### Sentimentalism

Emotions are not the enemy of thought: they orient us, motivate us, and inform us about what matters. Reason alone is powerless to judge and to act without the help of feeling.

*[Hume](../philosophers/hume.md) · [Nussbaum](../philosophers/nussbaum.md)*

## Live problems

- **Is an emotion a judgment?** : Is the fear that grips me a thought appraising a danger, as the Stoics and then contemporary cognitivism hold, or a bodily stirring felt before any thought, as William James would have it? What judges can be argued with and corrected; what shakes us can only be tamed.

## Grounding relations

- *[Status of emotions](../axes/emotions.md) : Intellectualism* grounds *[Relation to desire](../axes/desire.md) : Reasoned selection* : If the passions are not blind forces but judgments about what is worth pursuing, then reason can examine and sort them. To hold desire to be a revisable judgment grounds reasoned selection: one satisfies the desires that withstand examination and dismisses those resting only on a confused opinion. This is the shared move of [Epicurus](../philosophers/epicurus.md) and the Stoics.
- *[Status of emotions](../axes/emotions.md) : Sentimentalism* grounds *[Relation to desire](../axes/desire.md) : Affirmation* : If our affects are not obstacles to reason but a way of sensing what matters, desire ceases to be an impulse to correct: it reveals what carries us. To hold emotions to be a source of value rather than a disturbance inclines one to affirm one's desires rather than master them, from Romanticism to a sensitive reading of [Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md).
