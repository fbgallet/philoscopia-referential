# Interiority

**Should we examine our inner life, or fulfill ourselves first and foremost through action?**

*The self · value · bipolar*

Turning inward sheds light on who we are, but can slide into rumination far from the world; throwing oneself into action accomplishes much, but risks dodging the question of who is acting, and for what. To take a position is to say where the self is truly found: in the return to oneself, or in what one does.

## Poles

### The examined life

The unexamined life is not worth living: we must regularly withdraw into ourselves to question our opinions, desires, and actions. Self-knowledge is the condition of a lucid and genuinely free existence.

*[Socrates](../philosophers/socrates.md) · [Montaigne](../philosophers/montaigne.md)*

### Primacy of action

It is through action, commitment, and accomplishment that we discover and shape ourselves, not through introspection. Scrutinizing our inner life too closely risks paralysis, or even inventing imaginary problems: the self reveals itself by acting in the world.

*[Arendt](../philosophers/arendt.md) · [Marx](../philosophers/marx.md)*

## Live problems

- **What to do with one's attention?** : Examining one's life, like acting, demands a resource everything competes for: attention. What we look at, and what we turn from, already shapes an existence; the question is what share may go to diversion.

## Grounding relations

- *[Being or having a body](../axes/embodiment.md) : The lived body* grounds *[Interiority](../axes/interiority.md) : Primacy of action* : If I am not a soul folded in on itself but a living body engaged in the world, then it is by acting, not by examining myself, that I discover and shape myself. The lived-body thesis, which [Merleau-Ponty](../philosophers/merleau-ponty.md) places at the heart of existence, supports the primacy of action: for [Marx](../philosophers/marx.md), the point is less to interpret the world than to transform it.
- *[Nature of the self](../axes/self-nature.md) : Substantialism* grounds *[Interiority](../axes/interiority.md) : The examined life* : If there exists in each of us a substantial self, a soul distinct from the flow of appearances, then knowing oneself becomes the highest task. Substantialism grounds the examined life: from Socrates, for whom an unexamined life is not worth living, to [Augustine](../philosophers/augustine.md), who turns inward to find truth there.
