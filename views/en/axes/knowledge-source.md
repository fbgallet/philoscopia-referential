# Source of knowledge

**Where does our knowledge come from: sensory experience or reason?**

*Truth · descriptive (theory) · bipolar with median · core axis*

Does everything come from the senses, which nonetheless deceive us at times, or does the mind hold truths no experience could supply? Resting knowledge on experience alone deprives it of certainty; on reason alone, it cuts it off from the real world. To take a position is to say what to rely on, in the last resort, in order to know.

## Poles

### Empiricism

All knowledge derives from sensory experience: the mind starts as a blank slate that the senses gradually fill. Even our most abstract ideas can ultimately be traced back to received impressions.

*[Locke](../philosophers/locke.md) · [Hume](../philosophers/hume.md)*

### Rationalism

Reason has resources of its own, independent of experience: innate ideas, necessary truths, first principles. The senses can deceive us; only reason grounds genuinely certain knowledge.

*[Descartes](../philosophers/descartes.md)*

### Criticism (median position)

Knowledge comes neither from experience alone nor from reason alone, but from their collaboration. The senses supply the matter, the mind the forms that order it. Without concepts, perceptions would be blind; without perceptions, concepts would be empty.

## Live problems

- **Innate or learned?** : Are we born with an empty mind, a blank page the senses fill in, or do we already possess, before any experience, ideas or structures that experience merely awakens? → [Molyneux's problem](../thought-experiments/molyneux-problem.md)
- **Knowledge by intuition** : Some knowledge seems to come all at once, neither by reasoning nor through the senses: the self-evidence of a principle, the sudden grasp of an essence, the certainty of the heart. Is it a higher form of reason, a kind of experience, or an illusion that borrows the name of knowledge?
- **Where does the idea of cause come from?** : Empiricism would trace every idea back to a sense impression. But when one ball strikes another, we see only two motions in succession, never the force or the necessary link between them: where, then, does the idea of cause come from?
- **Does the mind give form to experience?** : What if the mind did not merely receive the world but gave it form? Space, time, causality would then be less things observed than frames we impose on every possible experience.
- **Emotions, a source of knowledge?** : Beyond the senses and reason, do our emotions let us know something of the world and of ourselves, or do they only cloud judgment?
- **Do the senses deceive us?** : If the senses can deceive us to the very root, as in dreams, can solid knowledge still be made to rest on them?
