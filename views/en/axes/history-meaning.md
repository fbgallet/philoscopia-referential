# The meaning of history

**Does human history follow a direction, or is it sheer contingency?**

*Others · descriptive (theory) · bipolar*

If history goes somewhere, our struggles add up and progress is real; if it is only a chain of chance events, nothing guarantees that tomorrow will be better than yesterday, and every gain can be lost. To take a position is to say whether political hope rests on the course of things or on us alone.

## Poles

### Directional meaning

Behind the apparent disorder of events, history follows an overall logic: the progress of reason, a march toward freedom, or a necessary succession of stages. Grasping this direction sheds light on the present and helps anticipate the future.

*[Hegel](../philosophers/hegel.md) · [Marx](../philosophers/marx.md) · [Condorcet](../philosophers/condorcet.md)*

### Contingency

History has neither plan nor destination: it results from human actions with unforeseeable consequences. Belief in laws of history is an illusion, sometimes a dangerous one, used to justify sacrifices in the name of a supposedly necessary future.

*[Popper](../philosophers/popper.md) · Aron*
