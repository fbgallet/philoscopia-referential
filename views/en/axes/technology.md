# Relation to technology

**Does technological progress improve the human condition?**

*The world · value · bipolar with median · core axis*

Each new tool frees us from old burdens, but breeds dependencies and powers we barely control; does technology serve our ends, or silently transform our desires and even who we are? To take a position is to say whether technical progress is a promise to seize or a hold to keep watch over.

## Poles

### Techno-optimism

Technology is the great lever of human progress: it frees us from need, pushes back suffering, and extends our power over nature. The problems it creates call for more ingenuity, not a step backward.

*[Bacon](../philosophers/bacon.md) · [Condorcet](../philosophers/condorcet.md)*

### Techno-criticism

Technology is not a mere neutral tool: it imposes its own logic, transforms our relation to the world, and can dispossess us of what gives existence its value. Its development calls for vigilance, limits, and questioning of ends.

- Marx criticizes the capitalist use of the machine, not the tool itself: technology could serve emancipation under different social relations.

*[Heidegger](../philosophers/heidegger.md) · Ellul · Anders*

### Instrumentalism (median position)

Technology is in itself neither blessing nor curse: it is a means, and everything depends on the use we make of it. The same tool can heal or destroy. The real problem is not technology but the ends we set for it and the wisdom of those who wield it.

## Grounding relations

- *[Explaining nature](../axes/causality.md) : Finalism* grounds *[Relation to technology](../axes/technology.md) : Techno-criticism* : If nature and the living are bearers of their own ends, then the technological will to master and reconfigure everything threatens an order that surpasses us. Finalism grounds techno-criticism: [Jonas](../philosophers/jonas.md) sets against the excessive power of technology a principle of responsibility toward life and future generations.
- *[Place of science](../axes/science-place.md) : Scientism* grounds *[Relation to technology](../axes/technology.md) : Techno-optimism* : If science is the highest form of knowledge, destined to solve human problems, then the technology that flows from it is the great lever of progress. Scientism grounds techno-optimism: from Bacon to [Comte](../philosophers/comte.md), applied knowledge is expected to push back need and suffering, and its setbacks are taken as challenges calling for yet more technology.
