# Bodily pleasures

**Should we distrust the pleasures of the body, or celebrate them?**

*The self · value · bipolar with median*

Is giving in to the body's pleasure a way of fulfilling oneself, or of being ruled by what one cannot master? To take a position is to say whether the body is an ally of the good life or a force to keep in check.

## Poles

### Asceticism

Bodily pleasures distract the soul from what truly matters: truth, the good, salvation. They must therefore be disciplined, or even renounced, for whoever surrenders to them becomes a slave to desire and misses the higher goods.

*[Plato](../philosophers/plato.md) · [Pascal](../philosophers/pascal.md)*

### Celebration

The body and its pleasures are not obstacles but an essential part of a fully human life. Eating, loving, sensing, delighting: knowing how to savor these joys with gratitude and intensity is a form of wisdom, and contempt for them often conceals a resentment against life.

*[Montaigne](../philosophers/montaigne.md) · [Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md)*

### Temperance (median position)

Neither renunciation nor excess: enjoy the pleasures of the body, but in measure. Temperance is not the enemy of pleasure but its right ordering: to savor what is good at the right moment, without becoming its slave. Pleasure is a good as long as reason stays in command.
