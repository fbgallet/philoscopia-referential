# Individual and society

**To understand social life, should we start from individuals or from the social whole?**

*Others · descriptive (theory) · bipolar*

Is society merely individuals and their choices, or a whole that shapes them more than they know? Depending on the answer, we explain a crisis or a custom by personal decisions or by structures, and we hold different parties responsible. To take a position is to say what truly makes history: the whole, or the parts.

## Poles

### Individualism

Society is nothing more than the individuals who compose it and their interactions. Their choices, interests, and rights explain social phenomena, and institutions must be conceived starting from them.

*[Locke](../philosophers/locke.md) · [Mill](../philosophers/mill.md)*

### Holism

The social whole is more than the sum of its parts: it has laws of its own and exerts an influence on individuals that exceeds them. Our ways of thinking, feeling, and acting are shaped first by the society that precedes us.

*Durkheim · [Hegel](../philosophers/hegel.md)*

## Grounding relations

- *[Individual and society](../axes/individual-society.md) : Holism* grounds *[The origin of the social bond](../axes/social-contract.md) : Organic community* : If the social whole precedes individuals and shapes them, society is not a contract struck between separate parties but a living community from which each one proceeds. Holism grounds the organic conception of the social bond: for [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md), the human being is by nature a political animal, and the city is prior to the individual.
- *[Individual and society](../axes/individual-society.md) : Holism* grounds *[Political liberty](../axes/political-liberty.md) : Positive liberty* : If the individual is realized only within the social whole, to be free is not to be left in peace but to take part in the government of one's city. Holism supports positive liberty: in [Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md), one is free only by obeying the law one has prescribed for oneself, and in [Hegel](../philosophers/hegel.md), freedom is fulfilled within institutions.
- *[Individual and society](../axes/individual-society.md) : Individualism* grounds *[The origin of the social bond](../axes/social-contract.md) : Contractualism* : If society is nothing more than the individuals who compose it, then the common order can draw its legitimacy only from an agreement among them. Methodological individualism grounds contractualism: from [Hobbes](../philosophers/hobbes.md) to [Locke](../philosophers/locke.md), it is free and equal individuals who institute power through a pact, not a community that precedes them.
- *[Individual and society](../axes/individual-society.md) : Individualism* grounds *[Political liberty](../axes/political-liberty.md) : Negative liberty* : If only individuals and their choices are real, to be free is first of all to have a space where no one interferes. Individualism grounds negative liberty: for [Mill](../philosophers/mill.md), society may constrain the individual only to prevent harm to others.
