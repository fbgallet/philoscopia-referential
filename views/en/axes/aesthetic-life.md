# Beauty in life

**Is beauty at the heart of a flourishing life, or a secondary luxury next to ethics and usefulness?**

*The world · value · bipolar · core axis*

Depending on the weight we give it, beauty becomes a decisive dimension of existence or a mere ornament, sacrificed without regret the moment duty or usefulness demands it. To take a side is to say whether a life with no room for beauty is impoverished, or simply more austere.

## Poles

### Aestheticism

Beauty is what justifies and fulfills existence: making one's life a work of art, seeking the intensity of beautiful sensations and beautiful forms. Existence is justified only as an aesthetic phenomenon.

*[Wilde](../philosophers/wilde.md) · Pater · [Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md)*

### Primacy of the ethical

Beauty is subordinate to the good and the useful: a secondary pleasure, even a suspect seduction of appearances. The good life is measured by moral uprightness, not aesthetic refinement, and art must serve that end or be a mere luxury.

*[Plato](../philosophers/plato.md) · [Tolstoy](../philosophers/tolstoy.md)*

## Live problems

- **Life as a work of art** : Making one's life a work of art, seeking the intensity of beautiful sensations: is such a life self-sufficient, or does it end up hollow, indifferent to good and evil and stalked by boredom? → [The picture of Dorian Gray](../thought-experiments/dorian-gray.md)
- **The beautiful and the good** : Are the beautiful and the good one, or are they rivals: does beauty raise the soul toward the good, or seduce it by flattering appearances against duty?
- **Does beauty save?** : Is beauty a mere ornament, or what redeems an otherwise absurd or suffering existence and makes it bearable?
- **Enjoying beauty or creating it** : Is the aesthetic life about gathering beautiful experiences as a connoisseur, or about fashioning oneself as a work by giving one's existence style?
- **Where the value of beauty comes from** : Is beauty a real perfection of things, or merely a pleasure of taste? If it is all a matter of taste, its claim to count in a flourishing life weakens.
- **Beauty of the senses or of the mind** : Does the aesthetic life rest first on the pleasures of the senses, falling then under the same suspicion as they do, or on a form that transcends them?
