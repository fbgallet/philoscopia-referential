# Universals

**Do general properties exist, or are they merely names?**

*Truth · descriptive (theory) · bipolar with median*

Many things are "red", many acts are "just": do redness or justice really exist, or do we have only individuals and words to sort them? The status of our concepts, and what our sciences claim to describe, depends on it.

## Poles

### Realism about universals

General properties really exist, and not merely in our mind: redness or the circle are realities we discover. It remains open whether they exist apart from things (Plato) or only within them (Aristotle).

- Transcendent realism (Plato: the Forms exist apart) and immanent realism (Aristotle: the universal exists only within particular things) are two versions of this pole.

*[Plato](../philosophers/plato.md) · [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md)*

### Nominalism

Only individuals exist. Redness is nothing apart from red things: it is a name we apply to objects that resemble one another. Universals are in language, not in the world.

- Ockham denies that universal things exist, yet admits universal concepts or signs in the mind: nominalism is not mere arbitrary labelling.

*Ockham*

### Conceptualism (median position)

Genera and species exist neither as realities in themselves nor as mere words. They are concepts the mind forms by noticing resemblances among things. The universal is real, but in thought: neither in a heaven of Ideas nor reduced to a breath of the voice.

## Grounding relations

- *[Source of knowledge](../axes/knowledge-source.md) : Empiricism* grounds *[Universals](../axes/universals.md) : Nominalism* : If nothing enters the mind that has not first passed through the senses, and the senses deliver only individuals, then general properties are not realities but names. Empiricism inclines toward nominalism: for [Hume](../philosophers/hume.md), the general idea is merely a term attached to particular impressions that resemble one another.
- *[Universals](../axes/universals.md) : Nominalism* grounds *[Essence and existence](../axes/essence.md) : Existentialism* : If only individuals exist and universals are merely names, then there is no human nature inscribed in things, prior to singular individuals. Nominalism opens the way to existentialism: lacking a general essence of the human, each person is defined by their existence and acts, as [Sartre](../philosophers/sartre.md) maintains. The link is an affinity more than an explicit lineage.
- *[Universals](../axes/universals.md) : Realism about universals* grounds *[Essence and existence](../axes/essence.md) : Essentialism* : If general properties really exist and are not mere names, then things have a nature, a form that defines them in their own right. Realism about universals grounds essentialism: whether the Forms subsist apart ([Plato](../philosophers/plato.md)) or within things themselves ([Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md)), the essence of a being is a reality that knowledge discovers.
- *[Universals](../axes/universals.md) : Realism about universals* grounds *[Beauty](../axes/beauty.md) : Objective beauty* : If general properties really exist, beauty is one of them: a perfection of form inscribed in things, which we discover more than we invent. Realism about universals grounds objective beauty: for [Plato](../philosophers/plato.md), beautiful things are beautiful only by participation in Beauty itself, a [Form](../glossary/theory-of-forms.md) that the sensible world reflects.
