# The meaning of suffering

**Can suffering have value, or should we strive to eliminate it as much as possible?**

*The self · value · bipolar with median · core axis*

Does suffering make us grow, mature, understand, or is it only an evil to be reduced wherever we can? Granting it value can lead to glorifying or tolerating the intolerable; wanting only to abolish it can make us flee every ordeal that might have ripened us. To take a position is to say what to do with our own pain and that of others.

## Poles

### Fecundity and redemption

Suffering is not pure evil: it can elevate, deepen, and transform. As an ordeal that draws us closer to what is essential, or a resistance that forges the strong, it carries a fruitfulness that an easy life never knows.

*[Pascal](../philosophers/pascal.md) · [Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md)*

### Abolitionism

Suffering is an evil with no redeeming counterpart: nothing justifies or redeems it. The moral and practical task is to prevent and reduce it wherever possible, for oneself and for others.

*[Epicurus](../philosophers/epicurus.md) · [utilitarianism](../movements/utilitarianism.md)*

### Stoic indifference (median position)

Suffering is neither a trial that ennobles or redeems, nor a sheer evil to be abolished at any cost. It belongs among the indifferents, in itself neither good nor bad: it is not suffering that decides our happiness, but the judgment we pass on it and the steadiness with which we endure it.

## Live problems

- **The ordeal that deepens** : Does an ordeal lived through ripen us, deepen us, "what does not kill me makes me stronger"? Or is that only a consolation, so much pain doing nothing but crush, embitter, destroy?
- **Useless suffering** : There is a pain, that of the innocent, of the child, that nothing redeems and no meaning recovers: to claim to justify it, is that not already to insult it?
- **Is pity born of pain?** : Would a world without suffering be a world without compassion, without courage, without solidarity? Pity presupposes another's misfortune: must we then keep the evil for its fruits, or can we pick the fruits without the tree?
- **Doing away with suffering** : Should we aim at the abolition of all suffering, whether by the spiritual cessation of the desire that breeds it or by an engineering that would erase it from our bodies? Would a life with no pain at all still be human, still desirable?
- **Neither good nor evil: the indifferent** : Perhaps it is not things that trouble us, but our judgments about them: suffering would not decide our happiness, only the steadfastness with which we endure it would. Wisdom, or denial of a very real evil?
- **A meaning found, or lent?** : Does suffering carry a meaning, set within an order that gives it a place, or do we lend it one after the fact, to make it bearable?
- **Hoping beyond suffering** : To pass through pain while hoping for an after, a healing, a salvation: is that the truest response to misfortune, or an escape that turns us from facing it?
