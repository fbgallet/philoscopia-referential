# Hierarchy of tastes

**Are some aesthetic pleasures higher than others, or is every hierarchy of taste a social construction?**

*The world · descriptive (theory) · bipolar*

Is a symphony really worth more than a pop song, or is "good taste" merely the taste of the dominant dressed up as universal? Admitting higher pleasures lets us uplift and educate, but can despise others' joys; putting all tastes on an equal footing respects everyone, but forbids ever saying one work is greater. To take a position is to say whether we can judge taste without simply imposing our own.

## Poles

### Elitism of taste

Not all tastes are equal: qualified judges, trained and sensitive, recognize true beauty, and some pleasures are qualitatively higher than others. Kitsch and refinement are not on the same level.

*[Hume](../philosophers/hume.md) · [Mill](../philosophers/mill.md)*

### Egalitarianism of taste

The hierarchy of tastes is in no way natural: it expresses relations of class. So-called legitimate culture is domination disguised as aesthetic merit, and no pleasure is intrinsically higher than another.

*Bourdieu*

- *[Beauty](../axes/beauty.md) : Objective beauty* grounds *[Hierarchy of tastes](../axes/taste-hierarchy.md) : Elitism of taste* : If some things are beautiful in themselves, then not all judgments of taste are equal: some are more correct, and educating one's eye lets one better perceive what is truly beautiful. Objective beauty supports a hierarchy of taste, where the trained eye of the connoisseur has authority over mere whim.
- *[Beauty](../axes/beauty.md) : Subjective beauty* grounds *[Hierarchy of tastes](../axes/taste-hierarchy.md) : Egalitarianism of taste* : If beauty is only a sentiment that varies across individuals and cultures, then no taste is truer than another. Subjective beauty supports the equality of tastes: there is no disputing about tastes, and no one is entitled to impose their own as the norm.
