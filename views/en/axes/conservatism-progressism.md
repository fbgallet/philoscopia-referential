# Tradition and transformation

**Should we preserve the legacy of our institutions, or transform society in the name of progress?**

*Others · value · bipolar · core axis*

The past hands down an experience no one designed alone, but also injustices no one dares touch any longer; reform can liberate, or undo what worked for reasons we never grasped. To take a position is to say where to place our trust: in what has lasted, or in our power to remake it better.

## Poles

### Conservatism

Inherited institutions condense an accumulated wisdom that no individual reason can replace. Reforms must be prudent and gradual: seeking to refound everything at once risks destroying fragile and precious balances.

*[Burke](../philosophers/burke.md) · [Montesquieu](../philosophers/montesquieu.md)*

### Progressivism

Societies can and must improve: reason allows us to identify inherited injustices and to correct them. Tradition carries no authority in itself; what matters is the emancipation and betterment of humanity.

*[Condorcet](../philosophers/condorcet.md) · [Marx](../philosophers/marx.md)*

## Live problems

- **Is what lasts thereby good?** : Does an institution's endurance prove its worth, what has held having proven itself, or does "it has always been so" also shelter injustices that have lasted just as long?
- **The wisdom of ages, or reason?** : Does the inherited order condense a knowledge no individual reason can match, calling for prudence, or can reason audit institutions and correct the injustice that has crept into them? → [Chesterton's fence](../thought-experiments/chestertons-fence.md)
- **Reform or refound?** : Even when change is called for, should we reform step by step, at the risk of a slowness that lets injustice endure, or refound all at once, at the risk of undoing balances we never understood?
- **The living, or the inheritance?** : Do the living have the right to rewrite what the dead built, "the earth belongs to the living", or are we the trustees of a legacy we must hand on to the generations to come?
- **Does history move toward the better?** : Progressivism assumes change tends toward the better, but does history have a direction, or is "progress" sometimes another name for a loss?
- **Progress, chosen or imposed?** : Technology transforms society whether we will it or not: is "progress" then a political decision, or a force that carries us along without our having voted for it?
