# The meaning of words

**Where do words get their meaning?**

*Truth · descriptive (theory) · triangular*

Depending on whether meaning comes from the thing named, from shared usage, or from the speaker's intention, a misunderstanding has a different source, and translating from one language to another becomes possible or hopeless. To take a position is to say what, at bottom, lets us understand one another, and why we sometimes fail to.

## Poles

### Referentialism

A word's meaning is what it refers to in the world: a name designates an object, a sentence describes a state of affairs. To understand is to know what one is talking about.

- Russell is an imperfect emblem: his theory of descriptions already corrects naive referentialism, for instance for names that designate nothing.

*[Russell](../philosophers/russell.md)*

### Use theory

A word's meaning is not a thing it stands for but its employment: meaning is use. We learn what a word means by seeing how it is used in the language games of shared life.

*Wittgenstein*

### Speaker's intention

Meaning lies in what the speaker seeks to get recognised: to communicate is to give another the means to grasp our intention. The conventional meaning of words derives from such intentions shared over time.

- Grice targets first the speaker's meaning in an utterance; the conventional meaning of words is taken to derive from it.

*Grice*
