# Forgiveness

**Should forgiveness be unconditional, or does it depend on the wrongdoer's repentance?**

*Others · value · bipolar*

Forgiving without condition can free the victim, but seems to wipe away the wrong and ask nothing of the offender; reserving it for repentance preserves justice, but chains the one who suffered to the other's remorse. To take a position is to say whether forgiveness is a gift one grants oneself or a debt the guilty must first redeem.

## Poles

### Unconditionalism

True forgiveness forgives only the unforgivable: if it requires apologies, reparation, or repentance, it is merely a transaction. Genuine forgiveness is a free, unconditional gift that exceeds any logic of desert.

*[Derrida](../philosophers/derrida.md)*

### Conditionalism

Forgiveness presupposes that the wrongdoer acknowledges and regrets their fault: forgiving someone who asks for nothing betrays the victims and trivializes the wrong. Some crimes, met with no repentance, remain unforgivable.

*[Jankélévitch](../philosophers/jankelevitch.md)*

## Live problems

- **Is there anything unforgivable?** : Are some wrongs, some crimes too great ever to be forgiven, or is nothing in principle beyond forgiveness?
- **Forgiving in the victims' place?** : Does one have the right to forgive a wrong done to another, or can only the victim forgive, so that forgiving in their name would be a usurpation? → [Forgiving on behalf of the dead?](../thought-experiments/wiesenthal-sunflower.md)
- **The person and the deed** : Is forgiving to distinguish the wrongdoer from their wrong, in order to receive them beyond what they did, or is it to excuse the deed itself and betray those who suffered from it?
- **Forgiving oneself?** : Can one, and should one, forgive oneself, or is it merely a way of excusing oneself and escaping the guilt one deserves?
- **Forgiving, excusing, forgetting** : Forgiveness differs from excusing, forgetting, or condoning: does any of it remain once we have stripped away everything that is merely an erasing of the wrong?
- **Undoing the irreparable** : What is done cannot be undone: is forgiveness the only power that releases us from the irreversible, freeing us from the weight of what took place?
- **Justice or mercy** : Is forgiving to give up on justice and leave the wrong unpunished, or do justice and forgiveness aim at two different things that can coexist?
- **The forgiveness of peoples** : Can a nation forgive, through amnesty or reconciliation, the crimes committed within it, or does forgiveness make sense only between the persons who lived through the wrong?
- **A duty, or something more?** : Is forgiveness owed, as an obligation toward the one who repents, or is it a free gift, beyond duty, that can be demanded of no one?
