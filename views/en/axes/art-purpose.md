# Purpose of art

**What is art for: to give pleasure, to reveal truth, or to transform the world?**

*The world · value · triangular*

Depending on the answer, a work that pleases without saying anything, or unsettles without pleasing, holds a different value, and we crown different masterpieces. What is decided here is the very measure of what makes a work great.

## Poles

### Pleasure

Art does not have to be useful, moral, or militant: its sole end is beauty and the pleasure of form. To subordinate it to an external cause is to betray it and reduce it to a mere instrument.

*Gautier · Mallarmé*

### Truth

Art is a path to knowledge: it discloses a truth that the concept cannot reach, the essence of the world, the opening of being, or time regained. Its greatness lies in what it lets us see, not in the pleasure it gives or the cause it serves.

*[Schopenhauer](../philosophers/schopenhauer.md) · [Heidegger](../philosophers/heidegger.md) · Proust*

### Transformation

The artist lives in his own time and cannot wash his hands of it: his work awakens consciences, denounces injustice, and takes part in the struggles of the age. Claiming to remain neutral is already taking sides.

*[Sartre](../philosophers/sartre.md) · Brecht · Hugo*

## Grounding relations

- *[Beauty](../axes/beauty.md) : Objective beauty* grounds *[Purpose of art](../axes/art-purpose.md) : Truth* : If beauty is a reality bound up with the order and essence of things, then the great work is the one that lets that truth be seen, beyond mere pleasure. Objective beauty grounds the idea of an art of truth: for [Schopenhauer](../philosophers/schopenhauer.md), art suspends the will and discloses the essence of the world that the concept cannot reach.
- *[Beauty](../axes/beauty.md) : Subjective beauty* grounds *[Purpose of art](../axes/art-purpose.md) : Pleasure* : If beauty is nothing but the pleasure a form gives, then art has no end other than that pleasure itself, without having to serve truth or a cause. Subjective beauty grounds art for art's sake: the work is sufficient unto itself, and subordinating it to an external end would only debase it.
