# Nature of art

**Does art imitate reality, or does it create something radically new?**

*The world · descriptive (theory) · bipolar*

Behind this choice lies the standard by which we measure a work: its faithfulness to the world, or its power to bring forth the unprecedented. The very status of the artist, skilful mirror or genuine origin, hangs on it.

## Poles

### Mimesis

Art is essentially imitation: it represents nature, human actions, the world as it is or as it could be. Its value lies in the accuracy and depth of that representation.

*[Plato](../philosophers/plato.md) · [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md)*

### Creation

Art does not copy the world: it brings forth forms, visions, and worlds that did not exist before it. The artist's genius lies in the power of invention, not in faithfulness to reality.

*[the Romantics](../movements/romanticism.md) · the avant-gardes*
