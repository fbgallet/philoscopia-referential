# Beauty

**Is beauty in things themselves or in the eye of the beholder?**

*The world · descriptive (theory) · bipolar with median*

If beauty belongs to things, we can train our eye, argue, be wrong; if it lies only in us, taste is beyond dispute and every judgement is as good as any other. What is at stake is the very possibility of a shared beauty, beyond mere preference.

## Poles

### Objective beauty

Beauty is a real property of things, grounded in harmony, proportion, or perfection of form. Some works are beautiful in themselves, whether or not we know how to appreciate them.

*[Plato](../philosophers/plato.md) · classicism*

### Subjective beauty

Beauty does not exist in things but in the mind that contemplates them: to call an object beautiful is to express the pleasure it gives us. Tastes vary across individuals and cultures, and none of them is the true one.

*[Hume](../philosophers/hume.md)*

### Subjective universality (Kant) (median position)

Beauty is not a property of the object, yet it is not a mere matter of personal taste either. When I call something beautiful, I voice a subjective pleasure while claiming that everyone ought to feel it too. The judgment of taste is subjective in its source, universal in its claim.

Empiricism grounds subjective beauty: [Hume](../philosophers/hume.md) holds that beauty is no quality in things themselves, but a sentiment in the mind that contemplates them.

Objective beauty grounds the idea of an art of truth: for [Schopenhauer](../philosophers/schopenhauer.md), art suspends the will and discloses the essence of the world that the concept cannot reach.
- *[Beauty](../axes/beauty.md) : Objective beauty* grounds *[Hierarchy of tastes](../axes/taste-hierarchy.md) : Elitism of taste* : If some things are beautiful in themselves, then not all judgments of taste are equal: some are more correct, and educating one's eye lets one better perceive what is truly beautiful. Objective beauty supports a hierarchy of taste, where the trained eye of the connoisseur has authority over mere whim.
- *[Universals](../axes/universals.md) : Realism about universals* grounds *[Beauty](../axes/beauty.md) : Objective beauty* : If general properties really exist, beauty is one of them: a perfection of form inscribed in things, which we discover more than we invent. Realism about universals grounds objective beauty: for [Plato](../philosophers/plato.md), beautiful things are beautiful only by participation in Beauty itself, a [Form](../glossary/theory-of-forms.md) that the sensible world reflects.
- *[Beauty](../axes/beauty.md) : Subjective beauty* grounds *[Purpose of art](../axes/art-purpose.md) : Pleasure* : If beauty is nothing but the pleasure a form gives, then art has no end other than that pleasure itself, without having to serve truth or a cause. Subjective beauty grounds art for art's sake: the work is sufficient unto itself, and subordinating it to an external end would only debase it.
- *[Beauty](../axes/beauty.md) : Subjective beauty* grounds *[Hierarchy of tastes](../axes/taste-hierarchy.md) : Egalitarianism of taste* : If beauty is only a sentiment that varies across individuals and cultures, then no taste is truer than another. Subjective beauty supports the equality of tastes: there is no disputing about tastes, and no one is entitled to impose their own as the norm.
