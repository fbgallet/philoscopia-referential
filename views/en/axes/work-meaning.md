# Meaning of work

**Does work fulfill us, alienate us, or is it merely a necessity?**

*The world · descriptive (theory) · triangular*

Is work the place where we realize ourselves and find our place among others, or what wears us down and dispossesses us, time taken from the rest of our existence? Seeing it as fulfilment can reduce us to our jobs; seeing it as mere drudgery can drain a huge part of our days of meaning. To take a position is to say what place work deserves in a good life.

## Poles

### Fulfillment

Through work, human beings transform the world and transform themselves: they objectify their faculties, win the recognition of others, and shape themselves as subjects. Work is thus a path of humanization, not a mere chore.

*[Hegel](../philosophers/hegel.md)*

### Alienation

In its dominant forms, work dispossesses the worker: one becomes a stranger to one's activity, one's product, and oneself, reduced to a cog paced by the machine. The meaning of work remains confiscated as long as its conditions are not transformed.

*[Marx](../philosophers/marx.md) · [Simone Weil](../philosophers/simone-weil.md)*

### Necessity, leisure as the end

Work answers the needs of life, but it is not the end of existence: leisure, understood as time freed for thought, action, or contemplation, is what gives human life its meaning. We work in order to be free not to work.

*[Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md) · [Arendt](../philosophers/arendt.md)*

## Grounding relations

- *[Being or having a body](../axes/embodiment.md) : The body as instrument* grounds *[Meaning of work](../axes/work-meaning.md) : Necessity, leisure as the end* : If the true accomplishment of the human being lies in the life of the mind, then what belongs to the body and to need remains subordinate to it. The conception of the body as a mere instrument supports work as necessity: it is leisure, freed from toil and open to contemplation, that gives existence its meaning, as in [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md).
- *[Essence and existence](../axes/essence.md) : Essentialism* grounds *[Meaning of work](../axes/work-meaning.md) : Fulfillment* : If the human being has a nature made of faculties to be actualized, then work, through which one objectifies one's capacities and transforms the world, fulfills as much as it tires. Essentialism grounds work as fulfillment: for [Hegel](../philosophers/hegel.md), it is by shaping the world that the human being forms themselves and wins the recognition of others.
- *[Social identity](../axes/social-identity.md) : Social realism* grounds *[Meaning of work](../axes/work-meaning.md) : Alienation* : If social categories like class are objective material realities, and not mere ideas, then the relation of production can genuinely dispossess the worker of their activity and of themselves. Social realism grounds alienation: [Marx](../philosophers/marx.md) shows how, under wage labour, work becomes foreign to the one who performs it.
