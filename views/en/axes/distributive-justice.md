# Distributive justice

**How should wealth and resources be distributed in society?**

*Others · value · bipolar with median · core axis*

Correcting gaps in wealth can trample each person's freedom and merit; leaving them as they are can enshrine luck, inheritance, and force. To take a position is to say what truly belongs to each, and what we owe one another.

## Poles

### Egalitarianism

A just society corrects inequalities that do not result from people's own choices: birth, talents, circumstances. Redistribution is not an assault on freedom but the condition for fair cooperation among citizens.

*[Rawls](../philosophers/rawls.md) · [Marx](../philosophers/marx.md)*

### Libertarianism

Each person owns themselves and the fruits of their labor. A distribution is just whenever it results from free exchanges and legitimate acquisitions: coercive redistribution amounts to violating individual rights.

*[Nozick](../philosophers/nozick.md) · [Locke](../philosophers/locke.md)*

### Sufficientarianism (median position)

What matters is neither perfect equality of shares nor the sheer freedom of exchange, but that no one falls below a threshold of sufficiency. A society is just when everyone has enough to lead a decent life; above that threshold, inequalities weigh far less.

## Live problems

- **Equality of what?** : To distribute fairly, should one equalize incomes, resources, well-being, or each person's real capabilities to act? One cannot be just without first saying which advantage is at issue.
- **Desert or natural lottery?** : Do we deserve the fruits of talents and efforts of which we are not the ultimate authors? If our gifts come down to a natural lottery, is meritocracy just?
- **To each according to what?** : Should we distribute according to need, according to merit, or equally among all? Three criteria of the just share that sketch three different societies.
- **A just outcome, or just exchanges?** : Is a distribution just by the outcome it produces, a certain sharing of wealth, or by the way each person legitimately came to hold what they have, through labor and free exchange? → [Wilt Chamberlain: Liberty against Equality](../thought-experiments/wilt-chamberlain.md)
- **Choosing without knowing one's place** : What principles of distribution would we choose if we did not know the place we will occupy, rich or poor, gifted or not? Does this veil secure impartiality, or does it skew it? → [The Veil of Ignorance](../thought-experiments/veil-of-ignorance.md)
- **Owning oneself** : If I own myself and the fruits of my labor, is taxing my earnings a kind of forced labor, or does property already presuppose a social order that makes it possible?
- **Just enough?** : Is justice satisfied once everyone has the means to lead a decent life? What remains is to say what having enough means, and in the name of what a threshold is deemed sufficient.
- **Equality of shares, or of relations?** : Is equality a matter of shares to be distributed, or first of all a certain relation between persons, without domination or hierarchy, in which no one stands above another?
- **Does justice stop at borders?** : Do we owe the same distributive justice to all of humanity, or do our duties of sharing stop at the members of our own political community?
- **Equality against liberty?** : Does every redistribution restrict the liberty of those it taxes, or does real liberty already presuppose a certain equality of means?

## Grounding relations

- *[Nature of rights](../axes/rights-nature.md) : Natural rights* grounds *[Distributive justice](../axes/distributive-justice.md) : Libertarianism* : If each person holds inviolable rights, beginning with ownership of themselves and of the fruits of their labour, then redistributing by coercion amounts to violating those rights. Natural rights ground libertarianism: for [Nozick](../philosophers/nozick.md), a distribution is just as soon as it results from free exchanges, and the state must confine itself to protecting persons.
- *[Nature of rights](../axes/rights-nature.md) : Political constructivism* grounds *[Distributive justice](../axes/distributive-justice.md) : Egalitarianism* : If rights are the terms of a fair cooperation chosen among equals, a just society must correct the inequalities no one has deserved, those of birth and talent. Political constructivism grounds egalitarianism: [Rawls](../philosophers/rawls.md) admits inequalities only if they also benefit the least advantaged.
