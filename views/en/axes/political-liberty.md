# Political liberty

**What is it to be politically free?**

*Others · descriptive (theory) · categorical*

To be free, is it to be unhindered, to govern oneself, or to depend on no one's good will? Depending on the answer, what a society must guarantee, and what it must protect us from, changes entirely.

## Poles

### Negative liberty

To be free is the absence of hindrance: no one prevents me from acting as I see fit. Liberty is measured by the space left without interference, and the law itself should confine itself to protecting that space rather than narrowing it.

*Constant · [Berlin](../philosophers/berlin.md) · Hayek*

### Positive liberty

To be free is to govern oneself: to be master of one's desires and, as a citizen, to share in making the laws one obeys. Liberty is not merely being left alone, but being the author of one's life and one's city.

- Rousseau is also claimed by the republican non-domination tradition.

*[Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md) · [Hegel](../philosophers/hegel.md)*

### Republican non-domination

To be free is to depend on no one's arbitrary power. The slave of a kind master who never interferes is still a slave: he lives under an ever-possible domination. Liberty requires protections that make arbitrariness impossible, not merely its present absence.

*Pettit · Skinner*

### Capability freedom

To be free is not merely to be unhindered, it is to have the real means to act: without health, education or resources, a purely formal liberty stays a dead letter. Liberty is measured by the effective capabilities to be and to do.

*Sen · [Nussbaum](../philosophers/nussbaum.md)*

## Grounding relations

- *[Individual and society](../axes/individual-society.md) : Holism* grounds *[Political liberty](../axes/political-liberty.md) : Positive liberty* : If the individual is realized only within the social whole, to be free is not to be left in peace but to take part in the government of one's city. Holism supports positive liberty: in [Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md), one is free only by obeying the law one has prescribed for oneself, and in [Hegel](../philosophers/hegel.md), freedom is fulfilled within institutions.
- *[Individual and society](../axes/individual-society.md) : Individualism* grounds *[Political liberty](../axes/political-liberty.md) : Negative liberty* : If only individuals and their choices are real, to be free is first of all to have a space where no one interferes. Individualism grounds negative liberty: for [Mill](../philosophers/mill.md), society may constrain the individual only to prevent harm to others.
