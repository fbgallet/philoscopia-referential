# Letter or spirit of the rule

**Should rules be applied to the letter or according to their spirit?**

*Others · value · bipolar*

Following the letter treats everyone alike and bars arbitrariness, but can yield absurd or cruel results the rule never intended; following the spirit fits the case, but opens the door to each interpreting as they please. To take a position is to say where fidelity to the rule ends and judgement must take over.

## Poles

### Legalism

A just rule holds for everyone and admits no exceptions: that is precisely what makes it reliable and fair. Allowing case-by-case accommodations opens the door to arbitrariness and to the indulgences we each grant ourselves.

*[Kant](../philosophers/kant.md)*

### Equity-phronesis

No general rule can anticipate every particular case: judging well requires practical wisdom, phronesis, capable of fitting the rule to the situation. Equity corrects the law where its literal application would become unjust, remaining faithful to its spirit rather than its letter.

*[Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md)*
