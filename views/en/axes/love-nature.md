# Nature of love

**What lies at the heart of love: a lack, a gift, or something else?**

*Others · descriptive (theory) · categorical · core axis*

To love, is it to want to seize what we lack, to give without expecting anything, to be played by forces that surpass us, or to build a chosen bond that is cultivated? Depending on the answer, love may last or die out once fulfilled, and it does not ask the same thing of us. To take a position is to say what we are really seeking when we say we love.

## Poles

### Eros as lack

To love is to desire what we lack: the beauty, the good, the completeness we do not have. Love is a striving toward what might fulfill us, and this very lack is the force that lifts the soul.

*[Plato](../philosophers/plato.md)*

### Agape as gift

True love does not seek to fill a lack: it gives itself without condition or calculation. It is directed at the other person for their own sake, regardless of their qualities or what they can offer us.

*Christianity · [Levinas](../philosophers/levinas.md)*

### Ruse of the species

Love is an illusion through which nature manipulates us: beneath the most exalted feeling operates the species' drive to reproduce. The individual believes they choose freely, when in fact they serve ends beyond themselves.

*[Schopenhauer](../philosophers/schopenhauer.md)*

### Philia (friendship)

The heart of love is neither a lack to fill nor a one-way gift: it is the reciprocal, chosen bond of friendship, in which each wills the other's good for the other's own sake. Fed by time, equality and shared virtue, it is love's most accomplished form: a good life led together.

*[Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md) · [Montaigne](../philosophers/montaigne.md)*

## Live problems

- **Is love something we decide?** : Is love a promise the will can keep over time, the commitment Kierkegaard calls for, or a passion that seizes us and leaves us without asking, as in Proust and Schopenhauer?

## Grounding relations

- *[Nature and culture](../axes/human-nature.md) : Naturalism* grounds *[Nature of love](../axes/love-nature.md) : Ruse of the species* : If the human being is first of all a natural being driven by impulses shared with the species, love ceases to be a mystery and becomes the mask of the drive to reproduce. Naturalism grounds the "ruse of the species": [Schopenhauer](../philosophers/schopenhauer.md) sees beneath the most exalted passion the will-to-live that uses lovers to perpetuate the species.
- *[Existence of God](../axes/god-existence.md) : Theism* grounds *[Nature of love](../axes/love-nature.md) : Agape as gift* : If God exists and defines himself as love, then the highest love is a free gift, in his image: it gives itself expecting nothing in return. Theism grounds agape: Christianity sets against the eros that desires and lacks the charity that is directed at the other for their own sake.
- *[Where the sacred resides](../axes/transcendence.md) : Transcendence* grounds *[Nature of love](../axes/love-nature.md) : Eros as lack* : If true beauty and goodness reside in an order that surpasses us, then to love is to desire what we lack and to rise toward it. Transcendence grounds eros as lack: for [Plato](../philosophers/plato.md), love begins in the desire for a beautiful body and rises, step by step, to [Beauty itself](../glossary/theory-of-forms.md).
