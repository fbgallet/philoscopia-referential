# Existence of God

**Does God exist?**

*The world · descriptive (theory) · bipolar with median · core axis*

On the answer hang the origin of the world, the existence of a justice beyond human courts, the foundation of our values, and what awaits us after death. Few questions bear so heavily on the whole shape we give our life, and none admits a proof that ends the debate.

## Poles

### Theism

There is a God, creator or ground of the world, personal or not, whose reality gives meaning to existence. This conviction may rest on faith, on experience, or on rational arguments.

*[Aquinas](../philosophers/aquinas.md) · [Anselm](../philosophers/anselm.md) · [Leibniz](../philosophers/leibniz.md)*

### Atheism

God does not exist: no divine reality, personal or impersonal, grounds the world, and the idea of God can be explained by human, psychological, or social causes. The meaning of life must be built without recourse to the divine.

*Feuerbach · [Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md) · [Russell](../philosophers/russell.md)*

### Agnosticism (Huxley) (median position)

We can neither prove that God exists nor prove that he does not: the question exceeds what we are able to know. Rather than affirm or deny, the agnostic suspends judgment and holds it more honest to say I do not know than to settle the matter for lack of proof.

## Live problems

- **Proving God from the idea alone** : Can one conclude, from the mere idea of a perfect being, that it necessarily exists, or is existence never deduced from a concept?
- **The experience of the divine** : Is believing in God a matter of inferring it from arguments, or of relying on an inner experience held to be a direct encounter, one that would need no proof?
- **A God beyond words** : If God exceeds every concept, can one even affirm or deny his existence, or does the question wrongly assume he is one object among others?
- **Why is there something rather than nothing?** : The world exists without having to exist: does accounting for this contingency require a necessary being that depends on nothing, or is the world simply there, a brute fact with no explanation?
- **A first cause, or an endless regress?** : Must the series of causes have a first term that depends on nothing, and why would that term escape the question we put to everything else?
- **The God of the philosophers** : "God of Abraham, God of Isaac, God of Jacob, not of the philosophers and the learned": is the God whose existence reason demonstrates, prime mover, ground or substance, still the one people pray to, or does proof only ever reach an impersonal principle?
- **Believing a miracle** : Can testimony make believable an event that contradicts the regular course of nature, or is it always more likely that the witness is mistaken?
- **Does the world's order have an author?** : Do the order, complexity, and fine tuning of the world call for an author, or can they arise from blind processes such as natural selection? → [The Watch and the Watchmaker](../thought-experiments/design-argument.md)
- **Evil against God** : Would a God both all-powerful and good let the innocent suffer, or is the evil of the world compatible with his existence?
- **God's silence** : If God existed and loved us, would he let those who sincerely seek him search in vain, or is his silence one more sign against his existence?
- **The wager of belief** : Even without proof, is it not in our interest to believe, since we would have everything to gain if God exists and little to lose otherwise? But can one decide to believe by calculation? → [Pascal's Wager](../thought-experiments/pascal-wager.md)
- **God, a projection of man?** : If belief in God can be explained by human needs, fear, desire, social order, must we conclude that he does not exist, or does explaining why we believe say nothing about whether the belief is true?
- **Does belief require proof?** : May one believe only on the strength of proof, or is faith a legitimate venture that need not justify itself before the tribunal of evidence?
- **Does meaning need God?** : Without God, does life lose all meaning, or can meaning be built entirely on a human scale?
- **Does morality need God?** : Does the good owe its value to God's will, or is it good independently of him? If God did not exist, would everything be permitted?

The link remains contested: many atheists defend, on the contrary, a rational and universal morality.

Theism pushes toward theodicy: [Leibniz](../philosophers/leibniz.md) holds that this world, for all its evils, is the best of all possible worlds. Other believers, however, refuse any justification of evil, a sign that the link is in no way necessary.

## Tensions

- With [Death](../axes/death.md) : Confidently asserting that no God and nothing divine exists, then expecting that at death a soul, or something of oneself, survives it, puts two positions in tension: immortalist hope usually presupposes an order, a judgment, or a spiritual reality that atheism rejects. Holding both calls at least for saying what such survival would then rest upon.
- With [Relation to certainty](../axes/certainty.md) : Holding that we can be certain of nothing, then confidently asserting that God does not exist, puts two beliefs in tension: the latter is a strong metaphysical claim, precisely the kind of certainty skepticism refuses. A consistent skepticism would lean toward agnosticism instead.
