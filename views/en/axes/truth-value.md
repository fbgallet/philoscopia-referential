# The value of truth

**Should we want the truth at any cost, or do some illusions help us live?**

*Truth · value · bipolar with median · core axis*

Knowing gives us a grip on the world, but some truths wound without offering anything in return, and uncertain beliefs sometimes seem to feed love, courage, or the drive to live. To take a position is to say whether lucidity is an unconditional good, or whether life has the right to judge what is worth knowing.

## Poles

### The primacy of truth

Truth is a good in itself: even when harsh it sets us free, and illusion, however sweet, diminishes us. Leaving the cave is costly, but only a life that faces reality squarely is fully human; consenting to illusion means giving up one's own power to understand and to act.

*[Plato](../philosophers/plato.md) · [Spinoza](../philosophers/spinoza.md)*

### Vital illusions

Nothing proves that truth is worth more than illusion: a belief is judged by what it does for life, by the strength it gives or takes away. Art, ideals, the fictions we live by may be illusions, but they are vital ones; the will to truth at any cost is itself a belief that must be questioned.

*[Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md)*

### Truth that empowers (median position)

Truth is worth having for the grip it gives: wherever knowing lets us act, adjust, and live better, it is preferable to illusion. But when it offers no grip and can only weigh on us, wisdom consists in turning our attention elsewhere and putting things in perspective, without ever replacing what is true with a consoling fiction.

## Live problems

- **The right not to know** : When a truth can neither guide nor change anything, is refusing to know it wisdom, or giving up on facing reality?
- **The happy fool** : Is a lucid life better, even if troubled by what it discovers, than a happy life in ignorance? Is truth a component of living well, or an instrument worth only what it brings?
- **Looking away** : To stop dwelling on a truth that gives no purchase is not to deny it; but where does the line run between this wisdom of attention and denial, if discomfort gets to decide what we look at?
- **Lying to oneself** : Choosing one's illusions would require believing at will; but can we really believe what we know to be false, or only arrange things so as not to look?
- **Living on fictions** : An ideal, a work of art, an "as if" we know to be fictions deceive no one: do such avowed fictions do the vital work we credit illusions with, without the price of falsehood, or are they just an illusion unaware of itself?
- **The city and the lie** : Many common orders rest on unverifiable narratives, from founding myth to official fiction: does a society owe its members the truth, or can it live on shared myths?
- **Hope, an illusion?** : To hope is not to believe falsely: since the future is not yet settled, hope may escape the divide between lucidity and illusion altogether, unless it is illusion's most discreet form.
- **Illusion for another's sake** : Withholding a diagnosis, embellishing a situation: deciding that someone will live better without a truth means judging in their place what it is worth to them; who has that right?
