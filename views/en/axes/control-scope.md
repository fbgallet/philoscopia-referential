# Scope of action

**Facing the world, should we focus on what is up to us or strive to extend our mastery?**

*The self · value · bipolar with median · core axis*

Is the only seat of our power within, in our judgements and desires, the course of the world wholly beyond us, or can we lay hold of that order and reshape it to our hand? To take a position is to say how far our real grip on things extends, and whether freedom is won within us or out in the world.

## Poles

### What is up to us

Some things are up to us, our judgments and our will; others are not, such as health, reputation, or external events. Wisdom consists in focusing our efforts on the former and accepting the latter with equanimity.

*[Epictetus](../philosophers/epictetus.md)*

### Prometheanism

The limits of our power are not fixed once and for all: through knowledge, technology, and collective action, humanity can transform the world and push back what seemed out of reach. Rather than accepting the order of things, we should work to change it.

*[Descartes](../philosophers/descartes.md) · [Marx](../philosophers/marx.md)*

### Knowing the difference (median position)

Between accepting what lies beyond us and trying to transform everything, the task is to discern, case by case, what we can change and what we must accept. Neither resignation nor omnipotence: act where we have a grip, let go of the rest.

## Live problems

- **Truly masters of our own judgments?** : Stoicism places our judgments on the side of what is up to us, but do we truly choose them, or are our opinions, emotions and desires dictated to us by the body, our history and the causes that make us? If even the inner world escapes us, does the dichotomy still hold?
- **Accept, or resign oneself?** : Where is the line between serenity before the inevitable and passivity, even a convenient excuse, before a wrong that effort could put right?
- **My power, or our power?** : What is not up to me alone, illness, injustice, the course of the world, may be up to us together: by seeking power in the inner self alone, does the dichotomy ignore the collective action that shifts the limits of the possible?
- **Serenity, or achievement?** : Is the good of a life the peace of an untroubled soul, or the active achievement of one who transforms the world and leaves a mark on it?
- **Is hoping wise?** : Is hope, which binds us to things beyond our power, a weakness that disturbs the soul, or the very spring that drives us to transform what can be transformed?
- **Making peace with what was** : The past is what is no longer up to us par excellence: should we accept it fully, even to the point of willing that it was, or stay its prisoner through regret?
