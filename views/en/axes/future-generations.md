# Future generations

**Do we have duties toward future generations?**

*The world · value · bipolar*

Can we owe anything to beings who do not yet exist, who claim nothing, and whom our choices will bring into being as they will be? Making room for them can weigh heavily on the living; ignoring them means mortgaging a world we will never see. To take a position is to say how far our responsibility extends in time.

## Poles

### Responsibility principle

Our power to act now extends to the living conditions of generations to come: we have a duty to act so that the effects of our actions remain compatible with the permanence of genuinely human life on Earth. The distant future enters the scope of morality.

*[Jonas](../philosophers/jonas.md)*

### Priority to the present

Our obligations are owed first to the people who exist here and now: their needs are real and urgent, whereas the interests of future individuals remain hypothetical and uncertain. Sacrificing the present to a distant future would be unjust to the living.
