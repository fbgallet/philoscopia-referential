# Relation to authority

**Is a political authority that commands and coerces necessary, or should we free ourselves from it?**

*Others · value · bipolar with median*

It all turns on what we expect of human beings left to themselves: able to order their own affairs, or bound for disorder without a power to settle disputes. To take a position is to set how far the duty to obey extends, and where the right to refuse begins.

## Poles

### Authority

Without a common authority able to decide and to coerce, social life collapses into conflict or chaos. The state, far from being mere domination, is the condition of peace, law, and even concrete freedom.

*[Hobbes](../philosophers/hobbes.md) · [Hegel](../philosophers/hegel.md)*

### Anarchism

Any authority imposed from above is an illegitimate domination that mutilates freedom. Society can organize itself through free association, mutual aid, and federalism, without a state or chains of command.

*Bakunin · Proudhon*

### Constitutionalism (median position)

Power is necessary, but it is legitimate only if it is limited. Between the obedience owed to authority and the refusal of all domination, constitutionalism subjects power to rules and rights: we obey, but laws rather than men, and no one stands above them.

## Grounding relations

- *[Vision of humanity](../axes/human-vision.md) : Anthropological optimism* grounds *[Relation to authority](../axes/authority.md) : Anarchism* : If human beings are naturally good and it is society that corrupts them, authority imposed from above looks less like a remedy than the source of the evil. Anthropological optimism feeds anarchism: the fundamental goodness affirmed by [Rousseau](../philosophers/rousseau.md) opens the way to the idea of a society that would organize itself without domination, through free association.
- *[Vision of humanity](../axes/human-vision.md) : Anthropological pessimism* grounds *[Relation to authority](../axes/authority.md) : Authority* : If man is by nature inclined to violence and self-interest, the absence of a common power leaves him to the war of all against all. Anthropological pessimism thus justifies a strong authority, the only thing able to guarantee civil peace.
