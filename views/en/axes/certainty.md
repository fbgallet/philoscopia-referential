# Relation to certainty

**Can we reach definitive certainties?**

*Truth · descriptive (theory) · bipolar with median · core axis*

If nothing is ever certain, no knowledge holds firm and action loses its footing; yet to believe oneself certain is to risk shutting one's eyes and learning nothing more. To take a position is to say whether doubt is a form of honesty or a dead end.

## Poles

### Dogmatism

Properly guided reason can establish certain and definitive truths that serve as foundations for knowledge. Doubt is a stage, not a destination.

*[Descartes](../philosophers/descartes.md)*

### Skepticism

No definitive certainty is within reach: every thesis faces an opposing thesis of equal strength. Wisdom consists in suspending judgment.

*[Pyrrho](../philosophers/pyrrho.md) · [Hume](../philosophers/hume.md)*

### Fallibilism (median position)

We can hold firm, well-justified beliefs while knowing they might be wrong. Between a certainty that forbids all doubt and a skepticism that doubts everything, fallibilism embraces knowledge that is always open to revision: we proceed on our best reasons, ready to correct ourselves.

## Live problems

- **What resists doubt?** : Pushing doubt to its limit, dream, illusion, a possible deceiver, does anything resist, or must we admit that no certainty holds? → [The dream and the evil demon](../thought-experiments/dream-argument.md)
- **Sure of yourself, sure of the truth?** : Should a conviction asserted with aplomb but no proof carry the day, or must we demand reasons before believing, even at the discomfort of suspending judgment?
- **Alone in seeing clearly?** : When everyone thinks the opposite of me, is it a sign that I am wrong, or is the clear-sighted one sometimes alone against the crowd: how far should I defer to others' judgment?
- **Mathematics, beyond doubt?** : Long the very model of certainty, do mathematics escape all doubt, or do the choice of axioms and the limits revealed by the incompleteness theorems show that even here certainty has a horizon?
- **Will tomorrow resemble yesterday?** : The sun has always risen, but what guarantees it will rise tomorrow? No accumulation of observations proves a law: does our trust in the future rest on reason, or on habit alone?
- **Living without certainty** : If nothing is certain, can we still act and choose, or does suspending judgment make life impossible: is fallibilism, acting on one's best reasons while staying open to revision, the way out?
- **When an equal disagrees** : When someone as informed and rigorous as I am reaches the opposite conclusion, must I temper my confidence, like the skeptic who makes isostheneia a rule, or may I hold firm on the strength of the examination I have carried out myself?
- **Justify, how far?** : Every reason rests on another reason: does the chain stop at a foundation, turn in a circle, or run off to infinity? This is the skeptic's lever.
- **The world, or our representations?** : Even granting our perceptions are coherent, do they reach things as they are, or only our representations, with no way to compare the two?

## Grounding relations

- *[Source of knowledge](../axes/knowledge-source.md) : Empiricism* grounds *[Relation to certainty](../axes/certainty.md) : Skepticism* : Pressed to its limit, [empiricism](../axes/knowledge-source.md) leads to doubt: if all knowledge comes from the senses, nothing guarantees the truths that outrun them, such as the necessity of a cause or the regularity of the future. This is [Hume](../philosophers/hume.md)'s road, from sensationism to [skepticism](../glossary/skepticism.md). The link stays contested: a moderate empiricism, in Locke, is in no way skeptical.
- *[Source of knowledge](../axes/knowledge-source.md) : Rationalism* grounds *[Relation to certainty](../axes/certainty.md) : Dogmatism* : If [reason](../axes/knowledge-source.md) commands first principles and [innate ideas](../glossary/innate-ideas.md) independent of the deceptive senses, then a certain and definitive truth lies within its reach. Rationalism thus feeds [dogmatism](../glossary/dogmatism.md): it is by seeking a foundation reason could not revoke that Descartes believes he closes off doubt.

## Tensions

- With [Existence of God](../axes/god-existence.md) : Holding that we can be certain of nothing, then confidently asserting that God does not exist, puts two beliefs in tension: the latter is a strong metaphysical claim, precisely the kind of certainty skepticism refuses. A consistent skepticism would lean toward agnosticism instead.
