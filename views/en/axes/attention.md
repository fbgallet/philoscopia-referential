# Attention and diversion

**Is diverting oneself a flight from what matters, or an art of living?**

*The self · value · bipolar*

We spend a growing share of our lives diverting ourselves, and whole industries now compete for every minute of our attention. A flight from what matters, or the art of inhabiting a life no one can spend forever face to face with the essential? To take a position is to say what the hours we give to nothing important are worth.

## Poles

### Recollection

Diversion is a flight: what matters, our condition, death, what is entrusted to us, asks to be faced, and diversion turns us away from it precisely when it weighs. The response is recollection: to gather one's attention and become present to the essential, however uncomfortable, rather than to numb oneself.

- Pascal: diversion is at once a blindness and an unavoidable consolation for misery without God; his critique targets what it prevents, seeking, not the pleasure itself.

*[Simone Weil](../philosophers/simone-weil.md) · [Pascal](../philosophers/pascal.md)*

### Diversion embraced

To divert oneself is not to betray one's life but to make it livable: no one can stare forever at death or misfortune, and diversion relieves, sometimes heals, sets us moving again. Knowing how to be distracted, to change objects, to play, to converse, is an art of living and a wisdom, not a weakness to blush at.

*[Montaigne](../philosophers/montaigne.md) · [Hume](../philosophers/hume.md)*

## Live problems

- **Why can we not bear rest?** : "All of humanity's unhappiness comes from one thing, not knowing how to stay quietly in a room": if we flee calm, is it the misery of our condition surfacing the moment the noise stops, as Pascal diagnoses, or is the mind simply made for movement rather than for facing itself?
- **Captured attention** : When entire devices are designed to capture every available minute, is diverting oneself still a choice one owns, or must attention be defended as a scarce good, diversion ceasing to be an art the moment it is administered?
- **Reclaiming attention** : Faced with devices designed to capture it, can we really reclaim our attention by working at it, withdrawing, fasting from the feed, returning to the present, or do such techniques only paper over a trouble far larger than us?
