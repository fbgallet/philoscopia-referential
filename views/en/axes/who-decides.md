# Who should decide

**Do political decisions belong to everyone, or first to the most competent?**

*Others · value · bipolar*

Giving everyone an equal voice can hand grave choices to ignorance or passion; reserving power for the most knowledgeable can hide the interests of the powerful behind "expertise" and strip people of a say over their own lives. To take a position is to say where the legitimacy to decide comes from: the equality of all, or the competence of a few.

## Poles

### Radical democracy

Politics rests on everyone's equal capacity to deliberate and decide: there are no experts in the common good. A free society institutes itself, one where anyone can take part in power without any special entitlement to rule.

*Castoriadis · Rancière*

### Epistocracy

Governing requires knowledge, like steering a ship or treating the sick. Entrusting decisions to ill-informed citizens produces bad policies: power should belong, at least in part, to those with the required competence.

*[Plato](../philosophers/plato.md) · Brennan*
