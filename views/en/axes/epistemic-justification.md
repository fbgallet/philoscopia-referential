# Justification of belief

**What makes a belief justified?**

*Truth · descriptive (theory) · triangular*

From pseudo-sciences to rumours, countless beliefs present themselves as justified: what remains is to know what that warrant rests on, first certainties, the agreement of the whole, or methods that have proven themselves. Depending on the answer, we draw the line between knowledge and what merely looks like it in a different place.

## Poles

### Foundationalism

Knowledge rests on basic beliefs, immediately certain, that need no others to justify themselves: everything else is built upon them, like a building on its foundations.

*[Descartes](../philosophers/descartes.md)*

### Coherentism

No belief is justified on its own: it is justified by its place in a coherent whole, where each supports the others. An idea holds if it fits without contradiction into the entire web of our experience, with no ultimate foundation.

*Quine · Davidson*

### Reliabilism

A belief is justified if it is produced by a reliable process, perception, memory, sound reasoning, even if the one who holds it could not prove it. What matters is not being able to justify it, but having been formed in the right way.

*Goldman*

## Grounding relations

- *[Nature of truth](../axes/truth-nature.md) : Coherence* grounds *[Justification of belief](../axes/epistemic-justification.md) : Coherentism* : If a statement is true only by its non-contradictory place within an overall system, then a belief is justified only by its coherence with the entire web of the others. The coherence conception of truth supports coherentism: there is no ultimate foundation, but beliefs that support one another, from the Hegelian whole ([Hegel](../philosophers/hegel.md)) to Quine's "web".
- *[Source of knowledge](../axes/knowledge-source.md) : Rationalism* grounds *[Justification of belief](../axes/epistemic-justification.md) : Foundationalism* : If properly guided reason can reach certain truths, knowledge must rest on basic beliefs, immediately evident, from which all the rest is deduced. Rationalism grounds foundationalism: [Descartes](../philosophers/descartes.md) seeks in the cogito the indubitable fixed point on which to rebuild all knowledge.
