# Being or having a body

**Am I my body, or do I have a body?**

*The self · descriptive (theory) · bipolar with median*

To treat the body as an instrument is to be able to stand back from it, repair it, leave it behind in thought; to identify with it is for nothing of me to remain when it suffers or wears out. Our relation to illness, to pleasure, to ageing and to our own animality is decided here.

## Poles

### The body as instrument

The true self is the mind, the soul or thought; the body is a vehicle, sometimes a prison, that I use and can regard as an object distinct from me. I "have" a body more than I am one.

- Descartes nonetheless insisted, in his later writings, on a genuine union of soul and body, beyond the mere pilot in a ship.

*[Plato](../philosophers/plato.md) · [Descartes](../philosophers/descartes.md)*

### The lived body

I am not a mind housed in a machine: I am this living body that perceives, acts and inhabits the world. Thought itself arises from this carnal anchoring and from our condition as an animal organism shaped by evolution.

- Varela belongs above all to enaction, the coupling of organism and world, more than to the strict "I am my body" thesis.

*[Merleau-Ponty](../philosophers/merleau-ponty.md) · [Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md) · Varela*

### Soul-body union (median position)

I am neither a mind steering a body-machine nor a mere organism. Soul and body form a union so close that I am one with it: I am my body as much as I have it. It is through my body that I feel, act and inhabit the world.

The lived-body thesis, which [Merleau-Ponty](../philosophers/merleau-ponty.md) places at the heart of existence, supports the primacy of action: for [Marx](../philosophers/marx.md), the point is less to interpret the world than to transform it.

Materialism thus supports the naturalist side of the lived-body thesis, the human being as an animal body shaped by evolution. It does not, however, deliver the "subject" in the sense of [Merleau-Ponty](../philosophers/merleau-ponty.md)'s [phenomenology](../movements/phenomenology.md), where one's own body is the original locus of all perception and meaning, a content that does not follow from materialism alone.
