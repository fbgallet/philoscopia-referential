# Place of science

**Is science the only valid path to knowledge?**

*Truth · descriptive (theory) · bipolar with median · core axis*

Is science the only road to truth, all the rest being mere opinion, or do art, ethics, and lived experience also yield truths it cannot reach? Making it the sole judge dismisses whole swaths of human life; denying it any special authority lets any belief pass itself off as knowledge. To take a position is to say what we accept as a valid source of truth.

## Poles

### Scientism

The scientific method is the most accomplished form of human knowledge, destined to replace religious and metaphysical explanations. Any question that can be answered will be answered by science, and by science alone.

*[Comte](../philosophers/comte.md)*

### Epistemic pluralism

There is no single method that guarantees knowledge: science itself advances by breaking its own rules. Art, myth, and traditional knowledge offer ways of grasping reality that nothing entitles us to dismiss in advance.

*[Feyerabend](../philosophers/feyerabend.md)*

### Critical rationalism (median position)

Science is our most rigorous knowledge, yet it proceeds by conjectures forever exposed to refutation. It does not answer everything and does not exhaust the real, and yet it is not merely one body of knowledge among others. Its strength lies precisely in acknowledging its limits and correcting itself.

## Live problems

- **The remainder, after science** : Science tells the how, never the why or what is worth doing: it could answer all its possible questions without having even touched what gives a life its meaning. Are there truths, about meaning or the good, beyond its reach?
- **Explaining, or understanding?** : To study man, history, a society, is it to seek causes as in physics, or to understand a meaning and intentions from within? One method for all objects, or other kinds of knowledge for what does not reduce to a mechanism?
- **What only lived experience gives** : Does objective, third-person knowledge grasp what it is to see red, to suffer, to love? Science presupposes a lifeworld it does not itself describe, and intuition reaches a duration that measurement lets slip. → [Mary's room](../thought-experiments/marys-room.md)
- **Does art tell the truth?** : Do tragedy, the novel, the poem disclose truths about the human condition that no scientific statement could formulate, or do they offer only an enjoyment with no knowledge-value?
- **Does scientism refute itself?** : "Only science gives knowledge": but this statement is not itself scientific, it is a philosophical claim about science. In claiming to ground everything on science, does it not saw off the branch it sits on?
- **What makes something a science?** : What separates a science from a belief that puts on its appearances, astrology or a doctrine that nothing could disprove? Its capacity to expose itself to the verdict of the facts, or is another criterion needed, if a sharp boundary exists at all?
- **Faith and reason** : Do religious faith and mystical experience give access to the real that science could not judge, or do they belong to a belief that is nothing like knowledge?
- **The true, or the useful?** : Do scientific theories describe reality as it is, down to the entities we cannot see, or are they only convenient instruments for prediction and action?

## Grounding relations

- *[Source of knowledge](../axes/knowledge-source.md) : Empiricism* grounds *[Place of science](../axes/science-place.md) : Scientism* : If only controlled experience yields genuine knowledge, then science becomes the only legitimate path to knowledge, and religious or metaphysical explanations are merely superseded stages. Empiricism feeds scientism: [Comte](../philosophers/comte.md) makes the positive state the culmination of the human mind's evolution.
- *[Status of knowledge](../axes/knowledge-status.md) : Constructivism* grounds *[Place of science](../axes/science-place.md) : Epistemic pluralism* : If science is one historical construction among others, shaped by its paradigms, then it holds no monopoly on access to the real. Constructivism about knowledge grounds epistemic pluralism: [Feyerabend](../philosophers/feyerabend.md) holds that no single method guarantees knowledge and that science itself advances by breaking its own rules.
- *[Place of science](../axes/science-place.md) : Scientism* grounds *[Relation to technology](../axes/technology.md) : Techno-optimism* : If science is the highest form of knowledge, destined to solve human problems, then the technology that flows from it is the great lever of progress. Scientism grounds techno-optimism: from Bacon to [Comte](../philosophers/comte.md), applied knowledge is expected to push back need and suffering, and its setbacks are taken as challenges calling for yet more technology.
