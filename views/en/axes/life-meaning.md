# The meaning of life

**How should we respond to the enigma of life's meaning?**

*The self · value · categorical · core axis*

The enigma is a common one: the meaning of life does not show itself, and everyone knows hours of disorientation when nothing seems to point to it. If there is a meaning, it must be sought and deciphered; if there is none, what remains is to create it, to revolt, or to give it up. To take a position is to say what attitude to hold in the face of this obscurity, and what can give our days their worth.

## Poles

### Teleology and providence

Meaning exists, but it does not give itself at once: existence belongs to a world order or a providential design that must be learned and deciphered. Faced with the enigma, the task is to seek that purpose, to trust it even while it remains obscure, and to align one's conduct with it.

*the Stoics · Christianity*

### Constructed meaning

If meaning eludes us, it is because none is written into things: there is nothing to discover, everything to make. Faced with the enigma, the answer is to create the significance of one's life through one's choices and projects, and this freedom makes us fully responsible for what we become.

*[Sartre](../philosophers/sartre.md)*

### Absurdism

No meaning to discover, and no substitute for it: the absurd arises from the clash between our thirst for meaning and the silence of the world, and no answer will come. Faced with the enigma, the point is to hold out without cheating: to live lucidly in revolt, without hope or resignation.

*[Camus](../philosophers/camus.md)*

### Renunciation

Since no meaning is given and nothing truly replaces it, the torment comes from the demand itself. Faced with the enigma, the lucid response is renunciation: to detach the will, to stop asking of life what it does not contain, and to find in this withdrawal a peace without illusion.

*[Schopenhauer](../philosophers/schopenhauer.md)*

## Live problems

- **Is meaning inscribed in things?** : Does the world carry an order or design that precedes us, as the Stoics and providentialisms hold, or is it silent? Constructing one's meaning, revolting, or renouncing all share the same starting point: nothing is given.
- **Sisyphus and his rock** : When our thirst for meaning meets the silence of the world, where can an answer lie: in an end that surpasses us, in the meaning we give ourselves, or in the lucid revolt of a life with no answer? → [Sisyphus and his rock](../thought-experiments/sisyphus-rock.md)
- **Meaning of life, or in life?** : Does life have an overall meaning, an answer to « why all this? », or only local meanings, the ones we find in a love, a work, a commitment, even if the whole has none?
- **Must it last?** : If everything ends, through death and then the erasure of every trace, is what we do in vain, or can something have full meaning without lasting forever?
- **What if nothing meant anything?** : When the frameworks that gave meaning fall away, God, History, progress, does any meaning remain possible, or does the vertigo open in which nothing is worth anything?
- **Will any meaning do?** : Can a life be full of meaning if it is devoted to something trivial, or even bad, or does meaning require that the thing be objectively worth it?
- **Meaning without God?** : Does the meaning of existence presuppose a divine order that surpasses us, or can it hold entirely without God, on wholly human grounds?
- **Happiness or meaning** : Is a successful life necessarily happy, or can it be worthwhile through its meaning while being hard, demanding, at times unhappy?
