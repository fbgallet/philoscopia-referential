# Relation to the past

**Faced with what has happened and cannot be changed, should we embrace it or rebel against it?**

*The self · value · bipolar*

Accepting, even loving, what has been brings peace and reconciliation, but can look like consenting to the unacceptable; revolting keeps alive the sense that it should not have been, but can rivet us to a wound that will not heal. To take a position is to say how to live with the irreversible.

## Poles

### Amor fati

To want nothing to be different, not forward, not backward: not merely to endure what has happened, but to love it as an integral part of one's life. Regret and resentment poison existence; consenting to fate sets us free.

*the Stoics · [Nietzsche](../philosophers/nietzsche.md)*

### Revolt

To consent to everything that has happened is to risk blessing injustice and evil as well. Human dignity demands that we maintain our refusal: acknowledging what was without ever endorsing it, and drawing from this revolt the strength to act.

*[Camus](../philosophers/camus.md)*
