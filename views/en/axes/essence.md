# Essence and existence

**Do we have a nature that precedes us, or do we become what we choose to be?**

*The self · descriptive (theory) · bipolar · core axis*

If a nature precedes us, there is a way to fulfil ourselves or to betray ourselves, but our freedom has limits; if we invent ourselves, everything becomes possible, but nothing guides us and nothing excuses us. To take a position is to say what we owe to what is given to us, and what we alone are answerable for.

## Poles

### Essentialism

Every being has an essence that precedes and defines its existence: there is a human nature, with its own ends. Living well means fulfilling what this nature calls for, as the seed fulfills the plant.

*[Plato](../philosophers/plato.md) · [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md)*

### Existentialism

Existence precedes essence: a human being is at first nothing definite and makes itself through its choices and actions. No nature assigns us a destiny, and invoking an essence to justify what one is amounts to bad faith.

*[Sartre](../philosophers/sartre.md) · [Beauvoir](../philosophers/beauvoir.md)*

## Live problems

- **"It's my nature": truth or excuse?** : To appeal to one's nature, "that's how I am, I can't change," is it an honest acknowledgment of what one is, or an excuse to flee what one remains answerable for? → ["That's just how I am"](../thought-experiments/its-my-nature.md)
- **Finding oneself, or inventing oneself?** : The injunction "be yourself" is itself divided: is self-realization bringing to light a self already there, or freely inventing one? "Become who you are" tries to hold both at once.
- **A nature that binds?** : Supposing we have a nature, does it tell us how to live? Does knowing what we are settle what we ought to become, or is there a leap from the one to the other?
- **Where does the essence of things come from?** : If things have an essence, where do they get it: from a plan that conceives them before they exist, from nature itself, from a convention of our minds, or from nothing?
- **Gender, race: nature or construction?** : Are the great categories of identity, gender, race, anchored in a nature, or historical constructions we mistake for natures?
- **Is there a human nature?** : Are our behaviors explained first by a shared human nature, or by the culture that shapes us?

## Grounding relations

- *[Existence of God](../axes/god-existence.md) : Atheism* grounds *[Essence and existence](../axes/essence.md) : Existentialism* : If there is no craftsman God to conceive man according to a plan, no human essence precedes concrete existence. Atheism thus grounds existentialism, for which man first is, and only then defines himself through his acts.
- *[Essence and existence](../axes/essence.md) : Essentialism* grounds *[The highest good](../axes/sovereign-good.md) : Eudaimonism* : If the human being has a proper nature, a function that defines it, then a life is good when it fully accomplishes that function. Essentialism grounds eudaimonism: [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md) draws the highest good from man's proper function, the excellent exercise of reason, of which pleasure is only the accompaniment.
- *[Essence and existence](../axes/essence.md) : Essentialism* grounds *[Criterion of right action](../axes/ethics-foundation.md) : Virtue ethics* : If the human being has a nature and a function of its own, acting well means excelling in the fulfilment of that function, that is, being virtuous. Essentialism grounds virtue ethics: [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md) defines the human good by the excellent activity of reason, a matter of character more than of rules.
- *[Essence and existence](../axes/essence.md) : Essentialism* grounds *[Meaning of work](../axes/work-meaning.md) : Fulfillment* : If the human being has a nature made of faculties to be actualized, then work, through which one objectifies one's capacities and transforms the world, fulfills as much as it tires. Essentialism grounds work as fulfillment: for [Hegel](../philosophers/hegel.md), it is by shaping the world that the human being forms themselves and wins the recognition of others.
- *[Essence and existence](../axes/essence.md) : Existentialism* grounds *[Death](../axes/death.md) : Living in view of one's death* : If the human being has no given essence and is only what they make of themselves, then it is by assuming their own finitude that they tear themselves from dispersion and become authentically themselves. Existentialism grounds being-toward-death: [Heidegger](../philosophers/heidegger.md) makes death, once assumed, one's ownmost possibility, the one that reveals existence to itself.
- *[Essence and existence](../axes/essence.md) : Existentialism* grounds *[The meaning of life](../axes/life-meaning.md) : Constructed meaning* : If existence precedes essence, no end is inscribed in advance in human nature. Existentialism therefore entails that the meaning of life is not found but constructed, through the projects and choices each person takes on.
- *[Essence and existence](../axes/essence.md) : Existentialism* grounds *[Freedom of the will](../axes/freedom.md) : Free will* : If existence precedes essence, no given nature settles my choices in advance: I am what I make of myself, and therefore radically free. Existentialism grounds [free will](../glossary/free-will.md), to the point of making freedom the very condition of the human being. Invoking a character or a nature to excuse oneself is then a matter of [bad faith](../glossary/bad-faith.md), according to [Sartre](../philosophers/sartre.md).
- *[Essence and existence](../axes/essence.md) : Existentialism* grounds *[Temporal orientation](../axes/time-orientation.md) : Project* : If existence precedes essence, the human being is not a given thing but what they make of themselves by projecting toward the future. Existentialism grounds the orientation toward projects: for [Sartre](../philosophers/sartre.md), I am my acts and my commitments to come, and to reduce myself to the present instant would be to give up on myself.
- *[Explaining nature](../axes/causality.md) : Finalism* grounds *[Essence and existence](../axes/essence.md) : Essentialism* : If each being tends toward an end of its own, it is because a form, an essence, defines it and guides its development. Finalism grounds essentialism: in [Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md), the [final cause](../glossary/four-causes.md) and the form coincide, and the nature of a thing is what it is meant to become.
- *[Universals](../axes/universals.md) : Nominalism* grounds *[Essence and existence](../axes/essence.md) : Existentialism* : If only individuals exist and universals are merely names, then there is no human nature inscribed in things, prior to singular individuals. Nominalism opens the way to existentialism: lacking a general essence of the human, each person is defined by their existence and acts, as [Sartre](../philosophers/sartre.md) maintains. The link is an affinity more than an explicit lineage.
- *[Universals](../axes/universals.md) : Realism about universals* grounds *[Essence and existence](../axes/essence.md) : Essentialism* : If general properties really exist and are not mere names, then things have a nature, a form that defines them in their own right. Realism about universals grounds essentialism: whether the Forms subsist apart ([Plato](../philosophers/plato.md)) or within things themselves ([Aristotle](../philosophers/aristotle.md)), the essence of a being is a reality that knowledge discovers.
